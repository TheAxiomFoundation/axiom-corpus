# Rhode Island tax-year-2026 personal-income-tax adjustments

This intake retains Rhode Island Division of Taxation Advisory ADV 2025-22,
published November 3, 2025.

Official source:
<https://tax.ri.gov/sites/g/files/xkgbur541/files/2025-11/ADV_2025_22_Inflation_Adjustments.pdf>

Official Division advisory index:
<https://tax.ri.gov/guidance/advisories>

The retained four-page PDF has SHA-256
`755bc7584a6c67ba01b57da27056cd0283dcc9501d9131e7b55217decfac6eef`.
The generic official-document adapter snapshots the complete PDF and produces
one document container plus four page provisions under
`us-ri/guidance/tax/adv-2025-22`. Coverage is complete at five inventory
citations and five normalized provisions. The four page provisions deliberately
raise the reviewed `page_n` citation-path ratchet from 27,108 to 27,112.

Pages 1 and 2 publish the following tax-year-2026 personal-income-tax amounts:

- standard deductions of $11,200 for single and married-separate filers,
  $22,400 for joint and qualifying-surviving-spouse filers, and $16,800 for
  head-of-household filers;
- a $5,250 personal and dependency exemption unit;
- a modified-federal-AGI deduction and exemption phaseout range from $261,000
  through $290,800, with a $7,450 phaseout increment; and
- a uniform individual schedule of 3.75% through $82,050, 4.75% through
  $186,450, and 5.99% above $186,450, with displayed cumulative bases of
  $3,076.88 and $8,035.88.

The advisory is a mixed-year document. Pages 3 and 4 explicitly label their
Social Security, pension/401(k)/annuity, and property-tax-relief parameters as
tax-year-2025 amounts. Those values are retained for source completeness but
are not authority for a tax-year-2026 computation. The manifest therefore
records both covered years and separately identifies 2026 as the target tax
year.

The retained capture date is July 23, 2026; the expression/publication date
remains November 3, 2025. A fresh extraction during review reproduced the
official PDF and all inventory, provision, and coverage artifacts byte for
byte.

# Belgium RD no. 38 Article 13 pre-2025-10-01 expression ingest reasoning

Impact basis:

- axiom-corpus issue 217 records that the corpus held only the **post-2025-10-01
  consolidation** of Royal Decree no. 38 of 27 July 1967 (numac 1967072702,
  self-employed social status) Article 13. In the `2026-06-30-be-tax-benefit`
  version, the Article 13 body carries the `811,20 EUR` exemption threshold and
  the `14,70 p.c.` post-pension-with-pension rate only inside a `[¹ … ]¹` block
  whose Justel footnote is `(1) 2025-07-18/06, art. 237, 099; En vigueur :
  01-10-2025` — i.e. the whole §1 body is the text as substituted by the law of
  18 July 2025 (`L 2025-07-18/06`), effective 2025-10-01. There was no citable
  span for the **pre-2025-10-01 expression** of Article 13 §1/§2/§3.
- `rulespec-be/be/regulations/social_security/self_employed/contributions.yaml`
  encodes the Article 13 post-pension surface. The rules were originally dated
  `effective_from: '2025-10-01'` (the only En-vigueur date the post-amendment
  snapshot shows), which made the surface unrunnable for any 2025 period under
  strict-null resolution (rulespec-be#81). The fix backdated the rules to
  `2018-04-01`, source-attested against the external RSVZ/INASTI compilation PDF
  (`legislation_statut_social_dispositions_legales_10.pdf`) but not against the
  corpus. This ingest grounds the earlier `effective_from` in a corpus provision.

Official source (primary official, genuine ejustice consolidation):

- **Justel archived consolidated version n° 098** of RD no. 38 (numac
  1967072702), fetched via the ejustice archived-version endpoint
  `article.pl?arch=098&numac_search=1967072702&cn_search=1967072701`. The version
  page is marked **"En vigueur jusqu'au 01-10-2025"** — the expression in force
  UNTIL the 2025-10-01 amendment. Justel archives 99 historical consolidated
  versions ("Consultation des versions antérieures à partir du 03-04-1981");
  n° 098 is the last archived version before the current n° 099. `source_as_of`
  is set to `2025-09-30`, the day before the amendment took effect.

Verification (corrupt-a-digit standard — the amounts are present as amounts in
the resolved Article 13 body, under the pre-amendment structure, and the
post-amendment footnote is absent):

- Article 13 body (version `2026-07-05-be-rd38-art13-pre-2025`,
  `be/regulation/arrete/1967/07/27/1967072702/article/13`, 3712 chars) contains,
  verbatim:
  - `811,20 euros` / `811,20 EUR` — the §1 exemption threshold.
  - `14,70 p.c.` — the §1 post-pension-with-pension rate ("l'assujetti dont la
    pension … est effectivement payable"), the category the RSVZ compilation
    attests unchanged across the 2015/2016/2018 in-force texts.
  - `14,16 p.c.`, `20,50 p.c.`, `15.831,12` and `23.330,06` — the companion rate
    bands and income ceilings.
- The body does **not** contain `01-10-2025` or `2025-07-18/06`: the amounts sit
  under the older `[¹ … ]¹` / `[² … ]²` / `[⁴ … ]⁴` footnote markers referencing
  the pre-2025 amendment history (§2 "(supprimé)" per `L 2002-12-24/31`; §3/§4
  per `L 1983-06-15/33` and `ARN1 26 mars 1981`), structurally distinct from the
  renumbered §1-§5 current version. So this is genuinely the pre-amendment
  expression, not the current consolidation re-fetched.

Scope and identity:

- `be/regulation` version `2026-07-05-be-rd38-art13-pre-2025`: 68 provisions
  (the full archived RD no. 38 consolidation), coverage complete (68 sources
  matched, 0 missing, 0 extra). Article 13 is the target provision; the rest of
  the decree's articles come with it as the archived consolidation is a single
  document.
- The `citation_path` namespace (`be/regulation/arrete/1967/07/27/1967072702/…`)
  is the same numac as the current `2026-06-30-be-tax-benefit` version by design
  — this is the historical-versioning case. The artifact `id` is the path-only
  uuid5, but the Supabase load re-derives a **version-qualified** uuid5
  (`deterministic_provision_id(citation_path, version)`,
  `corpus/supabase.py:281`) so the same citation can coexist in both versions
  without an `on_conflict=id` collision (the loader's documented multi-version
  behaviour). `validate_citation_paths.py` reports RESULT: OK with no identity
  drift and no ratchet regression.

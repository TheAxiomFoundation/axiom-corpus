# Wisconsin 2026 Form 1-ES instructions (2026-07-22)

This scope preserves the Wisconsin Department of Revenue's official
tax-year-2026 *Form 1-ES Instructions – Estimated Income Tax for Individuals,
Estates, and Trusts*. The complete three-page PDF is document D-101A, revised
January 2026.

Page 2 publishes the 2026 individual exemptions and standard-deduction
schedules. The exemptions are $700 each for the taxpayer, a spouse on a joint
return, and every dependent, with an additional $250 for each eligible
taxpayer or spouse age 65 or older. The maximum standard deductions are
$13,960 for single taxpayers, $18,030 for heads of household, $25,840 for
married taxpayers filing jointly, and $12,280 for married taxpayers filing
separately; the page also preserves the corresponding income thresholds and
phaseout percentages.

Page 3 publishes gross-tax Schedules A, B, and C for full-year residents. All
three use rates of 3.5, 4.4, 5.3, and 7.65 percent. Schedule A (single, head of
household, estates, and trusts) uses thresholds of $15,110, $51,950, and
$332,720. Schedule B (married filing jointly) uses $20,150, $69,260, and
$443,630. Schedule C (married filing separately) uses $10,080, $34,630, and
$221,820. The source's base amounts and its nonresident and part-year-resident
proration note are retained in the normalized body.

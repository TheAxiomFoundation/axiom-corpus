Delaware TANF Title 16 4000 regulation corpus ingest reasoning

Impact basis:
- The current Axiom PolicyEngine parity queue identifies `de_tanf` as a P1 household-program surface.
- The queue item had no legal IDs, so corpus work started from source discovery instead of encoding from the PolicyEngine variable name.

Source hierarchy review:
- Started with Delaware Code, Title 31, because statute is the upstream authority for Delaware public assistance.
- Relevant statutory anchors:
  - 31 Del. C. 107 gives DHSS authority to adopt rules and regulations for public assistance programs.
  - 31 Del. C. 501 states legislative intent for aid and rehabilitation.
  - 31 Del. C. 502 defines terms including "standard of need."
  - 31 Del. C. 503(d) delegates TANF eligibility and assistance amounts to DHSS rules and regulations and imposes the support-cooperation condition.
  - 31 Del. C. 505(1) identifies TANF as a category of assistance.
  - 31 Del. C. 518 authorizes DHSS to establish TANF sanctions.
  - 31 Del. C. 524 provides the felony drug conviction exemption for TANF.
- Because the statute delegates the detailed TANF eligibility and grant formulas to DHSS rules, the maximally upstream operative source for the payment-standard and budgeting formulas is the official Delaware Administrative Code rule text.

Official source:
- Delaware Administrative Code, Title 16, 4000 Financial Responsibility.
- Source page: https://regulations.delaware.gov/AdminCode/title16/4000
- Official API PDF: https://regulations.delaware.gov/api/AdminCode/title16/4000/e3ad16a9-edc5-4935-a97b-fb9e2ca33b12
- The generic AdminCode page returned 403 to the unattended command-line client, but the official Delaware regulations API file URL downloaded successfully and was archived as the source artifact.
- Cornell and other secondary mirrors were not used for extraction.

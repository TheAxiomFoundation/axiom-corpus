ABAWD SPAN

North Dakota uses a 36-month rolling calendar period. This calendar begins for a participant with the first ABAWD month within the 36-month look back period. The 36-month rolling calendar progresses with each passing month and continues uninterrupted even while the individual is not participating.

 

Regaining Eligibility Criteria

(For individuals who have exhausted their three months in a 36-month period)

 

An ABAWD may regain eligibility by working and/or participating in approved training for a total of 80 hours within a 30 consecutive-day period.

 

All 80 hours must be within 30 consecutive days.

  • If an applicant An individual who submits an application for assistance under the SNAP program. applies having met the 80 hours within the 30 consecutive days prior to date of application, and meets all other eligibility factors, the participant is eligible effective the date of application.

  • If an applicant applies before meeting the full 80 hours, they are ineligible.

    • Deny the application.

    • When the participant verifies that the full 80 hours were met, within a 30 consecutive- day period ending no later than 30 days after date of application, the application can be reverted to open with benefits beginning the date the individual requalifies.

    • If a participant does not provide verification Third-party information or documentation used to establish the accuracy of statements. by the 30th day after the application, they will need to provide a new application.

An ABAWD may continue to receive SNAP if they continue to meet the work or training requirement (see SNAP Policy 402). There is no limit to how many times an individual may regain eligibility.

 

ELIGIBILITY FOR A SECOND “EXTENDED” 3-MONTH PERIOD FOR ABAWDS

Sometimes we see the following scenario:

  • participant exhausts initial three months.

  • participant then regains eligibility.

  • participant loses that regained exemption at a later date.

When this happens, the participant is eligible for a subsequent 3-month period.

The second set of consecutive 3 months shall begin the month the participant loses the “regained” ABAWD exemption. The participant will receive the subsequent 3 months regardless of ABAWD status. If the participant has no ABAWD exemption by the end of the third month, the benefits will terminate. The extended months can only be used once per 36-month period.

 

Example:An individual received the three ABAWD months in April, May and June and the case closed June 30 for failure to comply with the ABAWD work requirements. On August 16, the individual reapplies and verifies having worked 80 hours in a consecutive 30 days and lost the employment through no fault of their own. This individual is eligible for the extended three-month benefit period, September, October, and November. August does not count as one of the three months due to proration.

 

NOTE: An individual is not eligible for the three additional months if all other SNAP eligibility requirements are not met. For example, an individual cannot receive extended ABAWD months if they are currently disqualified for another reason.

 

SNAP Employment & Training

An ABAWD may choose to meet the work requirement through E&T (see SNAP 402 for work requirement details). The ABAWD individual is referred to SNAP E&T and, if they comply with SNAP E&T to meet the work requirements, the ABAWD time clock will not start counting (SNAP 301).

Open Cases

When an individual gains an ABAWD exemption after exhausting their three ABAWD months and the remaining members are still open on SNAP, the ABAWD is added according to the following:

  • ABAWD exemption due to employment – the ABAWD is added the month after they report and verify (SNAP 1104) they are working 20 hours a week, averaged monthly.

  • ABAWD exemption for attending a work program (SNAP E&T, WIOA or TAA) –the ABAWD is added the month after they report and verify enrollment (SNAP 402) .

  • Other ABAWD exemption – the ABAWD exemption is added the month the exemption is gained, regardless of when it is reported (SNAP 1104).

     

Simplified Reporting

ABAWDs are informed of the ABAWD time limit provision and necessary reporting requirements prior to or upon approval via an eligibility worker and written notice. ABAWDs are required to report:

  • when their household income exceeds the gross income limit (GIL), and/or

  • when their hours of work decrease below 20 hours a week, 80 hours averaged monthly.

  • whenever a member of the household wins substantial lottery or gambling winnings of $4,500 or greater.

The eligibility worker must determine and narrate if the ABAWD meets an individual time limit exemption at each point of contact.

 

Participants who Gain an Exemption During a Certification Period

If an ABAWD exemption is discovered during the certification period A period of time a household is approved to participate in SNAP. that was NOT required to be reported, we must determine if we need to apply an ABAWD exemption retroactively. If the gained exemption requires verification, we must pend for that information. When verified, we will remove the ABAWD months retroactively. If the exemption does not require verification (unless questionable), the ABAWD months will be removed upon notification.

 

Note: All reported information should be filtered through the unclear information flowchart.

 

When the exemption is verified or does not require verification, the exemption should be added for the month the participant gained the exemption. It does not matter if the month the exemption is gained is a full month.

 

Example: Participant reported exemption on October 16, and the participant is currently in their 2nd ABAWD month. We verify that the participant gained the exemption in September. We would remove September and October ABAWD months and update the ABAWD exemption in the integrated eligibility system.

 

Participants Who Lose an Exemption During a Certification Period

If during the certification period we receive information that indicates a household member no longer meets an exemption, we must follow unclear information policy.

 

For example, if we have not screened the household member for other exemptions, the impact of no longer meeting a specific exemption may not be clear. If it is unclear whether the household member meets another exemption, we must not act on the information and must not subject the household member to the ABAWD time limit until the household’s next application for review Filing a signed Application for Review via paper review form or through the self-service portal of the eligibility system for SNAP eligibility with the human service zone to determine continuing eligibility and establish a new review period..

 

Note: If the individual’s work hours drop below 80 hours a month or average 20 hours a week during their certification period and the participant fails to report and there are no other ABAWD exemption, apply countable months retroactively and evaluate any additional months for an overpayment SNAP benefits received by a household which exceeds the amount for which that household was eligible./IPV.

 

Adding and ABAWD Individual

When an ABAWD in a multiple member household isn’t initially eligible but later reports employment, this change Information that is different from what is currently used to determine eligibility and/or benefits. is acted upon under the ‘adding a household member’ rules.

 

Countable ABAWD Months from Another State

When an ABAWD applies for SNAP and they indicate that they received SNAP in another State, the eligibility worker must verify the number of countable ABAWD months received in the other State. Once verified, these months are added manually to the ABAWD time clock by the eligibility worker.

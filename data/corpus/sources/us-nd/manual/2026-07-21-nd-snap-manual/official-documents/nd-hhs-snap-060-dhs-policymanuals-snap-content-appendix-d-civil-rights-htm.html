SNAP:

Appendix D - Civil Rights

Overview

The State of North Dakota administers all aspects of the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) in compliance with the following U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) and the North Dakota Department of Health & Human Services (HHS) Nondiscrimination Statements. Discrimination To make any distinction of one person or a group of persons from other, either intentionally, by neglect, or by the effect of actions or lack of actions based on race, color, national origin, religion, sex, disability, age, marital status, family/parenteal status, income derived from a public assistance program, political beliefs, or reprisal or retaliation for prior civil rights activity. in any aspect of program administration is prohibited by these regulations, the Food and Nutrition Act of 2008, the Age Discrimination Act of 1975 (Pub. L. 94-135), the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 (Pub. L. 93-112, section 504), Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 (42 U.S.C. 12101), Title VI of the Civil Rights The right of individuals to enjoy equal standing with other individuals in interactions with the government or with the community. Act of 1964 (42 U.S.C. 2000d), and the North Dakota Human Rights Act . Enforcement action may be brought under any applicable Federal or North Dakota state law.

Right to File a Complaint

Individuals who believe that they have been subject to discrimination as specified above in the USDA or HHS Nondiscrimination Statement may file a complaint with the USDA or with HHS or with both agencies. HHS shall explain both the USDA and, if applicable, the HHS complaint system to everyone who expresses an interest in filing a discrimination complaint and shall advise the individual of the right to file a complaint in either or both systems.

 

To file a discrimination complaint with USDA, use the link below directions.

https://www.usda.gov/about-usda/general-information/staff-offices/office-assistant-secretary-civil-rights/how-file-program-discrimination-complaint

 

To file a discrimination complaint with HHS, use the link below for directions.

https://www.hhs.nd.gov/nondiscrimination-policy

 

Complaint Requirements

Any individual wanting to file a discrimination complaint shall be advised immediately of their right to do so and shall be given the necessary information (listed below) to file the complaint. When an individual asks for assistance with the complaint filing process, Human Service Zone (HSZ) staff shall assist the individual.

 

A written complaint must be filed no later than 180 days from the date of the alleged discrimination. All written complaints shall contain the following information:

  1. The name, address and telephone number or other means of contacting the person filing the complaint.

  2. The name and location of the organization or office accused of discriminatory practice.

  3. A description of the alleged discrimination incident.

  4. The reason for the alleged discrimination.( race, color, national origin, religion, sex, disability, age, marital status, family/parental status, income derived from a public assistance program, political beliefs, reprisal or retaliation for prior civil rights activity)

  5. The names, titles (if appropriate) and addresses of individuals who may have knowledge of the alleged discriminatory acts; and,

  6. The date(s) the alleged discriminatory action(s) happened.

If a complainant makes allegations verbally and is unable or is reluctant to put the allegations in writing, the State employee to whom the allegations are made shall document Used as a noun, as in documentary evidence indicates a written record of the circumstances of an event or fact. Used as a verb, indicates the act of entering the case file actual proof or statement of proof that the contents of the record are accurate. the complaint in writing. Every effort shall be made by the individual accepting the complaint to have the complainant provide the information specified in 1-6 above.

 

Human Service Zone Responsibilities

  1. Inform individuals of their right to file a civil rights complaint with HHS and/or USDA using steps identified above.

  2. Describe the process to everyone expressing an interest in filing a complaint.

  3. Advise individuals of their right to file a complaint and that any complaint filed with the State Office is forwarded to the appropriate Federal or State Office for investigation.

  4. If the individual indicates they will submit a complaint directly to the USDA or HHS, advise client both USDA and HHS will accept the complaint via email and fax but it MUST be in writing.

  5. If the individual makes verbal allegations and is reluctant to put their complaint into writing, the HSZ should attempt to obtain all required information from the client, document the complaint in writing and send this documentation to their direct supervisor within 1 business day The official working days of the week defined as the days between and including Monday to Friday, but do not include weekends and those public holidays during which the North Dakota Department of Health and Human Services is closed. If an application or review is filed, a change is reported or verification is provided on a day in which the human service zone office is closed but the Department is open, it will be considered received on that date..

  6. Assure all complaints meet the requirements stated above.

  7. Respond promptly to any requests from management regarding the complaint, and include your direct supervisor in any responses; and

  8. Do not engage in any further discussion with the client about the complaint, if the client initiates additional conversation assure them that they will hear from the agency they submitted their complaint to within 10 business days and inform your direct supervisor of the discussion.

     

Supervisor Responsibilities

  1. Acknowledge receipt of the information and remind staff member not to engage in any further discussion with the client regarding the complaint.

  2. Review Filing a signed Application for Review via paper review form or through the self-service portal of the eligibility system for SNAP eligibility with the human service zone to determine continuing eligibility and establish a new review period. directions https://www.hhs.nd.gov/nondiscrimination-policy for next steps with Legal.

  3. Respond promptly to any requests from management regarding the complaint and include your direct supervisor in any response.

  4. Train all staff involved in administering SNAP, on an annual basis, in civil rights requirements.

  5. Ensure all offices involved in determining eligibility or issuing SNAP benefits display nondiscrimination posters approved or provided by FNS; and

  6. Provide language interpreters and translation of documents in languages as requested for non-English or limited English speaking persons per the policy of HHS.

  7. Upon reaching minimum thresholds under 7 CFR 272.4, provide bilingual SNAP services in project areas.

  8. Complete the tracking log

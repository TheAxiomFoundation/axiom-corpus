1003 Mass Change

Overview

Mass changes Certain changes imposed by the federal or state government which may affect the entire caseload or significant portions of the caseload. are initiated by the state or federal government and generally affect the entire caseload or a significant portion of the caseload.

 

Mass Changes

Mass changes are effective the same date for all households. A notice is not required for mass change Information that is different from what is currently used to determine eligibility and/or benefits. situations listed in 1-5 below; however, it is best practice to send a notice to the household People who buy and prepare food together. Spouses, parents, and children who live together are usually counted as the same household. informing them of the benefit change.

 

Adequate notice A notice that must be sent to a household at least 10 days before the effective date of an action that reduces, suspends, or terminates a household’s benefit. if required to be sent to the household listed in 6-8 below.

 

Mass Changes include, but are not limited to the following:

  1. Adjustments to income eligibility standards (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program GMI or NMI Limits).

  2. Adjustments in the maximum SNAP allotment The total value of SNAP benefits a household is authorized to receive. (Thrifty Food Plan Diet required to feed a family in accordance with guidelines established by the U.S. Department of Agriculture. The cost of that diet is the basis for uniform allotments for all households. The thrifty food plan is based on 100% of the federal poverty level.).

  3. Adjustments in the standard deduction.

  4. Adjustments in the shelter or dependent A person who relies on another for support. care deduction.

  5. Annual and seasonal adjustments to utility standards.

  6. Periodic cost-of-living adjustments to Retirement, Survivors, and Disability Insurance (RSDI), Supplemental Security Income (SSI), and other federal benefits.

  7. Periodic cost-of-living adjustments to TANF Cash, Tribal TANF, or General Assistance (GA) payments.

  8. Other changes in the eligibility and benefit criteria based on legislative or regulatory changes.

References: 7 CFR 273.12(e)

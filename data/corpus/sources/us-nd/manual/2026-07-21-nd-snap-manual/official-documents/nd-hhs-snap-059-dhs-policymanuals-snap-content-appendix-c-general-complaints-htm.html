SNAP:

Appendix C - General Complaints

Overview

Federal law requires states to maintain a system for handling general program complaints ﬁled by participants, potential participants, or other concerned individuals or groups. This does not include complaints alleging discrimination To make any distinction of one person or a group of persons from other, either intentionally, by neglect, or by the effect of actions or lack of actions based on race, color, national origin, religion, sex, disability, age, marital status, family/parenteal status, income derived from a public assistance program, political beliefs, or reprisal or retaliation for prior civil rights activity. based on the protected classes outlined under Civil Rights The right of individuals to enjoy equal standing with other individuals in interactions with the government or with the community. law, or complaints that can be pursued through the fair hearing process.

 

Definition of General Complaint

Any complaint regarding such areas as processing standards or service to participants and potential participants.

 

Examples of SNAP General Complaints

  • Consistently late beneﬁt delivery – including delays in EBT.

  • Late processing of applications and/or reviews.

  • Staﬀ rudeness or misunderstandings.

  • Concern about oﬃce hours or access.

  • Information not being shared or telephone calls not being returned.

     

SNAP State Office Responsibilities

SNAP State Office will maintain records for three full federal ﬁscal years of complaints received and their disposition and will follow up to see that the complaints are responded to and resolved. The records will be reviewed at least annually to assess whether patterns of problems may be present in Human Service Zones oﬃces, project areas, or throughout the state and to identify causes of those problems. When warranted, SNAP State Office may have to take corrective action in the resolution of certain complaints.

 

In addition, SNAP State Office will make information on the complaint system available to participants, potential participants, and other interested persons. This can be done with written materials, posters, or other appropriate means.

 

Human Service Zone Director and Supervisor Responsibilities

Human Service Zone Directors and Supervisors are responsible for notifying participants and the public of their right to ﬁle general program complaints. The agency may make the information available through written materials or posters at certification oﬃces or other appropriate means. They will maintain the SFN 701 -Annual SNAP Program Complaint Log that contains at least the minimum information listed below and will follow up on complaints to see that they are responded to and resolved. Complaints need to be reviewed at least annually to see whether patterns of problems may be present and to identify possible causes of the problems. They are responsible to ensure that staﬀ is trained on the proper processing of complaints. Follow the instructions on the SFN 701 for submission expectations.

 

Instructions for SFN 701
Responsibilities Steps
Customer Support Center

  1. Complete columns 1 and 2 of SFN 701.

  2. Route the SFN 701 to the Zone HR Supervisor of the staff the complaint has been filed on.
Front Desk Staff

  1. Complete columns 1 and 2 of SFN 701.

  2. Route the SFN 701 to the Zone HR Supervisor of the staff the complaint has been filed on.

Eligibility Worker

  1. Complete columns 1 and 2 of SFN 701.

  2. Route the SFN 701 to the Zone HR Supervisor of the staff the complaint has been filed on.

Zone HR Supervisor

  1. Complete columns 3-6 of the SFN 701.

  2. Follow Zone procedures for filing and storing the SFN 701.

 

Complaint Logs

Complaint logs need to contain the following minimum information:

  • Column 1 - The date complaint was received.

  • Column 2 - Description of the complaint.

  • Column 3 - Staff assigned to resolve the complaint.

  • Column 4 - How the complaint was resolved.

  • Column 5 - Date complaint was resolved.

  • Column 6 - Corrective action plans taken.

 

Monitoring

Complaint logs are routinely reviewed during the SNAP Management Evaluation (ME) An evaluation procedure used to determine if State and local agencies are administering and operating Food and Nutrition Services (FNS) programs in accordance with program and civil rights regulations and requirements. Process and reviewed in the SNAP State Oﬃce. Logs are evaluated for completion, complaint resolution, and are analyzed for problems and potential causes and solutions. Compliance with the General Program Complaint procedure is noted in the report generated following all MEs.

     STATE OF ALASKA

Department of Health and Social Services

Division of Public Assistance

 

 

TRANSMITTAL NUMBER:   2011-04

 

MANUAL:   Alaska Food Stamp Program

 

DATE:   August 1, 2011

 

We are making several changes to clarify existing policy as a result of feedback from the field.  These changes include:

 

    • Clarification of the requirement to verify shelter deductions for individuals who live in subsidized housing;
    • Instruction on registering a recertification received the month after it's due; and
    • Clarification explaining when stolen benefits can be replaced.

 

Other changes are either technical in nature or clarifications that are the result of policy questions or suggestions from the field.

 

Highlights of these and other policy revisions are described below.  If you have any questions please contact the Policy and Program Development Team at 465-3382 or e-mail dpapolicy@alaska.gov.

 

OVERVIEW OF CHANGES

 

MS 602-4  Deductions

    • Adds an exception to clarify that an expense must be verified if it results in a deduction and is questionable.

 

MS 602-4 E  Shelter Deduction

    • Adds a note to section 7 stating that rent for individuals in subsidized housing must be verified.

 

MS 604-2 B  Recertification:  Application Filing Deadlines

    • Adds instruction on registering a recertification received the month after it's due.

 

MS 604-3 B  Status Reporting

    • Clarifies that new shelter costs must be verified if questionable when a status reporting household reports new shelter expenses.

 

MS 605-4 B  Subsistence Hunting and Fishing

    • Changes field name from SUBSIST HUNT/FISH to FS SUBSISTENCE ID CARD in second paragraph.

 

MS 606-1 A  Monthly Food Stamp Issuance

    • Updates EIS Procedure from 2000-10 to 2010-1

    • Adds a link to  EIS Procedure 2010-1

 

MS 606-3  Replacing Food Stamp Benefits

    • Changes the title of the 2nd section from “Replacing Benefits Already Spent” to “Replacing Benefits Lost in a Disaster” to more correctly describe this section.

    • Adds a new paragraph to explain that if an Alaska Quest card is reported stolen by a client and benefits were used, only the benefits that were used after the report of loss are eligible for replacement.

    • Adds an example.

STATE OF ALASKA

Department of Health and Social Services

Division of Public Assistance

 

 

TRANSMITTAL NUMBER:   2008-02

 

MANUAL:   Alaska Food Stamp Program

 

DATE:   September 2, 2008

 

The recently enacted Food, Conservation and Energy Act of 2008 (also referred to as the 2008 Farm Bill) contains several provisions that improve the Food Stamp Program.  Effective October 1, 2008, the $175/$200 cap on dependent care costs is eliminated, the standard deduction and minimum benefit are increased, and individual retirement accounts (IRAs) and college savings plans are excluded resources.

  

In addition, we expanded the residency policy to allow households who receive Medicaid from Alaska to continue to get Food Stamp benefits when they are out of state for medical treatment.  Several minor clarifications throughout the manual have also been made.

 

Highlights of these policy revisions are described below.  If you have any questions please contact the Policy and Program Development Team at 465-3382 or e-mail dpapolicy@alaska.gov.

 

OVERVIEW OF CHANGES

 

MS 600-2  Definitions

    • Updates the federal minimum wage amount that increased effective July 24, 2008.

 

MS 601-3E  Interviews Conducted at the DPADivision of Public Assistance Office

    • Clarifies that, for Food Stamp applications, a scheduled interview is one in which the applicant is given a date and time to come in for the appointment.  This requirement does not apply to the other programs.

    • Clarifies the timing of the automatic application denial action when the Food Stamp application is registered as a request for service (RF).

 

MS 602-1A(5)  Excluded Household Members

    • Revises note to clarify that the worker must obtain documentation that the felony conviction is expunged, not just set aside, before finding the individual eligible for Food Stamp benefits.

    • Adds definition of expunged to clarify policy

 

MS 602-1B  Residency

    • Expands the residency policy to allow households who are receiving Medicaid from Alaska and are out of state for medical treatment to continue to meet the residency requirement for Food Stamp Program purposes.

 

MS 602-2A(1)  Liquid Resources

    • Removes references to individual retirement accounts as countable resources.  These accounts are excluded effective October 1, 2008.

 

MS 602-2B(4)  Retirement Funds

    • Excludes retirement savings accounts when determining Food Stamp Program eligibility effective October 1, 2008.  A new EISEligibility Information System resource code is available that excludes the accounts for the Food Stamp Program but counts them for the other programs.

 

MS 602-2B(30)  College Savings Plans

    • Excludes college savings plans when determining Food Stamp Program eligibility effective October 1, 2008.  A new EISEligibility Information System resource code is available that excludes the accounts for the Food Stamp Program but counts them for the other programs.  The University of Alaska College Savings Plan continues to be excluded for all programs.

 

MS 602-4C  Dependent Care Deduction

    • Removes the $175/$200 limit on dependent care expenses effective October 1, 2008. This change will be made via the EISEligibility Information System mass change job scheduled for the end of August.

 

MS 603-3  Determining the Monthly Allotment

    • Removes references to the $10 minimum benefit

    • Adds a reference to Addendum 4 that lists the three new minimum benefit amounts for Urban, Rural I, and Rural II

    • NOTE:  The policy at MS 603-3A(1) relating to the initial month allotment being less than $10 has not changed.  Eligible one and two-person households entitled to the minimum allotment will not receive Food Stamp benefits for the initial month if the prorated amount is less than $10.

 

MS 604-1A  Length of Certification Period

    • Replaces earnings with earned income to provide consistent terminology

 

MS 604-3E  Verification Requirements

    • Clarifies that verification of shelter costs is needed when the household reports new shelter costs

 

MS 605-1B(3)  Budgeting Countable Educational Income

    • Adds reference to use the business mileage rate

    • Adds IRSInternal Revenue Service website to get the current business mileage rate

 

MS 605-2D(3)  Costs of Doing Business

    • Adds IRSInternal Revenue Service website to get the current business mileage rate

 

MS 605-3B  Expedited Service

    • Adds note to clarify that the case is not closed if the household fails to provide verification of deductible expenses

 

MS 605-7  Permanent Fund Dividend Special Processing

    • Removes reference to the $10 amount when addressing the minimum benefit

    • Revises MS 605-7B on process for checking the PFDPermanent Fund Dividend Interface and PFDPermanent Fund Dividend website

 

Addendum 4  Standards and Maximum Allotments

    • Increases the standard deduction to $246 for all households  

    • Increases the minimum benefits for eligible one and two-person households to equal 8% of the one-person allotment.  For Alaska, this means there are three minimum benefits, one for Urban, one for Rural I, and one for Rural II.

    • Removes reference to the $175/$200 dependent care deduction cap

    • Updates the gross and net income limits

    • Updates the maximum Food Stamp allotments

    • Updates the heating and non-heating utility standards

    • Moves Lake & Peninsula Census Area, Bristol Bay Census Area, and Dillingham Census Area from the South Central Utility Region to the Southwest Utility Region.  This change is made to better align communities that have similar utility costs.

    • NOTE:  These changes are effective October 1, 2008, and will be made via the EISEligibility Information System mass change job scheduled for the end of August.

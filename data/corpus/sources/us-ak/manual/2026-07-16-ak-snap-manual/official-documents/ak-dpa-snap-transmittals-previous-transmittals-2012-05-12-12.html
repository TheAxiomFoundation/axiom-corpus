TRANSMITTAL NUMBER:   2012-05

 

MANUAL:   Alaska Food Stamp Program

 

DATE:   December 1, 2012

 

This year Social Security announced a 1.7% Cost of Living Allowance (COLA) increase effective January 1, 2013.  This manual change updates program policy in conjunction with the COLACost of Living Allowance increase, which affect the dollar fee limits charged for services performed as a representative payee.  These fees are related to recipients of SSISupplemental Security Income and are subject to increase by the cost-of-living adjustment.

 

Other changes are either technical in nature or clarifications that are the results of policy questions or suggestions from the field.

 

Highlights of these and other policy revisions are described below.  If you have any questions, please contact the Policy and Program Development Team at (907)465-3328 or email dpapolicy@alaska.gov.

 

OVERVIEW OF CHANGES

 

MS 602-1 D Citizenship and Eligible Alien Status

  • Adds subsections 8 and 9 to clarify policy involving citizenship for children born in another country to US citizens and for children who are being adopted as mandated by the "Child Citizenship Act of 2000."

 

MS 602-3 B(2) Unearned Income - SSI/SSA/VA Income

  • Updates COLA budget for SSI/SSA/VA income.

 

MS 604-4 C Notice Requirements During the Certification Period

  • Adds a number 7 to include adverse action regarding incarcerated household members.

 

MS 605-1 D Institutions

    •  Updates the contact information for Behavioral Health.

 

MS 605-2 D Income from Self-Employment

  • Adds a note containing semi-annual reporting and anticipated income for self-employment households.

 

MS 605-7 G PFD at Recertification

    • Adds an example containing suspension versus denial in month before receipt of PFD at recertification.

 

MS 605-7 K Incorrect Payments

    • Updates the claim process for PFD Hold Harmless and Food Stamps.

 

MS 606-3 Replacing Food Stamp Benefits

    • Adds subtext to further detail replacement benefits in a time of disaster (though, not DSNAP benefits). Includes request by client, process for client and caseworker, and benefit amount replaced.
    • Adds examples.

Division of Public Assistance 

 

TRANSMITTAL NUMBER:   2019-02

 

MANUAL:   Alaska Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program

 

DATE:   September 3, 2019

 

This update includes yearly changes to the income and utility standards that take effect October 1, 2019. Other changes are either technical in nature or clarifications that are the results of policy questions or suggestions from the field.

 

Highlights of these and other policy revisions are described below. If you have any questions, please contact the Policy & Program Development team at dpapolicy@alaska.gov.

 

OVERVIEW OF CHANGES

 

MS 601-4       VERIFICATION

 

    • Clarified that certain types of verification are considered "verified upon receipt" as the information provided is not questionable, the provider is the primary source of the information, and no further information is needed to take action.

 

MS 601-5(B)       BENEFIT START DATE

 

    • Clarified that applications from incarcerated individuals must be denied.  We cannot adjust the benefit start date to the date of release or the first of the next month

 

MS 602-1(A)       HOUSEHOLDS

 

    • Clarified individuals who are age 60 or older who are unable to purchase food and prepare meals with others in the household because of a permanent disability may not be considered a separate household to children in the home in which they are considered the caretaker relative

    • Added that ex-felons convicted of aggravated sexual abuse, murder, sexual exploitation and abuse of children, or sexual assault who have served their sentence, are prohibited from participating in SNAP unless they comply with the terms of their parole

 

MS 602-1(F)       WORK REQUIREMENTS

 

    • Revised due to ABAWD requirements expected in the near future

 

MS 602-1(G)       DETERMINING WORK REQUIREMENTS AND EXEMPTIONS

 

    • Clarified the difference between SNAP benefit reductions of 25% due to ATAP work requirement penalty and a SNAP disqualification

    • Revised section on ABAWD's for clarity due to ABAWD requirements expected in the near future

 

MS 602-1(L)       ABLE-BODIED ADULTS WITHOUT DEPENDENTS (ABAWD's)

 

    • Revised due to ABAWD requirements expected in the near future

 

MS 602-2(B)       EXEMPT RESOURCES

 

    • Clarified income producing property and when it can be used as a resource exemption

    • Clarified resources of SSI recipients are no longer considered exempt if the SSI recipient is ineligible for SNAP due to a drug related felony

 

MS 602-2(D)       OTHER HOUSEHOLD RESOURCES

 

    • Added that households contain individuals with significant lottery or gaming winnings($25,000 or more won in a single game) are ineligible for SNAP benefits until they again meet normal income and resource standards.

 

MS 602-3(B)       UNEARNED INCOME

 

    • Clarified when SNAP benefit is not reduced when there is no ATAP or NFAP benefit issued in the month.

    • Added that households contain individuals with significant lottery or gaming winnings($25,000 or more won in a single game) are ineligible for SNAP benefits until they again meet normal income and resource standards.

 

MS 605-1(B)       STUDENTS

 

    • Added that participants enrolled in school as a requirement of their SNAP Employment and Training plan are eligible for SNAP if they are enrolled in career and technical education, remedial courses, basic adult education, literacy instruction, or English as a second language

 

MS 605-1(D)       INSTITUTIONS

 

    • Revised drug and alcohol treatment center responsibilities due to a final rule published by FNS.

 

MS 605-2(B)       EXCLUDED MEMBERS

 

    • Added ex-felons convicted of aggravated sexual abuse, murder, sexual exploitation and abuse of children, or sexual assault who have served their sentence are excluded members

    • Clarified resources of SSI recipients are no longer considered exempt if the SSI recipient is ineligible for SNAP due to a drug related felony

 

MS 605-3(B)       EXPEDITED SERVICE

 

    • Removed SNAP notice F154 as this notice is no longer used

 

 

ADDENDUM 2      ABAWD WORK REQUIREMENT EXEMPT AND NON-EXEMPT AREAS

 

    • Revised ABAWD 36-month period due to ABAWD requirements expected in the near future

 

ADDENDUM 4       SNAP STANDARDS AND MAXIMUM ALLOTMENTS

 

    • Updated the FFY 2020 maximum allotments and utility standards

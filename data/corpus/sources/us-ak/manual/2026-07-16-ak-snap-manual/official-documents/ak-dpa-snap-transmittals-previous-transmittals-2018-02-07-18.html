STATE OF ALASKA

Department of Health and Social Services

Division of Public Assistance 

 

TRANSMITTAL NUMBER:   2018-02

 

MANUAL:   Alaska Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program

 

DATE:   July 2, 2018

 

This update clarified caseworker responsibilities when there is a work required SNAP household member and replaced the term "semi-annual" with "simplified reporting" throughout the manual. Other changes are either technical in nature or clarifications that are the results of policy questions or suggestions from the field.

 

Highlights of these and other policy revisions are described below. If you have any questions, please contact the Policy & Program Development team at dpapolicy@alaska.gov.

 

OVERVIEW OF CHANGES

 

MS 602-1(H)       CASEWORKER RESPONSIBILITIES - WORK REQUIREMENTS

 

    • Clarified that the F001 must be sent at application and recertification when there is a work required SNAP household member, anytime a previously exempt individual becomes subject to work requirements, and anytime a work required household member moves into the SNAP household

 

MS 602-2(A)       DEFINITION AND TYPES OF RESOURCES

 

    • Clarified that loans, other than deferred repayment educational loans, are counted as a liquid resources the month after receipt if the money is retained as cash or in a bank account

 

MS 602-2(B)24       EXEMPT RESOURCES

 

    • Clarified that in order for resources held by recipients of TANF, SSI, or APA to be exempt, they must be solely owned by the TANF, SSI, or APA recipient

 

MS 602-3(B)6&7       UNEARNED INCOME

 

    • Clarified that BIA GA payments made to the household or to a vendor for the household are considered countable unearned income unless received after the month of intended use

 

MS 602-3(D)30        EXEMPT INCOME

 

    • Added note to clarify that BIA GA payments made to the household or to a vendor for the household are considered countable unearned income unless received after the month of intended use. Only BIA Burial Assistance payments made to a vendor are exempt.

 

MS 602-4(D)       MEDICAL DEDUCTION

 

    • Replaced link to IRS Standard Mileage Rates in 602-4(D)1(h) for the current information

 

MS 604-3       REPORTING AND ACTING ON CHANGES DURING THE CERTIFICATION PERIOD

 

    • Clarified that an EBT transaction report showing out of state usage may be an indicator that a household moved out of state but should not be used as the only basis for closure. We must request proof of residency.

 

MS 605-1(B)            STUDENTS

 

    • Revised student eligibility section to match federal regulations for clarity

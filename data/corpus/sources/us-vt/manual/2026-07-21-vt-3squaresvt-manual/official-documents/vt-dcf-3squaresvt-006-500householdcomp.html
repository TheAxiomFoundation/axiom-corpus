500.1 Eligible member Determination

  1. To determine who is non-financially eligible for 3SVT you must:
    1. Start with everyone who lives together.
    2. Determine who purchases and prepares (shares food) together for home consumption.
    3. Apply relationship rules.
    4. Apply non-financial eligibility rules to determine who is an eligible or excluded member.
    5. No one can be an eligible member of two or more households in any month.
  2. Exception: Residents of Shelters for Battered Persons provided, the battered person was a member of the same household as the person who abused him or her.

500.2 Living Together

500.2.1 Determination

  1. Evaluate everyone living together to determine who must be included in the 3SVT household. 
  2. Living together means sharing a home where people usually sleep and share any common living spaces such as kitchen, bathroom, bedroom or living room. People who share only an access area such as an entrance or hallway or non-living areas such as a laundry room or garage are not considered living together.
  3. An individual spending any amount of time in the household can be considered living in the household. 

500.2.2 Temporary Absence

  1. A person who is temporarily absent from the household is considered living with the household.  
  2. A person’s absence is temporary if all the following are true:
    1. the person’s location is known,
    2. the person lived with the household before the absence (consider newborns to have lived with the household),
    3. there is a definite plan for return, and
    4. the absence has lasted or is expected to last 30 days or less.
  3. Note: People that maintain Vermont residency and vacation or temporarily live out of state are still considered as living together. See Residency.

500.3 Purchase and Prepare 

500.3.1 3SVT Household Determination

  1. People who live together and share food must be included in the 3SVT household. People share food if they:
    1. share in the cost of buying food, and/or 
    2. share in preparing the food, regardless of who paid for it, and/or
    3. eat from the same food supply, regardless of who paid for it.
  2. Note: A disabled person who is unable to purchase and prepare their own food and has arranged to have their food purchased and prepared separately is not considered as sharing food. They can have separate 3SVT status from others in the household. 
  3. Types of households are:
    1. A person living alone.
    2. A group of people living together who share food.
    3. A person (or group of people) living with others, but who do not share food with the others.
  4. Note: The intent to purchase food and prepare meals separately also establishes separate household status, even if the intent depends on receiving 3SVT.

500.3.2 Senior and Unable to Purchase/Prepare Food 

  1. A person and spouse may be a separate household even if they purchase and prepare with others if all the following are true:
    1. one of them is 60 years or older; and
    2. that person cannot purchase and prepare meals because of:
      1. a disability the SSA considers permanent, or
      2. another permanent physical or mental non-disease related disability; and
    3. the gross monthly income for the number of others in the household does not exceed 165% of the federal poverty level165% of the federal poverty level.

500.4 Relationships

500.4.1 Inclusion in 3SVT Household

  1. People in the specified relationships must be included in the 3SVT household

500.4.2 Spouses

  1. People who are legally married and living together must be in the same household.
  2. Note: Couples who are in a civil union are not spouses. 

500.4.3 Parents and Children

  1. Children include natural, step and adopted children.
  2. Parents and their children 21 years of age and younger who live together must be in the same household even if the child(ren) have their own spouse or child who lives in the same household.
  3. Children living in the home any amount of time can be screened for benefits.
  4. Note: Children residing in a household less than half time are not mandatory household members but can be included at the household’s request. Children residing in a household half time or more are mandatory members if not on 3SVT in another household. See Joint or Shared Physical Custody below.  
  5. Note: The step-parent relationship ends with the death of or divorce from the parent. The  Natural Parent relationship ends with the adoption of the child.
  6. Exception: If a child, biological parent and guardian of the child all live in the same household, then the 3SVT should be with the guardian unless the guardian is not seeking 3SVT for the child.  

500.4.3.1 Joint or Shared Physical Custody

  1. A child may only be a member of one 3SquaresVT household. The first household to apply may include the child regardless of the percentage of custody.  If a child spends an equal amount of time in each household, the custodians decide which household can claim the child. If the custodians cannot decide, include the child in the household that applies first.
  2. Transfer the child to the other household if:
    1. the parent dies, or
    2. the second household, after applying for benefits, provides verification that the child primarily resides with them.  
  3. Note: A child residing in the home 50% or more of the time and who is not on 3SVT in another household must be considered a mandatory household member.

500.4.4 Minor Under Parental Control

  1. A minor is a person under 18 years of age. A minor under the parental control of an adult must be in the same household as the adult. Parental control means the minor is dependent on the adult for financial or other support. 
  2. Exceptions:
    1. Foster care providers and their foster children, see Foster Children and Adults.
    2. A minor living with his or her own spouse or child and with an adult who is not the minor’s parent. The minor and spouse/children can be a separate household if they do not purchase and prepare food with others.

500.4.5 Foster Children and Adults

  1. The household may choose to include or exclude a foster child or foster adult who lives with them. If excluded the foster care payment is not income to the household. If excluded, the foster child or foster adult is not eligible as a separate group.
  2. If the household excludes the foster child and the foster child spends any amount of time with their biological parent, then the biological parent can apply for 3SVT for the child. 

500.4.6 Difficulty of Care Program (DCP) Recipients

  1. The household may choose to include or exclude the DCP recipient that lives with them and for whom they are providing care. If excluded, the DCP recipient is not eligible as a separate group. 
  2. See Difficulty of Care Program (DCP) Payments for information about how to handle the DCP payments.

500.5 Excluded Persons/Households

500.5.1 Types of Persons Excluded in Households

  1. There are three types of persons excluded from the household:
    1. Disqualified members 
    2. Ineligible members 
    3. Non-members 
  2. Sometimes entire households are ineligible for program benefits. 

500.5.2 Disqualified Members

  1. These are people who would be household members but are not allowed to be. Their resources, income and deductions are counted as if they were eligible members, see resources and income of Disqualified Members.
    1. Criminal Justice Issues - Persons who are fleeing felons.  Persons who are probation/parole violators. See Fleeing Felons and Probation/Parole Violators.
    2. IPV - Person who signed an IPV hearing waiver, or who was found guilty of an IPV by an administrative disqualification hearing or court of law. See Intentional Program Violations.
    3. Quality Control Non-cooperation - Households that do not cooperate with either state or federal quality control reviewers. See Quality Control.
    4. Work Requirements- Persons who failed to meet Work Requirements.  

500.5.3 Ineligible Members

  1. These are people who would be household members, but they did not meet one of the non-financial eligibility requirements in the sections noted below. Resources are counted as if they were eligible; income and deductions are prorated.  See Special Household Situations, Ineligible Members.
    1. Non-Citizens - Persons who failed to meet eligibility rules in Citizen/Non-Citizen.
    2.  SSN - Persons who failed to provide or apply for an SSN as per Social Security Number.
    3. Time Limited Benefits - Persons who are not meeting the work requirement and who have used-up their 3-month eligibility. See 3SquaresVT Time Limited Benefits.

500.5.4 Non-members

  1. Certain persons who live with and may even share food with the household are treated as non-members for purposes of determining eligibility and benefits. Non-members’ income and resources are not included when determining eligibility and benefits for the household.

500.5.4.1 Boarders

  1. A boarder is anyone (or group) who resides with a household and pays reasonable compensation for lodging and meals. The household may choose to include or exclude a boarder (or boarder group) that pays reasonable compensation. If excluded, the boarder (or group) is not eligible for 3SVT as a separate household.
  2. Reasonable compensation exists when the total paid by the boarder (or group) is equal to:
    1. The maximum benefit for the boarder (group) size when the boarder (group) pays for more than 2 meals per day. 
    2. 2/3 of the maximum benefit for the boarder (group) size when the boarder (group) pays for 2 meals or less per day.
  3. See  Reasonable Room and Board CompensationReasonable Room and Board Compensation

  4. Spouses, and parents and children under 22 are not treated as boarders even if a board payment is being made.
  5. Persons paying less than reasonable compensation are not boarders, they are household members.

500.5.4.2 Live-In Attendants

  1. Live-in attendants live with the household to provide medical, housekeeping, dependent care or other similar personal services.  Live-in attendants may be eligible for 3SVT as a separate household.
  2. Spouses, and parents and children under 22 are not treated as live-in attendants even if they are providing these types of services.
  3. Note: A spouse may be an essential person for purposes of the Essential Persons Program. See Essential Person Program Payments for treatment of the income.

500.5.4.3 Residents of Commercial Boarding Houses

  1. People who reside in a commercial boarding house are not eligible for 3SVT.
  2. A commercial boarding house is a business that offers meals and lodging for payment with the intent of making a profit. Determine intent to make a profit from a statement by the applicant or the owner. 

500.5.4.4 Residents of Institutions

  1. People who receive over 50% of three meals daily from an institution (college and reside in campus housing, nursing home, etc.) where they live, are residents of institutions. Residents of institutions are not eligible for 3SVT.
  2. Note: This rule does not apply to people who receive less than 50% of three meals daily.
  3. Exception: Residents of institutions described in Special Household Situations.

500.5.4.5 Roomers

  1. People who pay the household a flat-fee for lodging. They do not pay a separate amount for utilities. They do not purchase and prepare food with the household they rent from. Roomers may be eligible for 3SVT as a separate household.
  2. Spouses, and parents and children under 22 are not treated as roomers even if they pay each other for lodging.
  3. Note: Many separate households live together and share shelter expenses. This is not a roomer situation. Click for  Shared Households definition. Examples: Shared Households vs Roomers.

500.5.4.6 Strikers

  1. A household with a striking member(s) may or may not be eligible for benefits, see Strikers.

500.5.4.7 Students

  1. Students in higher education who do not meet the rules in Students are not eligible for 3SVT.

500.6 Special Living Situations

500.6.1 Institutional Eligibility

  1. People who receive more than 50% of their meals while living in the following institutions may be eligible if they meet program requirements. 

500.6.2 Addiction Treatment Centers

  1. A public or private nonprofit residential facility for the treatment of drug addicts or alcoholics. The children of a resident (but not the spouse) who lives with them in the center are part of the resident’s household. 
  2. See Addiction Treatment Centers for special requirements.

500.6.3 Group Living for the Disabled

  1. A public or nonprofit residence that provides housing for no more than 16 disabled persons. The resident must be  DisabledDisabled to be eligible for 3SVT.
  2. See Group Living for the Disabled for special requirements.

500.6.4 Homeless Shelters

  1. A public or private nonprofit facility that provides temporary housing for homeless people. The household is defined as a single person or a person and spouse and children.
  2. See Homeless Shelters and Meal Providers for special requirements.

500.6.5 Shelters for Battered Persons

  1. A public or private nonprofit residential facility that provides temporary housing for battered persons. The household is the battered person and his/her children.
  2. See Shelters for Battered Persons for special requirements.

500.6.6 Subsidized Housing for Seniors

  1. Federally subsidized housing for seniors built under either Section 202 of the Housing Act of 1959 or Section 236 of the National Housing Act. The household is the senior and spouse. Determine eligibility as for any other household.

500.7 Verification

500.7.1 Basic Household Composition

  1. Verify if questionable.

500.7.2 Excluded Persons

  1. Verification requirements are in the specified item. If no item is specified, verify if questionable.

500.7.3 Primary Residence to Transfer a Shared-Custody Child 

  1. In a shared custody situation, if the non-assigned household applies for benefits and wants to claim the child as a member, it must verify the child primarily resides with it.

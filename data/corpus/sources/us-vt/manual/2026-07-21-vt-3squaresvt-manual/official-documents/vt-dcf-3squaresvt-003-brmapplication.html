200.1 Overview

  1. The ESD 202, Application, must be readily available to potentially eligible households. Provide an ESD 202 to anyone who requests the form.
  2. 3SquaresVT in a  SNAP households have the option to complete a simplified application, the ESD 202 3SNP. If a 3SquaresVT in a SNAP application is submitted for a household that is not 3SquaresVT in a SNAP eligible, the ES will note in CATN all 202 questions during the interview.
  3. Encourage the household to file an ESD 202 the day they contact the agency requesting information about 3SVT.  Tell the household that applying for 3SVT does not affect time limits for other programs. 
  4. Tell households who contact the agency for 3SVT but do not wish to come to the office:
    1. they can apply online at http://dcf.vermont.gov/mybenefits/apply, or 
    2. an ESD 202 can be mailed to them the same day.

200.2 Application Process 

200.2.1 Application Requirements

  1. The application process begins when the household files a complete ESD 202 for 3SVT benefits. Households must complete the following actions as part of the application process:
    1. submit and complete the ESD 202,
    2. an interview, and 
    3. verify all required information.
  2. Deny the application if the household refuses to cooperate with any of these actions.

200.2.2 Filing and Completing the Application

  1. The application process begins when the household submits an ESD 202 for 3SVT benefits with, at a minimum, the:
    1. name,
    2. address, and 
    3. signature of the primary person or AR.
  2. Do not require the household to submit the remainder of the ESD 202 when only the minimum information is received. Ask the remaining questions during the interview and document the answers in CATN.

  3. Note: Electronic signatures through online applications are acceptable.  Electronic typed signatures, with or without authentication measures, are acceptable. Applications signed by a “mark” do not require the signature of a witness.

  4. Return incomplete ESD 202s to the household for completion. Obtain any other missing information needed to complete the application during the interview.
  5. The ESD 202 may be submitted:
    1. In-person,
    2. By mail, 
    3. By fax, or
    4. Online electronic application.  
  6. Application for 3SVT may be made by any of the following people:
    1.  Head of Household
    2. Spouse of the head of household
    3. Other responsible household member
    4. Authorized representative (AR)
      1. See: General Information for AR qualifications.
      2. See: Special Household Situations for AR requirements related to these households.

200.2.2.1 Online Applications 

  1. Provide households who file their ESD 202 online at a district office:
    1. An opportunity to review the electronic ESD 202, and 
    2. A copy of the information submitted on the electronic application.

200.2.3 Application Date

  1. The application date is the date a complete ESD 202 is received and date stamped in the:
    1. district office,
    2.  ADPC.

200.2.3.1 Online and FAX Submission

  1. If the ESD 202 is submitted during normal work hours, use that date as the application date.
  2. If the ESD 202 is submitted after hours, weekends or holidays, use the next business day as the application date.
  3. Interviews are not required to be completed on the application date. Accept ESD 202s with only the minimum requirements and record the date accepted as the application date.

200.2.4 ACCESS Entry of the Application

  1. Enter the application on the date it is received. Use the program identified under the Household Information section of the ESD 202 if the requested program isn’t marked on page one. Screen the application for expedited processing on the day the application is received in the district office. 
  2. Indicate on the STAT/STAT panel if the household is eligible for 3SquaresVT or 3SquaresVT in a SNAP.
  3. Review PERS/D/HIST to see if the household members are known to ACCESS.

200.2.4.1 Withdrawal of the Application

  1. Households may withdraw their application at any time before their eligibility is determined.  ES must:
    1. Contact the household to confirm the withdrawal,
    2. CATN:
      1. the contact with the household, and 
      2. the reason for withdrawal.
    3. Send an ESD 220, Notice of Decision to the household to advise:
      1. the application was denied due to the withdrawal request, and
      2. the household may reapply. 

200.2.5 Joint Applications for Agency Programs

  1. Households may use the same application and apply at the same time for 3SVT and other agency programs, such as  Reach Up.
  2. Complete a single interview for households jointly applying.

  3. Note: A 202 received for an active 3SVT case, not due for recertification, must be used as a change report. The certification period cannot be changed.

  4. Process 3SVT benefits following the required 3SVT policies and timeframes. Do not delay processing 3SVT because the other program is pending.

  5. Households are not required to file another application for 3SVT when their application for the other program is denied. 3SVT cannot be denied solely because the other program’s application was denied.
  6. Process 3SVT benefits without including cash assistance when it’s uncertain the cash program will be approved, or the amount is unknown. Process 3SVT to include the cash assistance when that program is approved. Notice of adverse action is not required for joint applications when 3SVT benefits are reduced because a cash assistance program is approved. 

200.2.6 Offender Benefits Enrollment Initiative

  1. A special application process for offenders who are about to be released. 
  2. Follow the normal application process outlined in Application Process and Interview.

200.3 Interviews

200.3.1 Interview Requirement

  1. Households must complete an interview at application.  

200.3.2 Scheduling

  1. Send the ESD 202C, Benefits Interview Appointment Notice, when you receive the ESD 202. Allow the household five calendar days to complete the interview.
    1. Note: The ADPC sends the ESD 202C for applications received by them.
    2. Note: The ADPC does not send the ESD 202C when a 3SquaresVT in a SNAP application is received by them. The ES will send the ESD 202C after two cold calls are unsuccessful.
  2. Complete the interview as soon as possible after the application date to allow the household to receive benefits within 30-days of that date.
  3. Exception: Complete an interview immediately for households qualifying for expedited processing.

200.3.3 Interview

200.3.3.1 Who to Interview

  1. Hold the interview with one of the following persons:
    1. Head of Household,
    2. Spouse,
    3. Other responsible household member,
    4. Authorized representative

  2. Note: People being interviewed may bring anyone they choose to the interview.

  3. Note: Do not use children as interpreters.

200.3.3.2 How to Complete the Interview

  1. Complete the interview by telephone. Make two cold call attempts within 5 days to the household to complete the interview when they are not at the office.
  2. Note: If the customer does not provide a phone number CATN that cold calls were attempted but could not be completed because a phone number was not provided.
  3. Exception: Complete a face-to-face interview for the following reasons:
    1. Household or authorized representative request.
    2. Needed to resolve or clarify specific issues.
  4. CATN if the interview was completed by telephone or face-to-face.

200.3.3.3 Interview Process

  1. Review ACCESS for household information known to the agency. 
  2. Review the ESD 202 by:
    1. going over each question and 
    2. resolving any unclear or missing information with the person being interviewed.  
  3. Advise the person of the household’s:
    1. Basic 3SVT facts
    2. Processing timeframes
    3. How benefits are issued
    4. Rights and responsibilities
    5. General Work Requirements
    6. 3SVT Time Limited Benefits
    7. Responsibility to report changes 
    8. Verification needed to determine eligibility
  4. Explain that time limits and requirements for other programs do not apply to 3SVT.
  5. Provide the household with an ESD 202V, Verification Request, listing the required verifications needed to determine eligibility and the due date. 
  6. Advise the household that the verifications must be returned within ten-days of the interview.

200.3.3.4 Missed Interview

  1. Send Households who miss their scheduled interview an ESD 202D, NOMI via ACCESS.  The NOMI tells the household:
    1. they missed their interview appointment, 
    2. they are responsible to reschedule the interview appointment, and 
    3. the application will be denied on the 30th day if the interview is not completed.
  2. Note: If not already done, make two cold call attempts to the household to complete the interview prior to sending the NOMI.
  3. Deny the application on the 30th day from the application date if the household misses their first interview and does not reschedule.
  4. Deny the application on the next business day when the 30th day falls on a weekend or holiday. 
  5. Households that complete their interview within the 30-day application processing period must have their eligibility determined. 

200.4 Verification

  1. Required verifications must be provided before approving the application.
  2. Exception: Expedited Service. 
  3. See Individual items for verification requirements.

200.5 Refusal to Cooperate 

200.5.1 Denial Procedures

  1. Deny households refusing to cooperate in completing the application process at the time of refusal. Refusal to cooperate means the household is able to cooperate but shows the ES they will not take required actions to complete the application process.  ExampleExample

    A household telling you they will not be interviewed shows refusal to cooperate. A household not showing for their scheduled interview is not refusal to cooperate, and the application must not be denied for refusal. 

  2. Households are not found uncooperative when someone outside of the household fails to provide requested verifications.
  3. Note: Disqualified and Ineligible Members are part of the household for this purpose.
  4. See Disqualification for Refusal to Cooperate for information about cooperation with Quality Control.

200.5.2 CATN Refusal

  1. CATN the following information when a household has refused to cooperate:
    1. The action the household was asked to take,
    2. The reason the action is required to determine eligibility,
    3. The household is capable of completing the action,
    4. How the agency informed the household of the actions they need to take, and the dates they were informed, and 
    5. The household has indicated verbally or in writing that it chooses not to take the action.

200.6 Processing the Application

200.6.1 Application Review Procedures

  1. Review the household’s:
    1. financial and non-financial eligibility requirements, 
    2. data match information, and 
    3. verifications.  

200.6.2 Processing Timeframes

  1. Process applications within ten days when all requirements have been met, but not later than 30-calendar days from the application date. 
  2. Exception: See Expedited Services, Processing Timeframe.  

200.6.2.1 Verifications Not Returned Within Ten Days

  1. Deny the application if the household has not returned the required verifications within ten days. 
  2. Send the household an ESD 220 advising them of the denial and that they may have their application reopened if they provide the required verifications by the 30th day from the application date.  
    1. If no response at all to verification request additional wording is not needed.
    2. If partial verification is received, add additional wording to explain what is still needed.  Example: xxxxxx was requested on x date and due by x date, as of the date of this notice the xxxxxx has not been received.
  3. Reopen the application when verifications are provided by the 30th day from the application date. Determine eligibility and provide benefits from the application date, if eligible.  Follow the process below when verifications are received 31 to 60-days from the application date.

200.6.3 Anticipated Changes

200.6.3.1 Eligible Initial Month Only

  1. Households eligible for the initial month but not eligible for future months receive benefits for the initial month only and then are denied.  

200.6.3.2 Ineligible for Initial Month Only

  1. Households ineligible for the initial month but eligible for the next month, or subsequent months do not need to reapply.  Begin the household’s certification period the month of application, but issue benefits beginning the month the household is eligible. Do not issue benefits for the initial month or ineligible months.

200.6.4 Delayed Processing

200.6.4.1 Review Determinations of Eligibility Not Completed

  1. Review determinations of eligibility not completed within 30-days from the application date for the cause of the delay. CATN the reason for the delay.  
  2. ACCESS will create a "daily edit" on the 26th day asking for a reason for the delay in processing.  ES must enter the reason in the STAT panel.  ACCESS will default to an agency caused delay if ES doesn’t enter a reason in the STAT panel.  ACCESS sends the household a 28-day notice advising the household of the delay and the reason. 

200.6.4.2 Agency Caused Delays

  1. Continue to pend the application until eligibility is determined. Do not deny the application when delayed due to the agency or SSA. 
  2. Determine eligibility as soon as possible, and if eligible, restore benefits from the application date.

200.6.4.3 Household Caused Delays

  1. Delays are the fault of the household if the household failed to complete the application process and ES cannot take any further action.

200.6.4.4 Verification Received 31 to 60-Days from Application Date

  1. Reopen the application when required verification is received after denial but 31 to 60-days from the application date. Issue benefits from the date the missing requirements were provided, if eligible.  Benefits are not provided for the month of application when the household caused the delay. Households are not required to complete a new ESD 202.  

200.7 Application Decision

200.7.1 Approval

  1. Provide eligible households the opportunity to receive benefits as soon as possible, but no later than 30-calendar days from the application date.  
  2. Issue a combined benefit when the household:
    1. applies after the 15th of the month, and 
    2. is found eligible for the initial month and the following month.

200.7.2 Denial

  1. Deny the application as soon as possible, but no later than 30-calendar days from the application date for households found ineligible for benefits.  
  2. Households must file a new ESD 202 if they wish to receive benefits.
  3. Exception: Verifications Not Returned Within Ten Days

200.8 Certification Periods

  1. Provide the longest certification period possible based on the household’s situation.
    1. Assign a 36-month certification period to 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households where all household members are:
      1.  Senior or  disableddisabled, and

        A person who meets one or more of the following:

        • Receives disability or blindness benefits from any of these programs: Social Security,   SSI, or   MABD.
          • A person meets this even when the full benefit is withheld or deferred due to recoupment.
        • Receives a federally or state administered SSI supplement based on disability or blindness, or section 212(a) of PL 93-66.
        • Receives a disability retirement benefit from a government agency for a   disability considered permanent by SSAdisability considered permanent by SSA.

          Any of the following conditions:

          • Permanent loss of use of both hands, both feet, or one hand and one foot.
          • Amputation of leg at hip. Amputation of leg or foot because of diabetes or peripheral vascular diseases.
          • Total deafness, not correctable by surgery or hearing aid. Statutory blindness, unless caused by cataracts or detached retina.
          • IQ of 59 or less, established after the person becomes 16 years old.
          • Spinal cord or nerve root lesions resulting in paraplegia or quadriplegia.
          • Multiple sclerosis with damage to the nervous system caused by scattered areas of inflammation. The inflammation recurs and has progressed to varied interference with the function of the nervous system, including severe muscle weakness, paralysis, and vision and speech defects.
          • Muscular dystrophy with irreversible wasting of the muscles, impairing the ability to use the arms or legs.
          • Impaired renal function caused by chronic renal disease, resulting in severely reduced function which may require dialysis or kidney transplant.
          • Amputation of a limb of a person at least 55 years old.
        • Is a veteran the VA considers totally (100%) disabled or permanently housebound or in need of regular aid and attendance.
        • Is a veteran’s surviving spouse who the VA considers:
          • in need of regular aid and attendance, or
          • permanently housebound, or
          • approved for benefits because of the veteran’s death and has a disability considered permanent by SSA.
        • Is a veteran’s surviving child who the VA considers:
          • incapable of self-support, or
          • approved for benefits because of the veteran’s death and has a disability considered permanent by SSA
      2. all household members have no  Earned Income.
        1. Note: RUFA and EP households are not eligible for 3SquaresVT in a SNAP.
    2. Assign a 24-month certification period to households where all adult members are:
      1.  Senior or  disableddisabled, and

        A person who meets one or more of the following:

        • Receives disability or blindness benefits from any of these programs: Social Security,   SSI, or   MABD.
          • A person meets this even when the full benefit is withheld or deferred due to recoupment.
        • Receives a federally or state administered SSI supplement based on disability or blindness, or section 212(a) of PL 93-66.
        • Receives a disability retirement benefit from a government agency for a   disability considered permanent by SSAdisability considered permanent by SSA.

          Any of the following conditions:

          • Permanent loss of use of both hands, both feet, or one hand and one foot.
          • Amputation of leg at hip. Amputation of leg or foot because of diabetes or peripheral vascular diseases.
          • Total deafness, not correctable by surgery or hearing aid. Statutory blindness, unless caused by cataracts or detached retina.
          • IQ of 59 or less, established after the person becomes 16 years old.
          • Spinal cord or nerve root lesions resulting in paraplegia or quadriplegia.
          • Multiple sclerosis with damage to the nervous system caused by scattered areas of inflammation. The inflammation recurs and has progressed to varied interference with the function of the nervous system, including severe muscle weakness, paralysis, and vision and speech defects.
          • Muscular dystrophy with irreversible wasting of the muscles, impairing the ability to use the arms or legs.
          • Impaired renal function caused by chronic renal disease, resulting in severely reduced function which may require dialysis or kidney transplant.
          • Amputation of a limb of a person at least 55 years old.
        • Is a veteran the VA considers totally (100%) disabled or permanently housebound or in need of regular aid and attendance.
        • Is a veteran’s surviving spouse who the VA considers:
          • in need of regular aid and attendance, or
          • permanently housebound, or
          • approved for benefits because of the veteran’s death and has a disability considered permanent by SSA.
        • Is a veteran’s surviving child who the VA considers:
          • incapable of self-support, or
          • approved for benefits because of the veteran’s death and has a disability considered permanent by SSA
      2. all household members have no  Earned Income.
    3. Households who do not qualify for a 36-month or 24-month certification period receive a 12-month certification period.
    4. Do not change a certification period based solely on a household applying for another program . 

200.9 Determination Notices

200.9.1 Determination Notice Procedures

  1. Provide an ESD 220 advising the household of their approval or denial for 3SVT benefits as soon as possible, but no later than 30-days after the application date. 
  2. Exception: Expedited Service, Processing Timeframes.

200.9.2 Approval Notices

  1. Approval notices must contain the following information:
    1. benefit amount,
    2. certification period,
    3. benefit level changes at certification,
    4. combined benefit amounts when appropriate,
    5. fair hearing rights, and
    6. contact name and number for questions.

200.9.3 Denial Notices

  1. Denial notices must contain:
    1. the reason the household was denied, 
    2. their right to a fair hearing, 
    3. their right to reapply,
    4. contact name and number for questions,
    5. actions the household must take to reactivate the application within 30-days of the notice date, if applicable, and
    6. a new application must be submitted if the household failed to complete an interview after two interview requests and made no further contact with the agency within 30-days of the application date.

200.10 Benefits

  1. Provide benefits to households found eligible for 3SVT.  
  2. See Benefits for issuance information for:
    1. Initial Months, 
    2. Combined Benefits, 
    3. Suspended Benefits, and 
    4. Prorated Benefits.

1600.1 Determination of Household Income

  1. To determine a household’s eligibility and benefit amount you must count all Earned Income and Unearned Income not specifically excluded in this item.  
  2. Households that contain a  Senior or  DisabledDisabled member must only meet the net income eligibility standards for the 3SVT program. Households that do not contain a senior or disabled member must meet both the net income and gross income eligibility standards for the 3SVT program. See Income Limits and Maximum Benefit Table.

    A person who meets one or more of the following:

    • Receives disability or blindness benefits from any of these programs: Social Security,   SSI, or   MABD.
      • A person meets this even when the full benefit is withheld or deferred due to recoupment.
    • Receives a federally or state administered SSI supplement based on disability or blindness, or section 212(a) of PL 93-66.
    • Receives a disability retirement benefit from a government agency for a   disability considered permanent by SSAdisability considered permanent by SSA.

      Any of the following conditions:

      • Permanent loss of use of both hands, both feet, or one hand and one foot.
      • Amputation of leg at hip. Amputation of leg or foot because of diabetes or peripheral vascular diseases.
      • Total deafness, not correctable by surgery or hearing aid. Statutory blindness, unless caused by cataracts or detached retina.
      • IQ of 59 or less, established after the person becomes 16 years old.
      • Spinal cord or nerve root lesions resulting in paraplegia or quadriplegia.
      • Multiple sclerosis with damage to the nervous system caused by scattered areas of inflammation. The inflammation recurs and has progressed to varied interference with the function of the nervous system, including severe muscle weakness, paralysis, and vision and speech defects.
      • Muscular dystrophy with irreversible wasting of the muscles, impairing the ability to use the arms or legs.
      • Impaired renal function caused by chronic renal disease, resulting in severely reduced function which may require dialysis or kidney transplant.
      • Amputation of a limb of a person at least 55 years old.
    • Is a veteran the VA considers totally (100%) disabled or permanently housebound or in need of regular aid and attendance.
    • Is a veteran’s surviving spouse who the VA considers:
      • in need of regular aid and attendance, or
      • permanently housebound, or
      • approved for benefits because of the veteran’s death and has a disability considered permanent by SSA.
    • Is a veteran’s surviving child who the VA considers:
      • incapable of self-support, or
      • approved for benefits because of the veteran’s death and has a disability considered permanent by SSA
  3. The income tests are applied at application, recertification and whenever there is a change that causes redetermination of eligibility.
  4. The following section lists in alphabetical order the most frequent types of income and how to treat them.  

1600.2 Types of Income

1600.2.1 ABLE Accounts

  1. Exclude all income directly deposited into or transferred into the account. Verify contributions to the account prior to excluding the income. 
  2. Achieving a Better Life Experience savings accounts provide secure funding for disability-related expenses on behalf of designated beneficiaries deemed DisabledDisabled before age 26. Section 529A of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986.

1600.2.2 Adoption Subsidy

  1. Count as unearned income.
  2. Adoption subsidies are payments made to adoptive parents. These payments come from federal funds and are based on need. The Adoption Assistance Agreement shows the subsidy. There is a separate agreement for each child. The agreement may be amended from time-to-time.

1600.2.3 Agent Orange Settlement Fund

  1. Exclude payments received from the Agent Orange Settlement Fund or any other fund established for the settlement in the In Re Agent Orange product liability litigation, M.D.L. No. 381 (E.D.N.Y.) only if the payment is by the Aetna Insurance Company.
  2. See Veteran’s Benefits for payments for disabilities based on Agent Orange exposure.  

1600.2.4 Alimony Payments

  1. Count as unearned income any alimony payments (court-ordered or voluntary) received from someone outside the household, excluding any fees withheld. Voluntary support payments are payments that are not legally ordered.  They include payments made over and above the court-ordered amount. See Costs Withheld From Unearned Income. 
  2. Exclude voluntary support payments that are paid to a third party for a household expense. These are vendor payments and are not legally obligated to the household. 

1600.2.5 AmeriCorps Payments (State, National and  NCCC)

  1. Exclude payments and allowances from AmeriCorps State,  NCCC, without exception.
  2. Note: The Dream program is an AmeriCorps National program. 

1600.2.6 AmeriCorps VISTA Payments

  1. Exclude payments and allowances from AmeriCorps VISTA (formerly known as VISTA) if the person was receiving one of the following benefits when they joined AmeriCorps VISTA:
    1.  SNAP,  
    2.  TANF,  
    3.  SSI, or  
    4. State SSI Supplementary Income 
  2. Count as earned income if a person begins receiving SNAP benefits after joining AmeriCorps VISTA.
  3. AmeriCorps Vista join date is the start date of the actual contract and not the date of the offer letter.
  4. Note: When joined and application date are the same, consider the customer as receiving benefits. 

1600.2.7 Annuities

  1. Count payments from an annuity as unearned income. An annuity is purchased from an insurance company or by an employer as consideration for services provided. It may be paid yearly or at other specified intervals.
  2. Note: Annuities should be averaged over the period of time they are intended to cover.  Example: annuities that are paid annually should be averaged over 12-months. See Determining Income and Deductions.

1600.2.8 Blood Plasma Sale

  1. Count income from the sale of blood plasma as earned income. The plasma center is the employer.

1600.2.9 Boarder Income 

  1. Count income from boarders as earned income (RBIN).  

1600.2.10 Bonuses

  1. Count as earned income if received while the person is employed.
  2. Note: If paid less often than monthly see Income Received Less Often Than Monthly 
  3. Count as follows if received after employment has ended:
    1. Paid as a single payment, exclude as a lump sum (nonrecurring)
    2. Paid in more than one payment, count as unearned income

1600.2.11 Cafeteria or Flexible Benefit Plans

  1. Count cafeteria or flexible benefit plans as earned income when:
    1. An amount for the plan is withheld from the employee’s gross wages even if the employee loses any money left over at the end of the year.
    2. A refund is issued to the employee because all the money for benefits covered by an employer-funded plan were not used. 
  2. Exclude employer contributions into the plan at the time they are made.

  3. Note: These benefits are not displayed in the same way on all pay stubs. The best source of information regarding them is the employer. 

  4. Exclude all funds in a Medicare flexible plan or Medicare spendable plan.

  5. Cafeteria or flexible benefits plans use either the employee’s or employer’s money to pay certain expenses, such as child care, medical expenses, health insurance, annual leave or sick leave.

1600.2.12 Cash Donations

  1. Exclude cash donations based on need when the household gets them from one or more private, nonprofit, charitable organizations. Do not exclude more than $300 per federal fiscal quarter. Count any amount over the $300 limit as unearned income.
  2. Exception: Crowdfunding accounts are treated differently than cash donations. 

1600.2.13 Chaffee Funds

  1. Chaffee Funds are given to youth aging out of foster care by the Vermont Youth Development Program.
  2. Count as unearned income any recurring payments given to the household.
  3. Exclude non-recurring lump sum payments and vendor payments.

1600.2.14 Child’s Earnings

  1. Exclude the earned income of any member of the household age 17 or younger who both:
    1. Lives with a natural, adoptive, or stepparent or under the parental control of a household member other than a parent. See Minor Under Parental Control.
    2. Is a student at least half time attending elementary or high school, home schooling or a GED program recognized, operated or supervised by the state or local school district.
  2. Continue to exclude the earnings during a temporary break in school attendance (like a semester or vacation break), as long as the child will go back to school after break.
  3. Exclude earnings for the month the student turns 18, for both applicant and participant households.
  4. Do not exclude the income if the student is living alone or living with others but not under Parental Control.
  5. If it is not possible to separate out the child’s earnings or amount of work performed from that of other household members, prorate the total earnings equally among the working members, and exclude the child’s pro rata share.

1600.2.15 Child Support Income

  1. Count child support (court-ordered or voluntary), excluding any fees withheld, as unearned income to the child for whom the payment is intended.  Voluntary support payments are payments that are not legally ordered. They include payments made over and above the court-ordered amount.
  2. For initial applications, count the child support received in the prior 30 days in the month of application and the following month. After that, count the child support received two months prior to. 
  3. Count, as unearned income, child support that is released to the 3SVT household for a Reach Up participant if:
    1. the amount is paid because it was over the Reach Up assistance grant; or
    2. the amount is paid on a regular schedule for months that Reach Up was not received. See also Lump Sum (Nonrecurring).
  4. Count, as unearned income, child support that is released to the 3SVT household for a Reach Ahead participant. See Adding Child Support Income for 3SVT and Reach Ahead Cases.
  5. Exclude voluntary support payments that are paid to a third party for a household expense.  These are vendor payments and are not legally obligated to the household.  
  6. Exclude arrearage payments as a lump sum (nonrecurring) unless they are received on a regular basis.
  7. Exclude child support payments that are transferred to the OCS to maintain Reach Up eligibility. Child support income is excluded as of the date it is assigned. 

1600.2.16 Combat Pay

  1. Exclude additional payments received by members of the United States Armed Forces, including the Reserves and National Guard, due to being deployed to or serving in a designated combat zone. Exclude the payments for the duration of the person’s deployment, including hazard pay received after deployment to the combat zone.  (Public Law 108-477) See Combat Zones current designated combat zones.
  2. If the person was part of the 3SVT household before deployment, determine the person’s military take-home pay at that time. Count that amount as unearned income if it is less than what the person is making available to the household after deployment.
  3. If the person was not a member of the 3SVT household before deployment, determine the amount of military pay the person made available to the household at that time. Count that amount as unearned income if it is less than what the person is making available to the household after deployment.
  4. If the amount of military income before deployment is more than what is made available to the household after deployment, count as unearned income the amount made available after deployment.

1600.2.17 Compensated Work Therapy (CWT)

  1. Count  CWT payments as earned income for 3SVT.
  2. CWT is a therapeutic program of vocational and rehabilitative services under the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA).  Unemployed, or underemployed, veterans are placed in a real work environment and are paid for their work via payments from the VA or through the employer.

1600.2.18 Contract Income

  1. Count as earned income or unearned income depending on the source of the income. Examples: Teacher income is earned income, annuity income is unearned.
  2. Contract income is a fixed salary for a contracted service paid to the earner over the duration of the contract. They are not paid hourly. Examples: teachers or other school employees who receive their annual pay in nine months, web designer who signs a contract to provide services over a certain period with agreed upon compensation.
    1. Calculation: Salary from contract divided by number of months contract covers regardless of payment schedule equals gross monthly income.
      1. Request a copy of the contract to review its terms, compensation and length.
  3. Contract income paid hourly is normal Wages not contract income.
  4. Contract income is self-employment income if the person works under contract to customers of their business.
  5. Payments to persons who are called subcontractors for the convenience of the employer are not contract income, they are self-employed.

1600.2.19 Costs Withheld From Unearned Income

  1. Exclude any reasonable income-producing costs withheld from gross unearned income if the income is not legally obligated to the household. “Costs” are the amount actually spent to produce the income, rather than the amount owed. The net amount remaining after deducting these costs is counted as unearned income. This includes, but is not limited to:
    1. Agency fees withheld from child support or alimony payments
    2. Attorney fees withheld from workers’ compensation

1600.2.20 Crime Victim Payments

  1. Exclude payments received from a crime victim compensation program funded by the Crime Victims fund. (Public Law 103-322)

1600.2.21 Crowdfunding

  1. Exclude payments received from the account. Examples: Go Fund Me, Kickstarter, Indiegogo and Tilt

1600.2.22 Debts Owed to a Household

  1. Exclude the principal portion of payments made to the 3SVT household to repay a loan or promissory note.  
  2. Count any interest paid as unearned income. 

1600.2.23 Difficulty of Care Program (DCP) Payments    

  1. Count the DCP payments (both the stipend and the room and board) as unearned income when the DCP recipient is included in the 3SVT household. Count the income as the DCP provider’s income. Exception: Exclude room and board payment if made by the DCP recipient. 
  2. Exclude the DCP payments (both the stipend and the room and board) to the household member providing the care when the DCP recipient is not included in the 3SVT household. 
  3. Note: DCP recipients are considered a foster adult. 

1600.2.24 Disability Benefits

  1. Count an employee’s disability benefit as unearned income.
  2. Count an employee’s disability payments as earned income when they are paid out of the employer’s funds. 

1600.2.25 Disaster Assistance

  1. Exclude disaster and emergency assistance payments provided under the Robert T. Stafford Disaster Relief and Emergency Assistance Act, as amended (Public Law 100-707).
  2. This exclusion applies to federal assistance provided to people directly affected and to comparable disaster assistance provided by states, local government, and disaster assistance organizations. 
  3. Most  FEMA funds are excluded. However, payments made to homeless people to pay rent, mortgage, food and utilities are not excluded under this provision when there is no major disaster or emergency declared by the President.

1600.2.26 Dividend Income

  1. Count as unearned income dividends the household has the option of either receiving as income or reinvesting in a trust or other investment. Regardless of the payment schedule, annualize these dividends.

1600.2.27 Domestic Volunteers Services Act Payments, Title II

  1. Exclude all payments under Title II. 
  2. Title II payments include those made through the
    1. National Senior Service Corps, including the RSVP, 
    2. Foster Grandparents, 
    3. Senior Companion and others.

1600.2.28 Earned Income Tax Credits

  1. Exclude as income any Federal or Vermont EITC. 
  2. These payments supplement the earned income of families with children. 

1600.2.29 Employment and Training (E&T) Payments

  1. Exclude any payment made to an  E&T participant for costs that are reasonably necessary and directly related to participation in the Employment and Training Program. These costs may include, but are not limited to:
    1. Dependent care costs
    2. Transportation
    3. Other expenses related to work
    4. Training or education
    5. Uniforms
    6. Necessary equipment
    7. Books or training manuals

1600.2.30 Energy Assistance

  1. Exclude payments or allowances made under any federal law for providing energy assistance. Exclude payments provided through:
    1. Seasonal Fuel Assistance Program (including the $21 payment)
    2. The Department of HUD even if the payment is received directly by the participant.
    3. The FmHA

1600.2.31 Essential Person Program Payments

  1. Count payments as unearned income. 
  2. Note: If a non-household member is paid for the service, the amount paid is an allowable medical expense. See Medical Expenses.
  3. Essential person program payments are for a senior and/or disabled household member who needs in-home help.

1600.2.32 Experience Works

  1. See Senior Community Services Employment Program 

1600.2.33 Farmers Home Administration Payments

  1. Exclude all utility payments made by  FmHA.

1600.2.34 Fellowship

  1. Exclude fellowship payments if the following criteria are met:
    1. funded under Title IV-C or IV-E of the Higher Education Act; and
    2. funds are intended to cover room and board and/or educational expenses.
  2. Count fellowship payments if they are used to cover normal living expenses. 

1600.2.35 Flexible Family Funding

  1. Exclude Flexible Family Funding if paid as a lump sum or as a reimbursement.
  2. Note: Verification must be provided to confirm the funds were used as a reimbursement.
  3. Count as income if paid over time and not a reimbursement.  

1600.2.36 Focus Group, Survey or Study Income

  1. Count payments received for participation in a focus group, survey or study unless the payment is a reimbursement or a gift certificate. Whether it is earned income or unearned income depends on how the payment is described by the entity providing it.

1600.2.37 Foster Care Payments

  1. Count the foster care payment as unearned income when a foster child or foster adult is included in the 3SVT household. 
  2. Exclude the foster care payment when a foster child or foster adult is not included in the 3SVT household. This includes payments made to a respite provider.
  3. Note: DCP recipients are considered a foster adult. 

1600.2.38 Gambling Winnings

  1. Count gross winnings from gambling as unearned income. Do not offset the winnings with any amount lost.

1600.2.39 General and Emergency Assistance

  1. Count the General Assistance personal needs payment as unearned income.
  2. Exclude all other General Assistance payments as vendor payments.

1600.2.40 Gifts

  1. Count a cash gift (other than one specifically excluded under Cash Donations) as unearned income if it can be anticipated.

1600.2.41 Gift Cards

  1. Exclude establishment specific gift cards.
  2. Count a credit card company gift card as unearned income if it can be anticipated. 

1600.2.42 HUD Utility Payments

  1. Exclude all utility payments made by the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD).

1600.2.43 Income Excluded by Federal Statute

  1. Exclude all income specifically excluded by any other federal statute.  This includes, but is not limited to the following:
    1. Payments received under the Alaska Native Claims Settlement Act Amendments. (Section 29 of Public Law 92-203 and Section 15 of Public Law 100-241)
    2. Payments of relocation assistance to members of the Hopi and Navajo Tribes under Public Law 93-531.
    3. Payments made under the Confederate Tribes of the Colville Reservation Grand Coulee Dam Settlement Act. (Public Law 103-436)
    4. Payments by the Indian Claims Commission to the Confederated Tribes and Bands of the Yakima Indian Nation or the Apache Tribe of the Mescalero Reservation. (Public Law 95-433)
    5. Payments to the Passamaquoddy Tribe, Maliseet Tribe and the Penobscot Nation or any of their members received per the Maine Indian Claims Settlement Act of 1980. (Public Law 96-420)
    6. Income received from certain sub marginal land of the United States which is held in trust for certain Indian tribes. (Public Law 94-114)
    7. Income received from the disposition of funds to the Grand River Band of Ottawa Indians. (Public Law 94-540)
    8. Payments made for children who suffer from birth defects and whose mothers are Vietnam veterans.  (Public Law 106-419)
    9. Payments made to a child of a Vietnam Veteran for any disability of the child resulting from Spina Bifida. (Public Law 104-204, section 1805(d))
    10. Payments made to Americans of Japanese or Aleut ancestry under Public Law 100-383.
    11. Payments made under Public Laws 101 and 201 for Agent Orange Settlement.
    12. Payments made to victims of Nazi Persecution under Public Law 102-286.
    13. Payments made under the Radiation Exposure Compensation Act to compensate injury or death resulting from exposure to radiation from nuclear testing and uranium mining. (Public Law 101-426)

1600.2.44 Income Tax Refund

  1. Exclude as lump sum (nonrecurring) payments.

1600.2.45 Individual Development Account (IDA)

  1. Exclude interest earned on IDA accounts.  Also exclude any deposits made into the account by an outside source.
  2. A savings account for lower income persons. An IDA is used for a specific purpose such as education, purchasing a first home, or starting a business. Savings are matched by private or public funds.

1600.2.46 In-Kind Benefits

  1. Exclude any non-monetary or in-kind gain or benefit provided directly to the household, such as meals, free rent, clothing, housing, or produce from a garden. Example: working off rent is an in-kind benefit.

1600.2.47 Insurance Settlements

  1. Exclude from income all money received from lump sum (nonrecurring) insurance settlements. 
  2. Count recurring payments as unearned income.

1600.2.48 Interest Income

  1. Count as unearned income.

1600.2.49 Irregular Income

  1. Exclude up to $30 of income per federal fiscal quarter when the income is received too infrequently or irregularly to be reasonably anticipated.

1600.2.50 Job Corps

  1. Count salary and performance bonuses as earned income for Job Corps participants and former participants who are still receiving Job Corps payments. 
  2. Note: Most Job Corps participants are residents of an ineligible  Institution, and therefore, not eligible for 3SVT.  See Special Household Situations for eligible institutions.
  3. Exclude any lump sum one-time payment of salary from prior periods as a lump sum (nonrecurring) payment.  If a performance bonus is received after the person leaves the program and it is paid out in one payment, exclude it from income as a lump sum (nonrecurring).

1600.2.51 Job Related Reimbursement

  1. Exclude reimbursements from the employer for job-related expenses including travel expenses and uniform allowances.

1600.2.52 Job Training Partnership Act

  1. See Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA).

1600.2.53 Jury Duty Payments

  1. Count compensation for jury duty as earned income unless it is a reimbursement such as for transportation, or meals and lodging.  

1600.2.54 Legally Obligated Money

  1. When legally obligated money is earned, count it as earned income.
  2. When it is unearned, count it as unearned income.  
  3. Count legally-obligated money when it is garnished or diverted by the provider of the payment to a third party for a household expense. Do not exclude it as a vendor payment.
  4. Legally obligated money includes money such as wages earned by a household member, Social Security, SSI, or a Reach Up payment to which the household is legally entitled, and support or alimony payments that legally must be paid to a household member.
  5. Legally obligated money is different from a vendor payment because legally obligated money belongs to the household. If the money was not diverted, the household would get it. Vendor payments are made with money that does not belong to the household.

1600.2.55 Loans

  1. Exclude loans from private persons and commercial institutions. A legally executed document is not required to verify the income is a loan.
  2. Exception: Deferred payment loans for education. See Student Income.

1600.2.56 Low-Income Home Energy Assistance Program (LIHEAP)

  1. Exclude payments made under the Low-Income Home Energy Assistance Program. See Energy Assistance

1600.2.57 Lump Sum (Nonrecurring)

  1. Exclude nonrecurring lump-sum payments, including but not limited to, payments received as a one-time payment for:
    1. Income tax refunds, rebates, or credits, including Vermont  EITC Vermont Child Tax Credit and renter rebate. 
    2. Retroactive lump sums for the past one month or more, such as Social Security,  SSI,  Reach Up, Unemployment Benefits, or other retroactive payments.
    3. Exception: SSI retroactive benefits may be paid out in more than one payment. Each retroactive payment is a nonrecurring lump sum and is excluded as income.
    4. The annual  VA disability pension adjustment.
    5. Insurance Settlements.
    6.  Reach Up parenting incentive payments.
    7. Refunds of security deposits on rental property or utilities including:
      1. A  HUD rental refund.
    8. From the sale of a household’s countable or exempt resources (such as the household’s homestead, personal effects, household goods, or an automobile) that are not sold as part of a self-employment business.
    9. Retroactive pay raise of an employee whether employed or not at the time of receipt.
    10. A bonus received after employment has stopped.
    11. Back pay that was not withheld at the request of the employee.
    12. Payments through the Low-Income Home Energy Assistance Program. 

1600.2.58 Medicare Advantage (MA) Supplemental Benefits

  1. Exclude all Medicare Advantage (MA) Supplemental Benefits.
  2. MA Supplemental benefits are benefits for items and services related to the health of the enrollee.

1600.2.59 Military Pay

  1. Count the following military allowances as earned income:
    1. Base pay
    2.  BAH
    3.  BAQ
    4.  BAS
    5. Sea-duty pay
    6. Career sea pay
    7.  VHA
    8. Ration ( BAS)
    9. Hazard Pay received prior to deployment to a combat zone or received due to deployment to an area not designated as a combat zone.   
  2. Exception: See Combat Pay.  
  3. If two payments are received in one month because a holiday or weekend changes the mailing cycle, count the payments for the months for which they are intended.  
  4. Exclude  CMAs as a reimbursement.  
  5. Exclude amounts withheld from paychecks for education programs.

1600.2.60 Native American Funds Excluded by Federal Statute

  1. Many payments made to Native Americans under federal law are totally excluded income. Some payments are partially excluded. Click here for a list of public laws and the treatment of funds. 

1600.2.61 Native American Tribal Dividends

  1. Exclude $2000 per year from tribal dividend payments. 
  2. Exceptions:
    1. Exclude all tribal dividend money deposited into a child’s trust.
    2. Count casino profit payments as unearned income in the month received. Do not apply the $2000 exclusion.

1600.2.62 Other Unearned Source

  1. Count as unearned income any direct money payments without a work requirement that can be construed to be a gain or benefit.

1600.2.63 Pensions

  1. Count payments as unearned income.  

1600.2.64 Plan for Achieving Self-Support (PASS)

  1. Exclude the amount of money necessary to fulfill SSI’s  PASS.  This includes money deposited into a special PASS account.  

1600.2.65 Property Sold on Contract

  1. Count as unearned self-employment income.

1600.2.66 Property Settlement

  1. Count as unearned income property settlements paid as cash in more than one payment.  
  2. Exclude property settlements paid as cash in one payment as a lump sum (nonrecurring). 

1600.2.67 Reach Ahead

  1. Exclude. 
  2. A temporary (up to 24-month) assistance program that helps families transitioning to work. It provides support services for employment such as help paying car repairs, work clothes, work supplies, 100% child care subsidy and a food benefit.

1600.2.68 Reach First

  1. Count as unearned income even if the assistance is given in the form of a vendor payment. Also count the payment as unearned even if the program requires the applicant/recipient to work without pay in order to be eligible.
  2. A temporary (four month) financial assistance program for families who have a plan to become self-sufficient within four months.

1600.2.69 Reach Up

  1. Count as unearned income even if the assistance is given in the form of a vendor payment. Also count the payment as unearned even if the program requires the applicant/recipient to work without pay in order to be eligible.

16002.70 Reach Up Parenting Incentive Payments

  1. Exclude this payment for completing a parenting education program.

1600.2.71 Refugee Cash Assistance (RCA)

  1. Count as unearned income. 

1600.2.72 Recoupment

  1. Exclude money that is withheld from the household’s income to repay a prior overpayment from that source. Also exclude money the household received, but voluntarily or involuntarily returned to repay a prior overpayment from that income source. Exception: See Repayment due to Intentional Program Violation (IPV).

1600.2.73 Refunds from Rent or Utility Deposits

  1. Exclude.

1600.2.74 Reimbursements

  1. Exclude reimbursements for past or future expenses as long as the reimbursements meet all of the following criteria:
    1. They do not exceed actual expenses. If the reimbursement exceeds actual expenses, count the excess as unearned income. Do not consider a reimbursement to exceed actual expenses unless the provider or the household says it exceeds actual expenses.
    2. They do not result in a gain or benefit to the household.
    3. They are identified and used for expenses other than normal living expenses (such as rent, mortgage, personal clothing, or food eaten at home). Count payments as income if they are not identified for a specific purpose or are not used for that purpose.
  2. If a reimbursement covers multiple expenses, each expense does not have to be separately identified, as long as none of the money covers normal living expenses.
  3. The following are examples of excluded reimbursements:
    1. Reimbursements or flat allowances for job or training related expenses, such as per diem, uniforms, and transportation to or from the job or training site.  Exclude reimbursements for these expenses that are over and above basic wages.
    2. Reimbursements for the out-of-pocket expenses volunteers incur in the course of their work.
    3. Medical or child care reimbursements.
    4. Medicare Advantage (MA) Supplemental Benefits
    5. Reach Ahead work support payments 
      1. The payments are reimbursements for past or future work expenses incurred by participating families.

1600.2.75 Rental Property Income

  1. Count as earned self-employment income. Exception: If a third party is employed to manage the property, it is unearned self-employment income.  

1600.2.76 Repayment Due to Intentional Program Violation (IPV)

  1. Count money as unearned income when it is withheld from federal, state, or local means-tested programs to recoup an overpayment caused by the household’s intentional failure to comply with program requirements.
  2. If the amount withheld is not due to intentional program violation, exclude it as a regular recoupment. See Recoupment.
  3. Federal, state or local means-tested programs include Reach Up, General Assistance, State Supplementary Assistance, SSI, and veteran pensions.  
  4. Note: To determine if recoupment from SSI is an IPV, ask if it is due to being criminally charged with an IPV. If it is, then it is considered an IPV. Verify IPV status for SSI only when the household states it was due to IPV.  

1600.2.77 Representative Payment

  1. A payment received by one person, the representative payee, for the needs of another person, the beneficiary. When the representative payee and the beneficiary are in the same 3SVT household, count the payment as unearned income to the household. 
  2. When the representative payee and the beneficiary are not in the same 3SVT household, treat the representative payment as follows:
    1. Count as unearned income to the beneficiary any part of the payment that the payee gives to, or uses for the needs of the beneficiary. 
    2. Count as unearned income to the payee any amount that is not given to or used for the needs of the beneficiary.  
  3. When a representative payment is made to a payee that is intended for more than one beneficiary, and the beneficiaries are both 3SVT household members and non-members exclude only the identifiable portion of the payment intended and used for the care and maintenance of the non-member.
  4. If a non-member’s portion cannot be readily identified, divide the payment evenly among all beneficiaries. Exclude the non-member’s prorata share or the amount actually used for the non-member, whichever is less.

1600.2.78 Retirement Payments

  1. Count retirement payments as unearned income. 

1600.2.79 Reverse Mortgage

  1. Exclude if there is a verifiable agreement to repay the loan.
  2. Exclude income from a loan against the home with no repayment required until the borrower dies, or the home is sold.
  3. Count as unearned income if there is no repayment agreement. 

1600.2.80 Royalties

  1. Count royalty payments as unearned income.
  2. A payment to an owner for the use of property, especially patents, copyrighted works, franchises or natural resources.

1600.2.81 Self-Employment Income

  1. Count the gross income from self-employment, including the total gain from the sale of any capital goods or equipment related to the business, excluding the allowable costs of doing business. See Self-Employment.

1600.2.82 Senior Community Service Employment Program (SCSEP)

  1. Exclude wages received from the Title V Senior Community Service Employment Program of the Older American’s Act.  An employment-based job training program for older Americans that provides training for low-income, unemployed seniors.
  2. Vermont  SCSEP includes:
    1. Experience Works
    2. Vermont Associates for Training and Development, Inc.
    3. National Able Network
    4. Vermont Department of Aging and Independent Living
    5. Visiting Nurses Association

1600.2.83 SeniorCorps Payments

  1. Exclude payments and allowances from SeniorCorps, without exception. 
  2. Note: Green Mountain Grandparents is a SeniorCorps program.

1600.2.84 Severance Pay

  1. Exclude as a lump sum (nonrecurring), if paid in a single payment. 
  2. Count as unearned income if paid in more than one payment.
  3. The compensation that an employer provides to an employee who has been laid off, whose job has been eliminated, who through mutual agreement has decided to leave the company, or who has parted ways with the company for other reasons.

1600.2.85 Sick Pay

  1. Count as earned income when the person gets it while employed.  
  2. Count as unearned income when the person getting it is no longer an employee and is paid out in more than one check over a period of time.
  3. Exclude the sick pay as a lump sum (nonrecurring) when paid in one payment and the person getting it is no longer an employee. 
  4. When co-workers donate their sick leave time, count the payments the same as if they were the person’s own sick pay.

1600.2.86 Social Security Benefits

  1. Count as unearned income. Count the gross amount even if Social Security tax or a Medicare premium is withheld. 
  2. Exclude Social Security retroactive payments as lump sum (nonrecurring). 
  3. Exclude Social Security benefits withheld to repay a prior overpayment regardless if due to intentional program violation or not. See Recoupment.

1600.2.87 Spousal Allocation

  1. Count as unearned income for a person when:
    1. their spouse is a  LTC recipient, 
    2. the spouse is not a member of the 3SVT household, and 
    3. spousal allocation is required in the LTC budget. 
  2. Exclude spousal allocation when the spouse is in the 3SVT household.  

1600.2.88 Strike Benefits and Picket Pay

  1. Counts as unearned income.
  2. See Strikers.

1600.2.89 Student Income 

  1. A scholarship, grant, loan, veteran’s educational benefit and the like awarded to a household member enrolled at a:
    1. Recognized post-secondary education institution
    2. School for the handicapped
    3. Vocational education program
    4. Vocational or technical school
    5. Program that provides for obtaining a secondary school diploma or the equivalent
  2. Exclude all student financial assistance received under programs in Title IV of the Higher Education Act or under Bureau of Indian Affairs student assistance programs. These include but are not limited to:
    1. Basic Education Opportunity Grants (BEOG or PELL Grants)
    2. Presidential Access Scholarships (Super Pell Grants)
    3.  SEOG
    4.  SSIG
    5. Federal Family Education Loan Program (Formally GSL)
      1. Supplemental Loans for Students
      2. PLUS Loans for parents
      3. Robert T. Stafford Student Loans
    6. Direct Federal Loans to students in institutions of higher education 
    7. Perkins Loans (formerly NDSL)
    8. Federal Work Study Funds (Note: Not all Federal work study funds come under Title IV of the Higher Education Act)
    9. Trio Grants
      1. Upward Bound
      2. Student Support Services
      3. Robert E. McNair Post-Baccalaureate Achievement
    10. Robert C. Byrd Honors Scholarship Program
    11.  CAMP for students whose families are engaged in migrant and seasonal farm work
    12.  HEP
    13. National Early Intervention Scholarship and Partnership Program
  3. Exclude all loans and scholarships received under Titles III, VII and VIII of the Public Service Health Act. These include, but are not limited to:
    1. National Health Service Corps
    2. NURSE Corps Scholarship Program
    3. Scholarships for Disadvantaged Students
    4. Loans for Disadvantaged Students
    5. Health Professions Student Loans
    6. Nursing Student Loans 
    7. Primary Care Loans
    8. NIH Undergraduate Scholarship
    9. NIH Clinical Research Loan Repayment
    10. Indian Health Service Scholarships
    11. Indian Health Service Loan Repayment
    12. Native Hawaiian Scholarship Program
  4. Count as unearned income other scholarships, educational grants, deferred payment loans for education and veterans’ educational benefits that do not have a work requirement attached to them. Count as earned income if they have a work requirement attached to them. Exclude amounts used for:
    1. Tuition
    2. Mandatory fees, including rental or purchase of any equipment, material and supplies related to the course of study pursued
    3. Books
    4. Transportation
    5. Miscellaneous personal expenses, other than normal living expenses, incidental to attending school such as, professional journals, typing of papers, membership in professional associations, etc.
    6. Dependent care (Note: any amount excluded from income cannot be claimed as part of the dependent care expense. See Dependent Care.)
    7. Origination fees and insurance premiums on educational loans
  5. Student income provided for living expenses is counted as unearned income unless there is a work requirement attached to its receipt. Count as earned income if it has a work requirement attached. Treat student income provided for living expenses this way whether or not the student receives the payment or it is paid directly to the provider.
  6. Count the following grants as unearned income if used for living expenses.  Exclude any portion earmarked for allowable expenses (tuition, books, transportation, etc.):
    1. VSA Grant
    2. Summit Grant 
    3. VT State Grant  

1600.2.90 Supplemental Security Income (SSI)

  1. Count as unearned income.  
  2. SSI benefits are sometimes paid to a  Representative Payee for the SSI recipient. See Representative Payment.
  3. Exclude retroactive SSI payments as a lump sum (nonrecurring).  
  4. Also, exclude retroactive or underpayment SSI payments deposited directly into a dedicated account when an SSI recipient is under the age of 18 and must have a representative payee. If the person is eligible for retroactive SSI, the representative payee must set up a “dedicated” account in a financial institution, and must use this money for allowable expenses of the child. Do not count any disbursements from this account as income.
  5. Exclude SSI benefits withheld to repay a prior overpayment, unless it was due to intentional program violation (IPV). Verify IPV status only when the household states it was due to IPV. See Recoupment.

1600.2.91 Temporary Census Worker

  1. Exclude earnings received as a temporary census worker. Send to AOPs as this must be tracked. 

1600.2.92 Tips

  1. Count as earned income.  
  2. Note:  Basic Wage Rate applies when employers are unable to estimate tips.

1600.2.93 Training Allowances

  1. Count training allowances from vocational and rehabilitative programs recognized by federal, state or local governments as earned income, as long as they are not a reimbursement.

1600.2.94 Tribal TANF Payments

  1. Count Tribal  TANF payments as unearned income.  
  2. Exclude any retroactive payments as a lump sum (nonrecurring). 

1600.2.95 Trust Income

  1. Count as unearned income money withdrawn from an inaccessible trust that is excluded as a resource.  Send the trust to AOPs for review.
  2. Exception: Exclude when the withdrawal is a reimbursement.
  3. If the trust is accessible to the household and therefore counted as a resource, exclude as income any money withdrawn from the trust.
  4. If the household has the option of either receiving dividends as income or reinvesting them in the trust, count them as unearned income in the month they are credited to the trust.

1600.2.96 Unemployment Compensation Benefits (UCB)

  1. Count unemployment insurance benefits as unearned income.
  2. Exclude retroactive unemployment insurance payments as a lump-sum (nonrecurring). Exclude unemployment insurance benefits withheld to repay a prior overpayment. See Recoupment.
  3. UCB is paid by the Vermont Department of Labor to a person whose job has ended due to lay-off, firing without good reason, or quitting with good reason.

1600.2.97 Uniform Relocation Assistance and Real Property Acquisition Policy Act

  1. Exclude as reimbursements from the Uniform Relocation Assistance and Real Property Acquisition Policy Act of 1970 (Public Law 91-646, Section 216).

1600.2.98 Universal Basic Income/Guaranteed Basic Income

  1. Exclude Universal Basic Income/Guaranteed Basic Income payments that provide money regardless of employment status to support basic needs if:
    1. Excluded by Reach Up,
    2. Paid with solely private or a combination of private and public funds.
      1. Amount must be the same amount excluded for Reach Up.
  2. Example: Spectrum Direct Cash Transfer Pilot Program. For any other programs, send an inquiry to AOPs.

1600.2.99 Vacation Pay

  1. Count as earned income if it is received before employment ends.  
  2. Count as unearned income when received after employment ends, when paid in more than one payment.
  3. Exclude as a lump sum (nonrecurring) when it is received in one lump sum after employment ends.  

1600.2.100 Vendor Payments

  1. Exclude the following vendor payments:
    1. Payments made from their own funds by a person outside the household or by an organization to a third party for a household’s expense.
    2. Payments made by a person sharing housing with the 3SVT household to a third party for household’s shelter expense.
    3. Rent or mortgage payments made to the landlord or lender by the Department of  HUD or by state or local housing authorities.
    4. Payments by a government agency/program to a third party on behalf of the household to pay the household’s medical or child care expenses.
    5. Payments through the Low-Income Home Energy Assistance Program.
    6. Housing assistance when a household lives in temporary housing.
    7. Payments converted to a direct cash payment when approved by a federally authorized demonstration project.
  2. Exceptions: See Legally Obligated Money and General Assistance. 
  3. See Determining Monthly Deductions - Disallowed Expenses on how to handle deductions.
  4. Exclude General Assistance or Reach Up vendor payments for the housing and transportation of  Migrant Worker and  Seasonal Farmworker households while workers are in the job stream. See Migrants and Seasonal Farmworkers.

1600.2.101 Vermont State Housing Authority (VSHA) Section 8 Homeownership Program

  1. Exclude the utility allowance portion of VSHA Section 8 Homeownership program payments. See HUD Utility Payments. Count as unearned income any remaining portion paid to the customer.
  2. Exclude VSHA Section 8 Homeownership program payments that are paid directly to the lender. See Vendor Payments.

1600.2.102 Veteran’s Benefits

  1. Count as unearned income payments made by the Department of Veterans Affairs to veterans or dependents of veterans. This includes:
    1. The Monthly Housing Allowance provided to veteran students for housing costs under the Post-9/11 GI Bill,
    2. Chapter 31
    3. Disability payments based on exposure to Agent Orange,
    4. Pension,
    5. Disability compensation,
    6.  Aid and Attendance Pension, and 
    7.  Household Allowance.  
  2. See Agent Orange Settlement Fund if the customer is receiving payments for Agent Orange exposure but it is not paid by the VA. 

1600.2.103 VISTA Payments

  1. See AmeriCorps VISTA.

1600.2.104 Vocational Rehabilitation (HireAbility VT) Training Allowance

  1. Count as earned income training allowances from vocational and rehabilitative programs recognized by Federal, State or local governments, such as work incentive programs, to the extent they are not a reimbursement.   
  2. Example: Career Training Offset.

1600.2.105 Wages

  1. Count all wages and salaries as earned income.  

1600.2.106 Welfare-to-Work Payments

  1. Exclude payments for work-related expenses as reimbursements.  

1600.2.107 Workers’ Compensation 

  1. Count as unearned income, excluding any fees withheld. See Costs Withheld from Unearned Income. 
  2. Workers’ compensation is a benefit paid to an employee who is injured on a job covered by the program. Workers’ compensation insurance is mandatory for all Vermont employers.

1600.2.108 Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA)

  1. Count as earned income WIOA on-the-job training, including Job Corps and YouthBuild earnings. These earnings include monies paid by WIOA and monies paid by the employer. WIOA sets the definition of these programs.
  2. Exception: Exclude WIOA on-the-job training earnings of household members under age 19 who are under the  Parental Control of an adult household member. Apply this exclusion regardless of school attendance or enrollment.
  3. Exclude all payments for living allowances, education, or other purposes that participants get for participating in WIOA programs.
  4. Note: The Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act was formerly known as JTPA. 

1500.3 Verification

1600.3.1 Requirement to Verify All Questionable Information

  1. All questionable information must be verified. Do not verify income that is excluded, unless the exclusion is questionable.

1600.3.2 Application

  1. Before initial approval, verify all gross countable income.
  2. Exception: Allow 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households to self-declare income, unless questionable.
  3. Postpone verification for expedited service month(s), unless verification can be provided in time to meet the seven-day standard of promptness.

16003.3 Interim Report

  1. Verify changes in gross non-excluded income.

1600.3.4 Unscheduled Changes

  1. Benefit increase: Verify changes in gross countable income.
  2. Benefit decrease: Verify changes at recertification.
  3. Exception: Allow 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households to self-declare income, unless questionable.

1600.3.5 Recertification

  1. Verify gross countable income.
  2. Exception: Allow 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households to self-declare income, unless questionable.

1600.3.6 Verification Not Available

  1. Base the income on the household’s written statement if good faith efforts to obtain the verification failed because the source failed to cooperate with the household and the ES.

1400 Categorical Eligibility

1400.1 Types of Categorical Eligibility

  1. There are two different types of categorical eligibility:  SCE and  ECE. SCE and ECE households are not required to meet certain income and resource tests and have less verification requirements. Categorical eligibility applies to the household, not to the person. Households must follow and meet 3SVT rules unless specifically identified in this item. 
  2. Determine if a household is SCE first.

1400.2 Standard Categorical Eligibility (SCE)

1400.2.1 Procedures for Determining SCE

  1. Determine household composition before determining SCE.
  2. Households are SCE when all members are eligible for any of the following qualifying programs:
    1.  SSI/AABD
    2.  PSE 
    3.  Reach Ahead
    4.  Reach First
    5.  Reach Up
    6. Vermont  EITC in the past 12 months. Household must have included a child for the tax year in which Vermont EITC was provided. Household must have a child under 18 included in their current 3SVT household. 
  3. Households are considered eligible for the qualifying programs upon approval, including when benefits:
    1. have not been received yet,
    2. are suspended, or 
    3. are withheld to repay an overpayment.

1400.2.2 Ineligible for Standard Categorical Eligibility (SCE)

  1. Households are not SCE when:
  2. The household loses eligibility from the program making them SCE.
  3. A member is disqualified for IPV.
  4. The  Head of Household is disqualified for failing to comply with work requirements. 
  5. Determine eligibility under regular 3SVT requirements.

  6. Note: A household member who fails to comply with workfare is ineligible for SCE. Remaining household members are eligible for SCE.

1400.2.3 Ineligible Household Members

  1. Do not include the following people as a member in a household otherwise SCE eligible:
    1. Non-member students
    2. Ineligible non-citizens
    3. Institutionalized in a non-exempt facility
    4. Disqualified non-head of household for failure to comply with a work requirement

1400.3 Expanded Categorical Eligibility (ECE)

1400.3.1 Procedures for Determining ECE

  1. Households not SCE are ECE because all applicant and recipient households are authorized to receive TANF-funded referral services. Households are notified of the referral services on notices at application and recertification. The notices include the following information in Additional Resources:
    1. Go to http://dcf.vermont.gov/benefits to find out about benefits available through the Department of Children and Families.
    2. Go to http://vermont211.org or call 2-1-1 toll free from anywhere in Vermont to find out about hundreds of community and statewide resources.
  2. ECE households’ gross income must be below 185% of the FPL.
  3. Households are considered ECE at application. 

1400.3.2 Ineligible for Expanded Categorical Eligibility (ECE)

  1. Households are not ECE eligible when:
    1. Gross income is over 185% of the FPL.
      1. Households with no senior or disabled members are not eligible for 3SVT. 
      2. Determine eligibility for households with a Senior or disabled member under regular 3SVT requirements. These households do not have a gross income test. They have a resource test and a net income test only.
    2. A member is disqualified for Intentional program violation (IPV).
    3. The  Head of Household is disqualified for failing to comply with work requirements. 
  2. Determine eligibility under regular 3SVT requirements.

  3. Note: A household member who fails to comply with workfare is ineligible for ECE. Remaining household members are eligible for ECE.

1400.3.3 Ineligible Household Members

  1. Do not include the following people as a member in a household otherwise ECE eligible:
    1. Non-member students
    2. Ineligible non-citizens
    3. Institutionalized in a non-exempt facility
    4. Disqualified non-head of household for failure to comply with a work requirement

1400.4 Eligibility Factors

  1. SCE and ECE households are not required to meet the following tests.
    1. 130% gross income
    2. Net income
    3. Resource limit including transferred resources policy
  2. Follow the other 3SVT eligibility rules to determine the household’s eligibility. 

1400.5 Verification

  1. Verification is waived for the following eligibility factors for SCE households:
    1. SSN
    2. Residency
    3. Sponsored non-citizen information
  2. Verify the following factors:
    1. Each member of a potential SCE household receives one of the qualifying programs, 
    2. Household’s countable gross income does not exceed 185% for ECE households.
    3. Eligibility factors not waived for SCE or ECE, if required.

  3. Note: SCE and ECE households applying for or receiving other benefit programs in addition to 3SVT must follow those programs’ information and verification requirements.  

1400.6 Benefits

1400.6.1 Benefits Summary

  1. Benefits for both SCE and ECE are determined following 3SVT eligibility rules.

1400.6.2 Households of One and Two Members

  1. SCE and ECE households of one or two members receive no less than the minimum monthly benefit, except when prorating the initial month. Benefits are not issued when the prorated initial month’s issuance is less than $10. See Initial Month and Prorated Benefits.

1400.6.3 Eligible for Zero Benefits

  1. SCE and ECE households of three or more receive benefits based on the monthly benefit tables. Suspend benefits if the household is SCE or ECE and eligible for a zero benefit. Do not deny the household. Notify the household their benefits are suspended, but they must continue to follow these requirements:
    1. Report changes,
    2. Complete recertification requirements.
  2. As long as the household follows these requirements the household remains SCE or ECE until the household’s situation changes allowing the household to receive benefits, or case closure. See Suspended Benefits.

1400.7 Reporting Requirements

  1. SCE or ECE simplified reporting households are not required to report any income changes when:
    1. the household reported income over the 130% gross income limit, and
    2. remained eligible.  
  2. The household has fulfilled their income reporting requirement until completing the interim report or recertification, whichever comes first.  
  3. SCE or ECE households are not required to report changes in resources.  
  4. See Reduced Reporting Requirements for further reporting requirements.

1400.8 Claims

  1. SCE and ECE cannot be cancelled retroactively. 
  2. Households cannot be over the income or resource limits in any month they were SCE or ECE.
  3. Do not establish a 3SVT claim when an overpayment is:
    1. due to excess resources for any household determined SCE or ECE, or
    2. because a household should not have been categorically eligible due to a mistake in granting eligibility for the program that made it SCE or ECE.
  4. Claims can be made against SCE or ECE households if an overpayment occurred because:
    1. the benefit determination was not correct, or
    2. the household was not eligible for SCE or ECE as described in Ineligible Households above. 

1400.9 CATN

  1. CATN categorical eligibility. Note SCE or ECE eligible.

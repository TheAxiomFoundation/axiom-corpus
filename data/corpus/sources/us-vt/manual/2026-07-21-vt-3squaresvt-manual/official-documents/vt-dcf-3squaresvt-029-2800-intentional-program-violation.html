2800.1 Overview

2800.1.1 Definition of Intentional Program Violation

  1. An  IPV occurs when a household member intentionally:
    1. made a false or misleading statement;
    2. misrepresented, concealed, or withheld facts; or
    3. trafficked benefits.

2800.1.2 Trafficked Benefits

  1. Trafficking means:
    1. Exchanging 3SVT benefits or  EBT cards for non-food items or cash;
    2. Exchanging 3SVT benefits for firearms, ammunition, explosives, or  Controlled Substance; 
    3. Returning a container for a deposit if the product was purchased with 3SVT benefits when the contents were intentionally discarded; or
    4. Reselling a product purchased with 3SVT benefits for non-food items or cash.
  2. See Overpayment Amounts Related to Trafficking.

2800.2 Suspected IPV Determination

2800.2.1 Fraud Referral

  1. Review the file to determine if the client may have committed an IPV to obtain incorrect benefits. If so, complete the ESD 108SFR, Suspected Fraud Referral. The  FACU completes the ESD 108FS, 3SquaresVT Claim, after the investigation is completed.  

2800.2.2 Overpayment Referral 

  1. Complete an ESD 108SFR, Suspected Fraud Referral, within 60 days from the  Date of Discovery. Send the form to the supervisor as part of the completion process. The supervisor will:
    1. return the form to the ES asking for additional verification (if necessary); and/or 
    2. electronically sign and send the form to the Fraud and Claims Unit and return a signed copy to the ES; and
    3. notify the ES of the FACU decision regarding the referral.

2800.2.3 Fraud and Claims Unit Process

  1. The Fraud and Claims Unit reviews the case circumstances and supporting information and:
    1. makes a decision on referral acceptance or rejection; and
    2. sends a memo to the supervisor with their decision:
      1. No overpayment, or
      2. Claim established by FACU as an  IHE or agency error (ESD 108FS is completed by FACU), or
      3. Accepted for investigation
  2. Once the investigation is completed, the Fraud and Claims unit determines the appropriate course of action. The case may be referred to the  AAG's office for an Administrative Disqualification Hearing or to the State’s Attorney for criminal prosecution. The FACU makes the appropriate referrals and notifications. 
  3. Note: If the IPV is referred for court action, the Fraud and Claims Unit will issue a memo detailing the court findings and restitution requirements. 

2800.3 Administrative Disqualification Hearings

2800.3.1 Hearings Procedures

  1.  ADHs are held, if a hearing waiver is not signed, to determine if an accused person committed an  IPV. 
  2. The hearing is heard before the  HSB and conducted by a Hearing Officer, appointed by the HSB. The hearing officer completes a fair and impartial review of the suspected violation. After the hearing, a hearing decision is issued confirming or denying the IPV.

2800.3.2 Waived Hearings

  1. The Fraud and Claims Unit will provide the accused person with an ESD 108FSHW, Administrative Disqualification Hearing Waiver, notifying them of the option to waive an administrative disqualification hearing. If they do not sign the waiver, FACU refers the case to the AAG to schedule the hearing. A hearing notice is then sent with a date, time and another copy of the waiver. The signed waiver is accepted by the:
    1. Fraud and Claims Unit; or
    2.  AAG; or 
    3. Human Services Board officer during the hearing process.

2800.3.3 Hearing Notice 

  1. The hearing officer issues an ESD 108FSHN, Administrative Disqualification Hearing Notice, to the household and the Fraud and Claims Unit at least 30 days in advance of the hearing date. The notice informs the household when, where, and how the hearing will be conducted. 

2800.3.4 Postponement

  1. The household may request a postponement of the hearing at least ten days in advance of the date of the scheduled hearing.
    1. The hearing must be rescheduled within 30 days.
    2. Only one postponement is allowed. 

2800.3.5 Hearing Process

  1. Within 90 days of the date the accused person is notified in writing that a hearing has been scheduled, the hearing officer shall:
    1. conduct the hearing; 
    2. arrive at a decision; and 
    3. notify the accused person and the Fraud and Claims Unit of the decision. 
  2. The accused person is allowed to participate in the program during the hearing process, if all 3SVT eligibility rules are met.
  3. The hearing is conducted by the rules of procedure outlined in Administrative Disqualification Hearing Process.

2800.3.6 Hearing Decision

  1. The hearing officer issues a decision notice to the household and the Fraud and Claims Unit. The Fraud and Claims Unit completes the ESD 108FS, 3SquaresVT Claim, and issues written notification to the supervisor. 

2800.4 Intentional Program Violation Establishment

2800.4.1 Final Determination of Intentional Program Violation

  1. The intentional program violation is final when the accused person:
    1. is convicted by Federal, state, or local court;
    2. is convicted by the ADH decision; or
    3. signs an ESD 108FSHW, Administrative Disqualification Hearing Waiver, waiving their right to an ADH.
    4. Note: See Disqualified Members to determine how to treat the income and resources for the individual with the IPV.  

2800.4.2 Disqualification and IPV Claim

  1. The FACU takes all actions necessary to implement the disqualification, establish the overpayment amount and claim. The supervisor receives a Fraud Disposition Report that summarizes the actions taken.
  2. The FACU sends the ESD 108FSDN, Administrative Disqualification Notice, to the household after the accused person’s intentional program violation is finalized. The notice informs the household that:
    1. The disqualification will start the first of the month, 10 days after the date the notice is mailed.
    2. The penalty is imposed for the accused person, even if the accused person is not currently receiving 3SVT benefits. 
    3. The penalty continues uninterrupted until the time period expires. 
    4. A new penalty time period may not be imposed for any action that occurred prior to the written notification of the disqualification period.

2800.4.2.1 Penalty Time Periods

  1. The disqualification penalty for intentional program violations (other than those described below) are:
    1. One year for the first violation;
    2. Two years for the second violation; and 
    3. Permanently for the third violation.
    4. Note: 1 or more violations that occurred before June 1, 1983 are counted as one previous disqualification when determining the above penalties.
  2. If the IPV is a result of trafficking benefits of $500 or more, the disqualification penalty is:
    1. Permanently for the first violation. 
  3. If the IPV is a result of trafficking benefits for a  Controlled Substance, the disqualification penalty is:
    1. Two years for the first violation;
    2. Permanently for the second violation. 
  4. If the IPV is a result of trafficking benefits for firearms, ammunition, or explosives, the disqualification penalty is:
    1. Permanently for the first violation. 
  5. If the IPV is a result of a person fraudulently reporting their identity or residence to receive multiple benefits at the same time, the disqualification penalty is:
    1. Ten years for each violation.

2800.4.3 Loss of Categorical Eligibility

  1. A household loses categorical eligibility for 3SVT benefits when any household member is found guilty of an Intentional Program Violation (IPV). Even if the household previously qualified for benefits based on categorical eligibility criteria, they will no longer be automatically eligible. Instead, the household’s eligibility will be re-screened based on standard eligibility. Update the EITC on the STAT/STAT panel from a "Y" to a "N" to ensure categorical eligibility is lost.
  2. The loss of categorical eligibility applies to the entire household, not just the individual who committed the IPV.
  3. The disqualified member’s income and resources will be treated according to Excluded Members.

2800.4.3.1 Transitioning a Household to Standard Eligibility Due to IPV

  1. When a household loses categorical eligibility because a household member has received an IPV the following steps will be taken:
    1. Actions by the Fraud and Claims Unit (FACU):
      1. Apply the IPV to the household member's MBR2 panel in ACCESS.
      2. Change the EITC flag on STAT/C/STAT to "N".
      3. Enter a WARN stating the household is not eligible to receive the "Y" in the EITC field until the IPV has expired. Include the expiration date in the warn.
    2. Responsibilities of the district:
      1. When a household loses categorical eligibility, ACCESS will automatically re-screen the household using standard eligibility requirements. See section below.

2800.4.3.2 Standard Eligibility Requirements

  1. The gross income limit is 130% FPL.
  2. The net income limit is 100% FPL.
  3. Households are subject to the Resource Limit.
  4. If the household fails the re-screening for standard eligibility, the case will appear on the district daily report, requiring manual processing for closure by the ES.

2800.5 Repayment Process

  1. The Fraud and Claims Unit initiates collection action against the household and monitors collections. See Repayment for additional information on the collection process.

2300 Change Processing

New information in the manual is highlighted and noted with a . See Manual Updates for details.

Section with expandable text are noted with a  throughout the manual. 

2300.1 Action on Known Changes

  1. ES must act on all known changes. Changes in a household’s situation become known to the agency when:
    1. reported by the household, or 
    2. information is received from other sources within the agency or outside the agency.  

2300.2 Action on Unscheduled Changes

2300.2.1 Process to Take Action on Unscheduled Changes

  1. Unscheduled changes are those that are reported outside of the Interim Report or Recertification.
  2. Allow the household ten days from the date the change is reported to provide required verifications.
  3. Act on changes within ten days of the date the change was reported.  
  4. The date a change is reported is the date:
    1. the household reports the change by telephone,
    2. the household reports the change in-person,
    3. information is received from another source regarding a household change, or
    4. a written notice is received in the office, including the ESD 200, Change Report.
  5. ACCESS generates the ESD 220, Notice of Decision, to inform the household of the effect of the change on their benefits. 
  6. CATN the date the change is reported, and the actions taken.  

2300.2.2 Benefit Increase

2300.2.2.1 Procedure to Increase Benefit Amount

  1. Increase the benefit amount no later than the first issuance that is ten days after the change was reported or verified, whichever occurs last.
  2. Example: The household’s normal issuance occurs on the 1st of every month. The household reports and verifies on August 10th their income decreased by $40. With this timeframe the household’s benefit increase must be effective for September. If the household reported and verified the change on August 28th the change must be effective by October. 
  3. See Underpayments when the benefits were not increased timely.

2300.2.2.2 Adding Household Members and Income Decreases 

  1. Benefit increases due to:
    1. adding a new household member, who is not a member of another household, or
    2. an income decrease is effective:
      1. the first issuance that is ten days after the change was reported or verified, but 
      2. no later than the month following the month the change is reported.  
  2. Process an underpayment if benefits cannot be increased by the following month. The underpayment issuance must be made available to the household by:
    1. the tenth day of the following month, or 
    2. the household’s normal issuance date, whichever is later.  

2300.2.2.3 Household Fails to Provide Required Verification

  1. Determine the household’s benefits without allowing the change when the household fails to provide required verifications. Obtain required verifications at recertification.

2300.2.2.4 Household Verifies After the 10-day Period

  1. Households failing to provide verification within ten days, who provide the verification later, must have their benefits increased by:
    1. the first issuance that is ten days after the change was verified, but 
    2. no later than the month following the month the change is verified.

2300.2.3 Benefit Decrease/Case Closure

  1. Take action to decrease the benefit within ten days after the change is reported. Use the information provided by the household. Verification is not required for decreases until interim report or recertification, whichever comes first. 
  2. Exception: When a household is active RUFA and 3SVT send a VCR for RUFA only, requesting the income verification. If the verification is received process the case according to the verification received. If verification is not received, update the 3SVT case with the self-reported information and process the case accordingly.
  3. Households must receive advance notice, when benefits are decreased or the case is closed. Benefit decreases or case closures are effective no later than the month following the month in which the adverse action period ends. 
  4. Exception: Adequate notice is allowed in some situations. 

2300.2.4 Unclear Change Information 3SquaresVT 

  1. Unclear change information is information that is received from the household or another source that requires  Clarification with a member of the household or verification before acting on the change. The following scenarios are the only unclear changes that should be clarified and/or verified. Information received that:
    1. is the result of a PVS match or a DOC Match;
    2. is the result of a deceased person match;
    3. is the result of a NDNH match;
      1. Note: NDNH match is run only at application and recertification.
    4. is the result from a DET hit and within 60 days after the receipt date of an application or recertification;
      1. Note: It takes 60 days from the start of employment for the DET hit to be received by ESD.
    5. is less than 60 days old relative to the current month of participation and was required to have been reported based on simplified reporting; or
    6. presents significantly conflicting information from what was provided by the household at application or recertification; or
      1.  Note: The reported information must be about circumstances that existed at the time of the last application or recertification. See Overpayments and Claims.
    7. is a change of address reported by the household. See Change of Address.
  2. Clarify and/or verify the unclear information by attempting to call the household or a collateral contact (such as an employer). If unsuccessful:
    1. Send an ESD 202VCR to the household requesting information.
      1. Include in the ESD 202VCR what will happen if the household fails to respond.
    2. Allow the household ten days to:
      1. verify, or 
      2. complete actions to clarify the change information.
    3. Households failing to clarify and/or verify the unclear information will receive an ESD 220 notice of adverse action NOAA.
  3. Do not clarify or verify any unclear change scenario other than those listed at 2200.2.4(a). Instead:
    1. provide a clear explanation in CATN of what you did and why you did it;
    2. create a future TODO for the end of the interim report or recertification month, whichever comes first, alerting of the unclear information that needs to be verified; and
    3. obtain required clarification and/or verification at the next interim report or recertification, whichever comes first.
  4. Exception: Households reporting a change of address must also report the shelter expenses for the new address. See Change of Address.

2300.2.5 Unclear Change Information 3SquaresVT in a SNAP

  1. Unclear change information is information that is received from the household or another source that requires  Clarification with a member of the household or verification before acting on the change. The following scenarios are the only unclear changes that should be clarified and/or verified. Information received that:
    1. is the result of a PVS match, or a DOC Match;
    2. is the result a deceased person match;
    3. is the result of a NDNH match;
    4. is the result of a DET hit;
    5. is less than 60 days old relative to the current month of participation and was required to have been reported based on 3SquaresVT in a SNAP Reporting Requirements; 
    6. presents significantly conflicting information from what was provided by the household at application or recertification; or
      1. Note: The reported information must be about circumstances that existed at the time of the last application or recertification. See Overpayments and Claims.
    7. is a change of address reported by the household. See Change of Address.
  2. Clarify and/or verify the unclear information by attempting to call the household or a collateral contact (such as an employer). If unsuccessful:
    1. Send an ESD 202VCR to the household requesting information.
      1. Include in the ESD 202VCR what will happen if the household fails to respond.
    2. Allow the household ten days to:
      1. verify, or 
      2. complete actions to clarify the change information.
    3. Households failing to clarify and/or verify the unclear information will receive an ESD 220 notice of adverse action, NOAA.
  3. Do not clarify or verify any unclear change scenario other than those listed at 2200.2.5(a). Instead:
    1. provide a clear explanation in CATN of what you did and why you did it;
    2. create a future TODO for the end of the interim report or recertification month, whichever comes first, alerting of the unclear information that needs to be verified; and
    3. obtain required clarification and/or verification at the next interim report or recertification, whichever comes first.
  4. Exception: Households reporting a change of address must also report the shelter expenses for the new address. See Change of Address.

2300.2.6 Change of Address

2300.2.6.1 Change of Address Procedures

  1. Households reporting a change of address must also report the shelter expenses for the new address. Verification of shelter expenses is not required unless questionable.   
  2. Act within ten days to change the address and determine the new benefit amount based on the shelter expenses for the new address, when all information is provided.  

2300.2.6.2 Shelter Expense Information Needed 

  1. If shelter expense information is not provided with the address change:
    1. Call the household to get the needed information about the new shelter expenses. 
    2. If the household cannot be reached or the information provided is questionable, send the household form ESD 202VCR to request the shelter expense information. Allow the household ten days to respond.
  2. Budget the shelter expenses for the new address within ten days from the date the information is provided.

2300.2.6.3 Household Fails to Provide Shelter Expense Information

  1. Act within ten days to determine the household’s benefits without allowing the shelter expenses when the household failed to provide the requested information by the ESD 202VCR due date.  

2300.2.7 Data Matches

  1. Information from data matches must be acted on within ten days of receipt. Follow policy in benefit increase, benefit decrease/case closure, or unclear change information when acting on changes reported through data matches.
  2. The following data matches are verified upon receipt:
    1. BENDEX
    2. SDX 
    3. SAVE 
    4. UCB

2300.2.8 Medical Expenses

  1. Medical expense changes reported outside of application, interim reporting, or recertification, are acted on only when reported by the household or by a source that is verified upon receipt.
  2. Changes in medical expenses are not acted on when:
    1. the change is reported by a source other than the household, and
    2. the change action cannot be completed without contacting the household for verification.

2300.2.9 Returned Mail

2300.2.9.1 Forwarding Address Provided

  1. This is an unclear change. Do not send an ESD 202VCR. Update the mailing address on the ADDR panel. Create a future TODO to verify the change at the next interim report or recertification, whichever comes first.

2300.2.9.2 Forwarding Address Not Provided

  1. This is an unclear change. Do not send an ESD 202VCR. Create a future TODO to verify the change at the next interim report or recertification, whichever comes first. 

2300.2.10 Reporting Through Other Programs

  1. Changes reported for other programs are reported for 3SVT, including when reported using the ESD 200. Other programs include, but are not limited to:
    1. Reach Up
    2. Reach First 
    3. Reach Ahead
    4. Green Mountain Care (Medicaid)
    5. Dr. Dynasaur, only if it is determined in ACCESS
  2. Act on any changes reported through other programs.  
  3. Note: 3SVT benefits cannot be ended solely due to the other program’s benefits ending. 3SVT continued eligibility must be determined based 3SVT eligibility requirements.
  4. Notify the household and  VDOL of any changes in work registration or time limited status resulting from the other program’s reported change.
  5. Certification periods terminated based on a reported change end the month following the determination. Send an ESD 220 advising the household their case is closing and that they may reapply.
  6. Send an ESD 220 to the household stating what actions were taken on their 3SVT case because of changes reported by other programs.

2300.2.11.2 Household Notice of Mass Change

  1. Households are sent a notice when a mass change affects their eligibility or benefit amount. Additionally, the notice advises the household of their fair hearing rights and when they may receive continued benefits. Notices are mailed the month before the change is effective.

2300.2.11.3 ES Notifications of Mass Changes

  1. ESs are sent reports prior to running a desk review and after the update occurs. Mass change ES reports include:
    1. What changes are occurring,
    2. When the desk review will be run,
    3. What ACCESS will complete,
    4. What ES must complete, 
    5. Instructions for ES required actions, and
    6. Due dates for ES required actions.

2300.2.11.4 Abbreviated Transactions

  1. Mass change updates fully completed by ACCESS are called abbreviated transactions. ACCESS sends an ESD 220 to all households whose eligibility or benefits were updated in an abbreviated transaction. ESD 220 is sent to households the month before the change occurs, but no later than the month when benefits are first affected by the mass change. 

2300.2.11.5 Full Transactions  

  1. Cases that cannot be updated through an abbreviated transaction have:
    1. Edits that stop eligibility,
    2. Unapproved results, such as:
      1. pending cases,
      2. ongoing cases with unapproved results, or
      3. FIAT eligibility results.
  2. Districts are sent reports identifying cases not completed through abbreviated transactions that require full transactions. Full transactions require ES to:
    1. Update the benefits, or
    2. Close the case, and
    3. Notify the household of the results using the ESD 220.

2300.2.11.6 Fair Hearings

  1. Households have the right to request a fair hearing when they disagree with a change made by a mass change.

2300.2.11.7 Continued Benefits

  1. Households requesting a fair hearing are eligible to receive continued benefits, at the amount received before the mass update, if the household:
    1. did not waive its right to continued benefits, and
    2. makes a timely hearing request, and
    3. claims benefits were:
      1. computed incorrectly, or
      2. the rules were misapplied/misinterpreted.

2300.3 Benefit Suspension

  1. Benefits may be suspended for one month if the household is temporarily ineligible because of a change not expected to continue in future months. Close the case if the household will not be eligible after suspending benefits for one month.

2300.4 Failure to Report 

  1. File a claim against the household following Overpayment and Claims and/or Intentional Program Violation (IPV) policies when the household:
    1. fails to report a required change, and 
    2. received benefits they were not eligible to receive.

2300.5 Notification

2300.5.1 Notice of Adverse Action (NOAA)

  1. Notice of adverse action advises the household that their benefits will decrease or close.  NOAAs may be issued allowing:
    1. adequate notice, or 
    2. advance notice.

2300.5.1.1 Adequate Notice

  1. Adequate notice means the household receives an ESD 220 advising them of a decrease in benefits or case closure no later than the date the household would normally receive their next benefit.  
  2. Provide adequate notice for decreases or case closures when the change was reported using the ESD 202IR, Interim Report.
  3. Households receiving adequate notice retain the right to:
    1. a fair hearing, and
    2. continued benefits when a fair hearing is timely requested. 
    3. Note: Benefits must be returned to the previous level within five days of the household’s request for a fair hearing.

2300.5.1.2 Advance Notice

  1. Advance notice means the household receives an ESD 220 at least ten days plus two days for mailing (12 days) before the decrease in benefits or case closure. This is the adverse action period.    
  2. Use advance notice for all decreases except when the decrease is the result of information reported on the ESD 202IR.

2300.6 Verification

2300.6.1 Refer to the Reported Change

  1. See item relating to the reported change.

2300.6.2 Verified Upon Receipt

  1. Some information is considered verified upon receipt - meaning that once it is given to ESD, it is considered verified, and no further verification is required. Verified upon receipt is information received directly from the source - be it the customer's statement or data matches in ACCESS. Act on verified upon receipt information unless it is questionable or inconsistent with known facts. See What is Questionable Desk Aid.
  2. Items verified by customer's verbal or written statement include but are not limited to:
    1. Age;
    2. Death of household member;
    3. Dependent care expenses;
    4. End of self-employment;
    5. Household members;
    6. Move to an ineligible institution;
    7. Relationship;
    8. Residency;
    9. Resources;
    10. School enrollment;
    11. Shelter expenses;
    12. Social Security Number (SSN); and
    13. Utility expenses.
  3. Data matches that are verified upon receipt only include:
    1. Beneficiary Data Exchange (BENDEX);
    2. Changes in the amount paid in another program within ACCESS:
      1.  RU,
      2.  RF,
      3.  PSE,
      4.  GA, and
      5.  EP.;
    3. Numident match;
    4. Office of Child Support (OCS) interface;
    5. State Data Exchange (SDX);
    6. Systematic Alien Verification for Entitlements (SAVE);
    7. Unemployment Compensation Benefits (UCB) tape match.
  4. Documents that are verified upon receipt include but are not limited to:
    1. VA award notice
    2. Rental agreement
    3. Railroad retirement board notice
    4. Property tax statement
    5. Federal income tax return

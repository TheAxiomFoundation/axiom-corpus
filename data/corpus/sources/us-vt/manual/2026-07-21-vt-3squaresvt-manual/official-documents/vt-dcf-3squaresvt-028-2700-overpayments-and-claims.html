2700.1 Processing Overpayments and Claims

  1. Complete ESD 108FS, 3SquaresVT Claim, when a household:
    1. received more benefits than they were eligible to receive; or
    2. has been found to have trafficked benefits.
  2. Note:  FACU completes the ESD 108FS. See Overpayments and Underpayments SOP and Overpayment Guide for referral process. 
  3. In addition to overpayments discovered through case management, other potential sources include, but are not limited to:
    1. Quality Control review findings
    2. Information reported by the household or other sources
    3. Supervisory case reviews
    4. Automated resources

2700.2 Types of Overpayments

2700.2.1 List of Overpayment Types

  1. There are three types of overpayments:
    1.  IPV
    2.  IHE
    3. Agency Errors

2700.2.2 Intentional Program Violation (IPV)

  1. An overpayment was intentionally caused by a household member or authorized representative. See Intentional Program Violation.

2700.2.3 Inadvertent Household Error (IHE)    

  1. An overpayment resulted from a misunderstanding or unintended error on the part of the household. Examples include but are not limited to:
    1. Incorrect or incomplete information was provided. 
    2. Required changes were not provided to the agency; or if reported, they were not reported timely.  
    3. Fair hearing decision upheld the agency’s actions and continued benefits were issued to the household.

2700.2.4 Agency Error 

  1. An overpayment caused by the agency’s action or failure to take action. Examples include but are not limited to:
    1. Failure to take prompt action on a reported change.
    2. Incorrect computation of 3SVT benefits amount.
    3. Benefits were issued after the certification period expired.
    4. Reach Up grant changes were not budgeted.
  2. Note: When an Inadvertent Household Error and Agency Error occur at the same time, consider the error as an Agency Error if it is not possible to determine the overpayment amount for each error. If it is possible, establish two overpayments. 

2700.3 Determining the Overpayment

2700.3.1 Determining the Applicable Dates of Overpayment

  1. Determine the correct amount of 3SVT benefits from the  Date of Discovery back to the month the overpayment occurred, but not beyond 12 months for inadvertent household and agency overpayments.
  2. Intentional Program Violation overpayments are calculated by the  FACU from the date of discovery back to the month the overpayment occurred, but not beyond 6 years. 

2700.3.2 Calculating Overpayment Amounts Except for Trafficking

  1. Correct only those items that were incorrect when calculating the overpayment amount. For example, if the overpayment was caused by incorrect unearned income, use the corrected unearned income amount but don’t change any other items from the original budget.
    1. Obtain verification.
    2. Determine the correct amount of benefits for each month that an overpayment occurred.
      1. Use the ESD 203C, Food Stamp Eligibility Worksheet, or the Quick Eligibility Check function. Attach copies of the worksheet to the ESD 108FS filed in the case record.
      2. Send a copy of the worksheets along with the ESD 108FS to the FACU and to OnBase.
      3. Remember to use the rules and benefit tables that were in effect at the time of the overpayment. 
    3. Use the actual income for the month received, when correcting income. Do not average or convert income being corrected.
      1. Note: Do not correct income if the payer determines that the income paid out was incorrect, budget the income amount actually received. For example, if the SSA determines that SSI was underpaid, do not correct the past 3SVT budgets. Change the budget when the household begins to receive the correct SSI amount.
    4. When correcting earned income, allow the 20% earned income deduction only for Agency Error claims. Do not allow the 20% earned income deduction for earned income that was reported incorrectly or not reported which results in an IHE or IPV claim.
    5. Subtract the correct amount of benefits from the benefits actually received.
    6. Reduce the overpayment amount by any  Expunged Benefits. 

2700.3.3 Overpayment Amounts Related to Trafficking

  1. The amount of the overpayment is the value of the trafficked benefits as determined by:
    1. the person’s admission;
    2.  Adjudication; or
    3. the information that forms the basis for the trafficking determination.
  2. The  FACU establishes the overpayment amount and claim.

2700.3.4 When Not to Establish Overpayments

  1. Do not establish an overpayment for the following reasons:
    1. The application form is not signed.
    2. The change was not required to be reported.
      1. Exception: Complete an overpayment as an Agency Error if a change was reported by the household and the agency should have acted on the change but did not.
    3. The amount of the overpayment is less than $125 for Inadvertent Household Error (IHE) or less than $600 for Agency Errors.
      1. Exception: Overpayments must be completed whenever it is determined there is an overpayment as a result of a Quality Control review.

2700.3.5 Overpayment Establishment

  1. Complete an ESD 108FS, 3SquaresVT Claim, within 180 days from  Date of Discovery. The form must be signed by the supervisor as part of the completion process. 
  2. Complete an entry in RECO. The  FACU generates the form from RECO and sends with notice to the household.  

2700.4 Repayment Process

2700.4.1 Members Responsible for Repayment of Overpayment

  1. The following members are responsible for repaying the overpayment:
    1. Each adult receiving 3SVT benefits when the overpayment occurred; or
    2. Persons connected to the household, such as an authorized representative, who actually trafficked or otherwise caused an overpayment or trafficking.
  2. The  FACU initiates collection action against the household. 
  3. When an adult member of an overpayment household moves to a different household, ACCESS issues an informational message to the ES. Notify FACU if this message is received. FACU will transfer the overpayment in RECO to the new case (DISP panel) if the overpayment will be pursued in the new household.  

2700.4.2 Notice Procedures

  1. The ESD 108FSA, 3SquaresVT Claim Notice, is automatically sent the day after RECO is updated. Refer all repayment questions to the  FACU. 
  2. Exception: The ES must resolve all questions regarding why the overpayment occurred or how the amount was calculated. 

2700.4.3 Suspended Collection

  1. The FACU may suspend collection action when there is no usable address to contact the client. The overpayment is written off after twelve months if a reapplication has not been submitted or the agency hasn’t located a usable address.

2700.4.4 Compromised Overpayments

  1. The overpayment amount for Agency Error and Inadvertent Household Error may be reduced if the household’s economic circumstances dictate the overpayment will not be paid in three years. The FACU is responsible for compromising overpayment amounts.

2700.4.5 Terminated Overpayments

  1. The fraud supervisor may terminate and write off the overpayment if:
    1. All household members are deceased;
    2. A fair hearing finds no overpayment;
    3. The overpayment has a balance less than $25 and no payment has been received in the last 90 days; or
    4. There has not been a payment on the overpayment for 3 years or more.

2700.4.6 Types of Repayment

2700.4.6.1 Recoupment 

  1. If the participating household does not make full repayment, the household’s 3SVT benefits will automatically be reduced by:
    1. IHE and Agency Error overpayments: The greater of $10 per month or 10% of the household’s monthly benefit, unless the household requests a larger amount. 
    2. IPV overpayments: The greater of 20% of the household’s  Monthly Entitlement for households with a disqualified member or $20. The recoupment changes to 20% of the benefit when the disqualification period is over. The household may request a larger amount.
    3. Exception: Benefits are not recouped from the initial month benefits. ACCESS is programmed to start recoupment the month following the month of application approval.

2700.4.6.2 Benefits Expungement

  1. The overpayment amount is reduced by any  Expunged Benefits.

2700.4.6.3 Household Repayment

  1. The household may pay the overpayment partially or in full with cash, check, money order, or with unused EBT benefits.  FACU is responsible for obtaining a written agreement from the household prior to using active EBT benefits to repay an overpayment.  If paid by cash or its equivalent, issue the household an ESD 140 receipt, and
    1. Give the payment to the assigned staff member.  
    2. Send to Accounts Receivable, Administrative Services.

2700.4.6.4 Underpaid Benefits 

  1. Underpaid benefits must first offset the overpayment amount. Amounts remaining after offsetting the claim are issued to the household.

2700.4.6.5 Treasury Offset Program (TOP)

  1. A delinquent overpayment may be transferred to TOP by the  FACU. The referral allows reduction of any federal payment due to the client from the US Treasury. Example: Income tax refunds, federal salary, etc.

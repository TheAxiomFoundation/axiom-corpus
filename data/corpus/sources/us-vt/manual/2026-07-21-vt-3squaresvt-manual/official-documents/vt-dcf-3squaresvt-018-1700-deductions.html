1700.1 Eligible Household Deductions

1700.1.1 Types of Household Deductions

  1. Households may be eligible for 6 deductions from gross income. These deductions are:
    1. Legally Obligated Child Support
    2. Dependent Care 
    3. Earned Income Deduction
    4. Medical Expenses
    5. Shelter Expenses
    6. Standard Deduction
  2. The household does not have to actually make a payment to get a deduction, but the household must be responsible for the expense. Exception: Child support payments must be paid to get a deduction for that expense.
  3. Expenses covered by excluded reimbursements or excluded vendor payments are not allowed.  
  4. This section defines allowable expenses. See Determining Income and Deductions for instructions on budgeting the expense.

1700.2 Legally Obligated Child Support

1700.2.1 Child Support Deduction

  1. Allow a child support deduction when the support is Legally Obligated and is:
    1. Paid by a household member for a child who is not part of the 3SVT household; or
    2. Paid by a household member to an agency or person outside of the household if the payments are not returned to the household.

1700.2.2 Child Support Amount

  1. Allow the amount actually paid by the household member, including any past-due (arrearage) amounts.  
  2. Child support may include but is not limited to:
    1. Monthly child support
    2. Legal fees
    3. Any past-due (arrearage) amounts
    4. Health Insurance payments

1700.2.3 Expense Not Allowed

  1. Do not allow payments if the following occurs:
    1. The payment is made through an IRS offset payment.
    2. The payment is defined as alimony, spousal support, or property settlement.
    3. The payment is not court-ordered (voluntary).
  2. Do not allow the portion of a processing fee that is not passed along to the parent/guardian. 

1700.2.4 Verification

  1. Verify the legal obligation, the legally obligated amount, the actual amount paid, and that it is paid to a non-household member before a deduction is allowed. 

1700.2.4.1 Application

  1. If verification is not submitted prior to the end of the 30-day processing period, process the application without the deduction. 
  2. Postpone verification for expedited service month(s), unless verification can be provided in time to meet the seven-day standard of promptness.

1700.2.4.2 Interim Report

  1. If a new payment or change is reported and verification is not submitted by the requested due date, process the interim report without the deduction. 

1700.2.4.3 Unscheduled Changes 

  1. If a new payment is reported and verification is not submitted by the requested due date, process without the deduction. 
  2. If a change in payment is reported and benefits will:
    1. Increase, verify the new amount, if verification is not provided do not make a change; use the previously verified amount. 
    2. Decrease, process the change and verify at recertification. 

1700.2.4.4 Recertification

  1. If a new obligation is reported and verification is not submitted prior to the end of processing time period, process the recertification without the deduction. If a change in payment is reported, verify the change. If verification is not submitted by the due date, process without the payment. 

1700.3 Dependent Care

1700.3.1 Dependent Care Provider Examples

  1. Allow the actual cost of dependent care expenses, for a child under 18, including transportation. Examples of dependent care providers are:
    1. Private arrangements with persons not in the household
    2. Before or after school programs, such as the Boys and Girls Club, YMCA, YWCA, etc.
      1. Note: Elementary, middle and high school tuition, fees and transportation are not allowable dependent care expenses.
    3. Summer camp fees 
    4. Preschool programs

1700.3.2 Expense Allowed

  1. Allow child or dependent care costs only if the care is needed to permit all parents or guardians to:
    1. Look for work 
    2. Accept a job or continue working, regardless of whether the income is counted or excluded
    3. Comply with Work Requirements
    4. Attend training or education to prepare the person for work
  2. All parents or guardians in a household must need the care by meeting one of the above. Exception: See Special Dependent Care Situations.
  3. Allow the following costs:
    1. The unreimbursed billed expense for children or another dependent member(s); and/or
    2. Transportation costs to and from program sites. Use the actual cost of public transportation or .585 cents per mile. 

1700.3.2.1 Look for Work

  1. Allow if:
    1. Applied for or in receipt of unemployment compensation; or
    2. Complying with the Reach Up requirements. 
  2. Note: Reach Up case manager must verify dependent care is required for compliance.  

1700.3.3 Expense Not Allowed

  1. Do not allow a child care expense:
    1. If the expense is for elementary, middle or high school; 
    2. If another parent or guardian is in the household and able to provide the care;
    3. If another household member provides the care; 
    4. If the care is paid for by some in-kind benefit, such as food; or
    5. For the portion of expense covered by an excluded reimbursement or vendor payment, such as Child Care Services.
      1. Note: This includes any portion covered by excluded educational income.
  2. If a parent or guardian report that they are unable to provide care for the child and require dependent care, see Special Dependent Care Situations. 

1700.3.4 Special Dependent Care Situations 

1700.3.4.1 Unable to Provide Care

  1. Allow dependent care costs if parents or guardians claim:
    1. They are  Disabled and unable to care for the child, 

      A person who meets one or more of the following:

      • Receives disability or blindness benefits from any of these programs: Social Security,   SSI, or   MABD.
        • A person meets this even when the full benefit is withheld or deferred due to recoupment.
      • Receives a federally or state administered SSI supplement based on disability or blindness, or section 212(a) of PL 93-66.
      • Receives a disability retirement benefit from a government agency for a   disability considered permanent by SSAdisability considered permanent by SSA.

        Any of the following conditions:

        • Permanent loss of use of both hands, both feet, or one hand and one foot.
        • Amputation of leg at hip. Amputation of leg or foot because of diabetes or peripheral vascular diseases.
        • Total deafness, not correctable by surgery or hearing aid. Statutory blindness, unless caused by cataracts or detached retina.
        • IQ of 59 or less, established after the person becomes 16 years old.
        • Spinal cord or nerve root lesions resulting in paraplegia or quadriplegia.
        • Multiple sclerosis with damage to the nervous system caused by scattered areas of inflammation. The inflammation recurs and has progressed to varied interference with the function of the nervous system, including severe muscle weakness, paralysis, and vision and speech defects.
        • Muscular dystrophy with irreversible wasting of the muscles, impairing the ability to use the arms or legs.
        • Impaired renal function caused by chronic renal disease, resulting in severely reduced function which may require dialysis or kidney transplant.
        • Amputation of a limb of a person at least 55 years old.
      • Is a veteran the VA considers totally (100%) disabled or permanently housebound or in need of regular aid and attendance.
      • Is a veteran’s surviving spouse who the VA considers:
        • in need of regular aid and attendance, or
        • permanently housebound, or
        • approved for benefits because of the veteran’s death and has a disability considered permanent by SSA.
      • Is a veteran’s surviving child who the VA considers:
        • incapable of self-support, or
        • approved for benefits because of the veteran’s death and has a disability considered permanent by SSA
    2. They are unfit to care for the child, or
    3. They claim domestic violence where the child is at risk being left alone with the 2nd parent.
  2. Verification of the exception is mandatory. Send to AOPs for review.

1700.3.4.2 Future Return to Work Date 

  1. Allow dependent care costs when parents or guardians are temporarily out of work and have a return-to-work date.
  2. Temporarily out of work can include but is not limited to:
    1. Family or medical leave (FMLA) 
    2. Seasonal or short term lay off 
    3. Short term disability 
  3. Verification is not required, unless questionable.  

1700.3.5 Verification

  1. Verification is not required unless the information is questionable. Take the following actions only if verification is requested.

1700.3.5.1 Application

  1. If verification is not submitted prior to the end of the 30-day processing period, process the application without the expense. 
  2. Postpone verification for expedited service month(s), unless verification can be provided in time to meet the seven-day standard of promptness.

1700.3.5.2 Interim Report

  1. If a new expense or change is reported and verification is not provided by the requested due date, process the interim report without the deduction. 

1700.3.5.3 Unscheduled Changes 

  1. If a new expense is reported and verification is not submitted by the requested due date, process without the deduction. 
  2. If a change in expense is reported and benefits will:
    1. Increase - if verification is not provided by the requested due date, do not make a change; use the previously verified amount. 
    2. Decrease - process the change and verify at recertification. 

1700.3.5.4 Recertification

  1. If a new expense or a change in expense is reported and verification is not submitted by requested due date, process the recertification without the expense.

1700.4 Earned Income Deduction 

  1. Subtract 20% of Gross Earned Income. 
  2. Do not allow the 20% gross earned income deduction when determining an overpayment amount if the household failed to report earned income correctly or timely. See Overpayment for more details.

  1. Do not allow the following medical expenses:
    1. Expenses paid or that will be paid by another source (insurance, Medicare, Medicaid, etc.);

      1. Water,
      2. Vinegar; 
    4. Insurance premiums for health and accident policies such as:
      1. those payable in lump sum settlements for death or dismemberment, 
      2. income replacement policies regardless of payment schedule, or

    5. Special diets regardless if the diet is related to a medical condition. ExamplesExamplesEnsure, juices, nutritional supplements, etc.

    7. Any Schedule I controlled substance considered illegal by federal law.
      1. Marijuana 
      2. MDMA (Ecstasy)
      3. CBD (Cannabidiol) which contains more than .3 percent THC. 

1700.5.4 Standard Medical Expense 

  1. No deduction is allowed if the household’s allowed medical expenses are less than $35.01.
  2. The standard medical expense is used if allowed medical expenses are at least $35.01 and under $191.
  3. If the household's allowed medical expenses are greater that $191, the household may choose the standard medical expense or actual expenses. 
  4. The medical deduction is the total cost of the allowed medical expense minus $35. Example: the medical deduction is $156 when the standard medical expense is claimed ($191 - $35).

1700.5.5 Verification

  1. Verify medical expenses (including the amount of reimbursements) before allowing a deduction.  
  2. Postpone verification for expedited service month(s), unless verification can be provided in time to meet the seven-day standard of promptness.
  3. Verification of other factors, such as the eligibility of the Senior or  DisabledDisabled person or if services provided are allowable, is required if questionable.

1700.5.5.1 Application

  1. Verification is required for at least $35.01 in medical expenses. If the household reports more than $191 in medical expenses, verification is required for all reported medical expenses if the household chooses to have actual medical expenses deducted. If the expenses are greater than $191 and the household chooses the medical standard, and at least $35.01 is verified, give them the standard.
  2. Postpone verification for expedited service month(s), unless verification can be provided in time to meet the seven-day standard of promptness.

1700.5.5.2 Interim Report

  1. If a household that was not receiving the medical deduction reports medical expenses that would result in a deduction, follow the verification requirements in application.
  2. If a change of more than $25 is reported and the household wishes to:
    1. Continue with the medical standard, no verification is necessary.
    2. Continue with actual expenses, verify the new expense(s). If verification is not provided, do not allow the new expenses. Continue with the previously verified expenses.
  3. If a change of more than $25 is reported and it results in the household choosing to switch between the medical standard and actual expenses the household must verify all reported expenses prior to receiving a deduction for actual expenses.  If verification is not received, continue with the medical standard.
  4. If a change of more than $25 is reported and benefits will decrease:
    1. If the household is receiving the medical standard and the change results in less than $35.01 in medical expenses, remove the medical standard. Verification is not required.
    2. If the household is using actual medical expenses, and the change results in
      1. Expenses less than $191 but more than $35.01 switch the household to the medical standard. Verification is not required.
      2. Continued expenses of over $191 make the change and verify at recertification.

1700.5.5.3 Unscheduled Changes 

  1. If a household that was not receiving the medical deduction reports medical expenses that would result in a deduction, follow the verification requirements in application.
  2. If a change of more than $25 is reported and the household wishes to:
    1. Continue with the medical standard, no verification is necessary.
    2. Continue with actual expenses, verify the new expense(s). If verification is not provided, do not allow the new expenses. Continue with the previously verified expenses.
  3. If a change of more than $25 is reported and it results in the household choosing to switch between the medical standard and actual expenses the household must verify all reported expenses prior to receiving a deduction for actual expenses.  If verification is not received, continue with the medical standard.
  4. If a change of more than $25 is reported and benefits will decrease:
    1. If the household is receiving the medical standard and the change results in less than $35.01 in medical expenses, remove the medical standard. Verification is not required.
    2. If the household is using actual medical expenses, and the change results in
      1. Expenses less than $191 but more than $35.01 switch the household to the medical standard. Verification is not required.
      2. Continued expenses of over $191 make the change and verify at recertification.
  5. Exception: If the change is not reported by the household, act on the change only if it is verified upon receipt, such as the Medicare Part B premium reported through BENDEX. Otherwise, do not request verification and do not act on the change.

1700.5.5.4 Recertification

  1. Follow the verification requirements in application.
  2. Exception: Do not re-verify expenses for a household that is currently receiving the medical standard.

1700.6 Shelter Expenses

1700.6.1 Deductible Shelter Expenses

  1. The household’s shelter expenses are used for the calculation of net income and benefit amount. The shelter expense is based on the BSD (see below) and the HSD (see Homeless Shelter Deduction).

1700.6.2 Basic Shelter Deduction (BSD)

  1. The shelter expense must be a continuing charge that leads to ownership of the residence or rental payments for the residence. Only allow expenses for the residence the household lives in. Exception: Expenses for an unoccupied home are allowed if the home meets the unoccupied home requirements.

1700.6.2.1 Rent and Mortgage

  1. Allow costs of current rent or mortgages including:
    1. All mortgage loan repayments, including second mortgages, home equity loans and home equity lines of credit, if the home property is used as collateral for the loan;
    2. Payments to buy a mobile home, RV or travel trailer when used as a primary dwelling;
      1. Must include a kitchen and bathroom to be allowed. 
    3. Lot rent; 
    4. Interest fees on mortgage/mobile home payments; 
    5. Property taxes;
    6. Home insurance;
    7. Condominium and association fees; 
    8. Unreimbursed repair or replacements costs that was substantially damaged by a natural disaster, such as flood or fire; and/or
    9. Hotel cost when used as a primary dwelling.
  2. Do not allow the following costs:
    1. A “mechanic’s lien” or similar lien
    2. Mortgage life insurance premiums;
    3. Late payment fees;
    4. Extra charges, such as garage space, pet charges, etc.;
    5. Repair or replacement costs unless they result from a natural disaster;
    6. Down payments;
    7. Charges added to the rent to recoup for past unpaid rent amounts;
    8. Portion of expense covered by an excluded reimbursement or vendor payment;
    9. Payment for a vehicle without a kitchen and bathroom, even if used as the primary dwelling; or
    10. Auto insurance for a vehicle used as a primary dwelling. 

1700.6.2.2 Hotel Cost

  1. Allow hotel costs as shelter expense when the cost is related to a short or long-term stay and the hotel room is the customer's primary dwelling. Allowable cost related to hotel rooms are:
    1. Standard Room cost
    2. Taxes
  2.  Non-Allowable cost related to hotel rooms are:
    1. Room service charges
    2. Cost of Damages
  3. Do not allow hotel cost as shelter expense when the cost is related to a temporary stay, such as vacations.
  4. Allow the hotel cost for households in Emergency Housing subject to income contribution requirements. Use the number of days multiplied by the hotel rate if paying or incurring the expense.

1700.6.2.3 Property Taxes

  1. Allow property taxes as shelter expenses. Allowable cost related to property taxes are:
    1. Net property tax amount listed on property tax bill (amount after any State/Fed payments).

1700.6.2.4 Property Insurance

  1. Allow the cost of insurance on the structure itself, such as fire, flood and liability insurance, if the policy separates the costs of the structure from the contents. If the insurance policy does not separate these costs, allow the entire amount. 
  2. Allow service charges such as an added fee for monthly billing but do not allow late payment charges. 
  3. Do not allow renters insurance.

1700.6.3 Homeless Shelter Deduction (HSD)

  1. The HSD is a standard deduction for homeless households who incur or expect to incur allowable homeless shelter cost. The HSD is not determined by actual shelter cost. 
  2. Allow the HSD when: 
    1. All household members are Homeless; 
    2. The household incurs or expects to incur monthly shelter, utility expenses or basic living cost; and
    3. The household’s BSD is equal to or less than the current HSD standard. 
  3. Note: Homeless household with a PHON panel qualify for the HSD. 
  4. Do not allow the HSD when:
    1. All household members are not homeless. 
    2. All household members are homeless but:
      1. are receiving free shelter; or
      2. do not claim shelter, utility, or basic shelter cost. 
    3. The BSD provides the household a larger deduction. 

1700.6.3.1 Allowable Homeless Shelter Cost

  1. Shelter and utility cost incurred or expected to be incurred by the household can include basic shelter and utility costs or basic living costs (see below). 

1700.6.3.2 Basic Living Cost

  1. Basic living cost are considered shelter cost for a homeless household. These costs include but are not limited to:
    1. Doing Laundry 
    2. Trash Disposal
    3. Storage Fees
    4. Phone Calls 
    5. Transportation Costs 
    6. Parking Fees
    7. Mail Fees 
    8. Fuel or Wood for heating or cooking
    9. Equipment for camping or alternative shelter
  2. Note: Basic living cost are not included in the calculation of the BSD. They make a homeless household eligible for the HSD. 

1700.6.4 Unoccupied Home

  1. Shelter costs of an unoccupied home may be allowed if the household intends to return to the home and the members are away from the home for:
    1. Training;
    2. Employment;
    3. Illness; or 
    4. Abandonment because of natural disaster or casualty loss.
  2. Do not allow unoccupied home costs if:
    1. The home is being rented out or leased during the absence of the household members; and/or
    2. Any current residents are claiming shelter costs for the home for 3SVT or SNAP deductions in any state.
  3. The household is allowed only one utility allowance to cover both residences. Allow the highest applicable utility allowance. 

1700.6.5 Multiple Households in One Residence

  1. When separate 3SVT households share a residence, and one household gives its share of basic shelter expenses to the other who then pays them, give each household a deduction for their part of the basic shelter expenses. Shared Households

1700.6.6 Imminently Homeless 

  1. Individuals who report they are will lose their housing within 14 days are considered Homeless. 
  2. Shelter costs must be updated based on the details the household provides.
    1. Note: Dependent care transportation cost must also be updated. 

1700.6.6.1 Changes Reported in Person or Over the Phone

  1. Shelter costs should be removed starting the month after they will lose housing, or earlier if they report that costs have already ended.
  2. Households must report any changes in their situation to have shelter costs re-added to the case.
  3. The HSD indicator on the ADDR screen should be updated to “Y” (if eligible) in the month they become homeless or imminently homeless.

1700.6.6.2 Changes Reported in Writing or reported to other programs. 

  1. Attempt to call the household to gather more information.
  2. If you’re unable to reach them, send a VCR.
  3. If details are provided (by phone or in response to the VCR), follow the changes reported in person or other the phone process above.
  4. If there is no response to the VCR:
    1. Remove shelter costs starting the month after they will lose housing (unless other details are available in CATN).
    2. Clarify shelter costs at the next IR or recertification.
    3. Households must report changes in their situation to have shelter costs added back.
    4. Update the HSD indicator to “Y” (if eligible) in the month they become homeless/imminently homeless.

1700.6.7 Utilities

1700.6.7.1 Assignment of Utility Allowances

  1. Assign the appropriate utility allowance based on the household’s utility obligation. The utility allowances are:
    1. Standard Utility Allowance (SUA)
    2. Basic Utility Allowance (BUA)
    3. Telephone Allowance 

1700.6.7.2 Allowable Utility Costs

  1. Heating fuel, including propane tank rental fee and wood purchases when wood is the primary heating source
  2. Cooling (air-conditioning)
  3. Electricity
  4. Cooking Fuel
  5. Water/sewage, including well and septic tank installation and maintenance
  6. Garbage/trash collection, including fees paid at a transfer station to dispose of household trash
  7. Telephone, including a mobile phone
  8. Fees charged for installation of utilities
  9. Excess heating or cooling charges

1700.6.7.3 Standard Utility Allowance (SUA)

  1. The SUA includes all utility costs including installation/maintenance.
  2. Assign the SUASUA to a household that meets one of the following criteria:
    1. Is responsible for paying any portion of the heat or air conditioning costs directly to the provider, to a landlord, or to someone with whom the household shares these expenses (including excess or flat fees for these costs); or
    2. Households with a Senior or disabled member that receive LIHEAP payments (Fuel or Home Heating Assistance); or
    3. Households that have a Senior or disabled member and receives the $21 annual Fuel Assistance Program payment; or
    4. Receives a HUD, FmHA, or other program energy assistance payment, reimbursement or vendor payment and the household incurs out-of-pocket heating or cooling costs that exceed the amount of the payment; or
    5. Households with a Senior or disabled member who received weatherization services through the Vermont Home Weatherization Assistance Program within the past 12 months. Verification is not required unless questionable.
      1. Note: The $21 Annual Fuel Assistance Program Payment will still be issued for these households.
      2. Renters whose building was weatherized in the past 12 months qualify. 

    Utility Allowances 

    Allowance Amount
    SUA (with heat) $1,096
    BUA (without heat) $311
    Telephone $37
  3. Households that meet one of these criteria are eligible for the SUA year-round.
  4. Heating costs must be for primary heating and do not include:
    1. Space heaters
    2. Electric blankets 
    3. Heat lamps
    4. Use of the cooking stove to heat
  5. Cooling costs are for central or room air conditioners and do not include fans.

1700.6.7.4 $21 Annual Fuel Assistance Program Payment

  1. A $21 annual payment will be paid to 3SVT households if the household is not currently eligible for the SUA through any other criteria. The payment is made the month of application, and annually thereafter. The payment does not have to be for the current residence nor does the household have to have a current heating or cooling cost. 
    1. Only households with a senior or disabled member are allowed the SUA if the household received this payment the month of application or anytime in the prior 12 months. 

1700.6.7.5 Basic Utility Allowance (BUA)

  1. The BUA includes all non-heating/non-cooling costs including installation/maintenance.    
  2. Assign the  BUA to a household that does not qualify for the SUA but is responsible for paying any portion of two of the following expenses directly to the provider, to a landlord, or to someone with whom the household shares these expenses (including flat fees for these costs):
    1. Electricity
    2. Cooking fuel
    3. Water/sewage, including well and septic tank installation and maintenance
    4. Garbage/trash collection
    5. Telephone 

    Utility Allowances 

    Allowance Amount
    SUA (with heat) $1,096
    BUA (without heat) $311
    Telephone $37

1700.6.7.6 Telephone Allowance 

  1. Give the  telephone allowance to households when this is their only utility expense. This includes households which share the telephone expense.

    Utility Allowances 

    Allowance Amount
    SUA (with heat) $1,096
    BUA (without heat) $311
    Telephone $37

1700.6.7.7 Multiple Households in One Residence

  1. Multiple households are assigned the full amount of the SUA/BUA or Telephone Allowance as long as each household qualifies for it. For example, if two households split the heating costs for the residence, each will get the full SUA. 

1700.6.8 Verification

  1. Verification is not required unless the information is questionable. Take the following actions only if verification is requested.

1700.6.8.1 Application

  1. If verification is not submitted prior to the end of the 30-day processing period, process the application without the expense. 
  2. Postpone verification for expedited service month(s), unless verification can be provided in time to meet the seven-day standard of promptness.

1700.6.8.2 Interim Report

  1. If a new expense or change is reported and verification is not provided by the requested due date, process the interim report without the deduction. 

1700.6.8.3 Unscheduled Changes 

  1. If a new expense is reported and verification is not submitted by the requested due date, process without the deduction.
    1. If a change in expense is reported and benefits will:
    2. Increase, if verification is not provided by the requested due date, do not make a change; use the previously verified amount. 
  2. Decrease, process the change and verify at recertification. 

1700.6.8.4 Recertification

  1. If a new expense or a change in expense is reported and verification is not submitted by requested due date, process the recertification without the expense.

1500.1 Determining Resource Eligibility

  1. Use countable resources at the time the household is interviewed to determine eligibility.
  2. Households that are SCE or ECE are resource eligible.
  3. Exclude resources of any household member if that member receives any of the following: 
    1. SSI /AABD 
    2. PSE
    3. Vermont  EITC
    4.  Reach Ahead
    5.  Reach First 
    6.  Reach Up
    7. TANF-funded referral services
  4. Note: TANF-funded referral services benefit the entire household. This includes ineligible and disqualified members who made the household ineligible for SCE or ECE.

1500.3.2 Non-member Resources

  1.  Do not count resources of the following people: 
    1. Boarders, unless included in the household
    2. Ineligible students
    3. Residents of commercial boarding houses
    4. Roomers
    5. Live-in attendants
    6. SSI/Senior Cash Out recipients
    7. Residents of institutions
    8. Strikers
    9. Other people living with the household who: 
      1. are not mandatory household members, and
      2. do not purchase and prepare meals with the household.
  2. For a full description, see Non-members.

1500.4 Types of Countable Resources

1500.4.1 Equity Value Determination

  1. Count the equity value of all countable resources unless excluded. The equity value is the fair market value minus: 
    1. the amount owed on the resource, and
    2. the cost to sell or transfer the resource.
  2. Exception: The value of vehicles is determined based on how the vehicle is used. See Vehicles.
  3. The fair market value is the amount of money the resource can be sold for on the open market.

1500.4.2 Liquid Resources

  1. Liquid resources are resources the household can easily convert to cash, such as: 
    1. Cash on hand
    2. Credit card company gift cards
    3. Checking accounts
    4. Savings accounts
    5. Debit accounts
    6. Savings certificates
    7. Stocks
    8. Bonds
    9. Lump sum payments
    10. Cryptocurrency

1500.4.3 Non-liquid Resources

  1. Non-liquid resources are resources the household cannot easily convert to cash, such as: 
    1. Personal property
    2. Buildings
    3. Land
    4. Other property not listed under Excluded Resources
    5. Recreational vehicles, such as, but not limited to: 
      1. Boats
      2. Four wheelers
      3. Snowmobiles
    6. Vacation homes, such as, but not limited to: 
      1. Cabins
      2. Campers
      3. Mobile homes
      4. Travel trailers
  2. Vehicles unless excluded.

1500.4.4 Jointly Owned Resources

  1. Resources owned by two or more households, or resources showing more than one name are entirely available to each household.
  2. Exception: Exclude jointly owned resources: 
    1. the household verifies all or part are inaccessible;
    2. that cannot be subdivided, and the household cannot access or sell the resource unless the joint owner approves; or
    3. are jointly owned by: 
      1. people living in a shelter for battered persons and someone living in the abuser’s household, and
      2. the resource is controlled by the person living in the abuser’s household.
  3. Households may claim less than full ownership or even no ownership of jointly owned resources, such as a bank account.  Verify the household’s claim.  Based on the verification obtained, determine if the resource is: 
    1. counted,
    2. partially excluded, or
    3. fully excluded.
  4. Supervisor approval is required if a jointly owned resource is partially or fully excluded.  The owners of the resource must sign a statement attesting to the fact that: 
    1. the person’s name is on the asset solely for a specified reason, and
    2. the person owns none or less than the full resource amount.

1500.4.5 Commingled Funds

  1. Excluded resources commingled with countable resources remain excluded for six months from the date the resources are commingled.
  2. Exceptions: 
    1. Prorated income, such as self-employment or student income. Prorated income remains an excluded resource for the entire proration period.
    2. EITC  are excluded for a specific period of time, regardless if the money is commingled.  See Excluded Resources, Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC).
    3. Federal Income Tax Refunds.

1500.5 Excluded Resources

1500.5.1 Resources to be Excluded

  1. Exclude the following resources:

1500.5.2 ABLE Accounts

  1. Achieving a Better Life Experience savings accounts that provide secure funding for disability-related expenses on behalf of designated beneficiaries deemed disableddisabled before age 26. Section 529A of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986.

1500.5.3 Burial Plot

  1. One burial plot for each household member.

1500.5.4 Burial Trusts

  1. Exclude funds in a burial trust.

1500.5.5 Cafeteria Plans

  1. The value of a Cafeteria Plan  if the household cannot withdraw funds from the plan.

1500.5.6 Conservatorship Funds

  1. Treat conservatorship funds the same as an irrevocable trust.  See Trust Funds.

1500.5.7 Crowdfunding

  1. Exclude funds until they are accessible to the household. Examples: Go Fund Me, Kickstarter, Indiegogo and Tilt

1500.5.8 Crime Victim’s Payments

  1. Payments from a crime victim compensation program funded under the Victims of Crime Act of 1984.

1500.5.9 Current Month’s Income

  1. Income for the current month, even if deposited into a checking or savings account. This includes prorated income during the months it is intended to cover. See Determining Income and Deductions.
  2. When determining resources at application, subtract all income received in the month, up to and including the date of interview.

1500.5.10 Deferred Compensation Plans

  1. All plans with funds contributed by the employer or the employee. Exclude as long as the funds remain in a deferred compensation plan, regardless: 
    1. if accessible, or
    2. whether the person is still employed.
  2. See Pension Plans/Retirement Accounts.

1500.5.11 Disaster and Emergency Assistance Payments

  1. Disaster and emergency assistance payments as provided under the Disaster Relief Act of 1974, and as amended by Public Law 100-107, the Disaster Relief and Emergency Assistance Amendments of 1988. See Disaster Assistance.

1500.5.12 Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)

  1. EITC payments are excluded as follows: 
    1. Federal EITC payments under section 3507 of the Internal Revenue Code for the month of receipt and the following month.
    2. Federal, State or local EITC for 12-months if the household: 
      1. participated in SNAP /3SVT  when the EITC was received, and
      2. continues to participate during the 12-month period. Breaks of one month or less are not non-participation.

1500.5.13 Education Accounts

  1. Money in qualified tuition programs under Internal Revenue Code, Sections: 
    1. 529, (the Vermont Higher Education Investment Plan), or
    2. 530, Coverdell Education Savings Account.
  2. Money deposited into non-tax exempt accounts is not excluded.
  3. See Students.

1500.5.14 Energy Assistance Payments

  1. Vermont Seasonal Fuel Assistance Program payments (including the $21 payment).

1500.5.15 Employment Property

  1. Property needed for the employment or self-employment of a household member.

1500.5.16 Farm Property

  1. Property needed for self-employment farming including, but not limited to: 
    1. Land
    2. Equipment
    3. Supplies
    4. Tractors
  2. Exclude self-employment farming property for one year after the household stops self-employment farming.

1500.5.17 Federal Income Tax Refunds

  1. Exclude as a resource for 12-months after receipt.

1500.5.18 Federal Payments

  1. Federal payments excluded by law include but are not limited to: 
    1. Federal tax refunds, Public Law 112-240, exclude for 12-months;
    2. AmeriCorps Payments;
    3. Domestic Volunteers Services Act payments, Public Law 93-113;
    4. Uniform Relocation Assistance and Real Property Acquisition Policy Act of 1970 reimbursements, Public Law 91-646, Section 216;
    5. Agent Orange Settlement, Public Laws 101 and 201;
    6. Nazi Persecution, Public Law 103-286;
    7. Student income under: 
      1. Title IV of the Higher Education Act, or
      2. Bureau of Indian Affairs;
    8. Special Supplemental Food Program for Women, Infants, and Children (WIC) benefits, Public Law 92-433, Section 9;
    9. Japanese and Aleuts Wartime Relocation of Civilians payments, Public Law 100-383;
    10. Radiation Exposure Compensation Act payments, Public Law 101-425;
    11. Children of Women Vietnam Veterans Born with Certain Birth Defects payments, Public Law 106-419.
  2. For more information, see Types of Income.

1500.5.19 Flexible Benefit Plan

  1. The value of a flexible benefit plan (Cafeteria Plan) if the household cannot withdraw funds from the plan.

1500.5.20 Funeral Contract

  1. Exclude the value of burial trusts.

1500.5.21 Gift Cards

  1. Exclude establishment specific gift cards.

1500.5.22 Homestead

  1. The home the household lives in and the Surrounding Property.  Homestead property includes rights of way, such as roads running through the property. Exception: Count any part of the household’s equity value of property separated from the home and lot by another person’s property.
  2. A partially completed home, unless the household: 
    1. owns, or
    2. is buying a home elsewhere.
  3. A lot the household plans to build a home on, unless the household: 
    1. owns, or
    2. is buying a home elsewhere.
  4. Buildings on the property, other than homes.
  5. The homestead remains excluded when the household is temporarily away from home due to: 
    1. Employment
    2. Training for future employment
    3. Illness
    4. The home and lot are unlivable due to Household Misfortune 
  6. If the homestead contains two or more homes, exclude the home where the household lives. Exclude only one home at a time, unless the home meets another exclusion.

1500.5.23 Household Goods/Personal Effects

  1. Household Goods and Personal Effects, regardless of their value.

1500.5.24 HUD Family Self-Sufficiency Program

  1. Money held in a family self-sufficiency account as long as the money is not withdrawn. Treat as a non-recurring lump sum when the money is withdrawn.

1500.5.25 Inaccessible Resources

  1. Resources the household cannot access, such as, but not limited to: 
    1. Security deposits
    2. Crowdfunding accounts
    3. Prepaid irrevocable burial trust
    4. Prepaid irrevocable funeral contract
    5. Certain vehicles (See Vehicles)
    6. Property in probate
    7. Unpaid portion of loans owed to a household member
    8. Certain trust funds (See Trust Funds)
    9. Resources discovered by the agency but unknown to the household
    10. Real property the household is making a good effort to sell
    11. Certain jointly owned resources (See Jointly Owned Resources)
    12. Resources with a lien prohibiting the household from selling
    13. Resources with an Equity Value  of $1,500 or less
    14. Exception: Does not include: 
      1. Stocks,
      2. Bonds, or
      3. Negotiable financial instruments.

1500.5.26 Income Producing Property

  1. Property annually producing income consistent with the property’s Fair Market Value, even if only used on a seasonal basis. Income producing property includes but is not limited to: 
    1. Rental homes
    2. Vacation homes

1500.5.27 Individual Development Accounts (IDA)

  1. Individual Development Account is an optional, interest-bearing account, through Community National Bank. IDAs encourage people to save for long term goals without the savings affecting their 3SVT eligibility or benefit amount. The IDA program matches every $1 saved with $2, up to $2,000.

1500.5.28 Installment Sales Contracts

  1. The value of installment contracts for land or buildings if: 
    1. the contract produces income consistent with the fair market value of the property, or
    2. is held as security in exchange for a purchase price consistent with the Fair Market Value of the property.

1500.5.29 Leased Vehicles

  1. A leased vehicle is not a resource to the household during the time the vehicle is under contract, even if part of the lease payments are applied to the principal. The vehicle becomes a resource at the end of the contract if retained.
  2. Note: Add CARS panel to STATs for a leased vehicle. Be sure the panel is not countable and noted as a leased vehicle.

1500.5.30 Life Insurance Policy Cash Value

  1. The cash value of a life insurance policy.

1500.5.31 Loans

  1. Loans for business purposes that legally bans the household from using for a reason other than the business.

1500.5.32 Native American Gambling Operations

  1. Native American gambling payments made to children placed in an inaccessible trust.

1500.5.33 Native American Tribal Land

  1. Land that: 
    1. is held jointly with the tribe, or
    2. can only be sold with the approval of the Bureau of Indian Affairs.

1500.5.34 Native American Tribal Judgment Funds

  1. Funds from judgment awards due to breaches of treaty provisions, and funds held in trusts by the Secretary of the Interior.  Native American tribal judgment funds are not from Native American gambling operations.  The funds come from the sale or lease of: 
    1. oil,
    2. gas, and
    3. other tribal assets, such as land.
  2. Native American tribal judgment funds payments include, but are not limited to: 
    1. Alaska Native Claims Settlement Act
    2. Sac and Fox Indian Claims Agreement
    3. Public Law 94-114, Section 6, regarding sub-marginal land held in trust by the United States
    4. Relocation assistance to members of the Hopi and Navajo Tribes under Public Law 93-531
    5. Disposition of funds to the Grand River Band of Ottawa Indians, Public Law 94-540
    6. Confederated Tribes and Bands of the Yakim Indian Nation from the Indian Claims Commission, Public Law 95-433, Section 2
    7. Confederated Tribes and Bands of the Apache Tribe of the Mescalero Reservation from the Indian Claims Commission, Public Law 95-433, Section 2
    8. Passamaguoddy Tribe or any of the members pursuant to the Maine Indian Claims Settlement Act of 1980, Public Law 96-420, Section 5.
    9. Penobscot Nation or any of the members pursuant to the Maine Indian Claims Settlement Act of 1980, Public Law 96-420, Section 5
  3. Exclude payments of: 
    1. $2,000 or less for each household member, per payment, per calendar year, and
    2. any amount placed in an inaccessible trust.

1500.5.35 Other Resources Sold Under Installment Contract

  1. Other resources sold under contract by installment payments, such as personal goods.

1500.5.36 Plan for Achieving Self-Support (PASS) Account

  1. Money deposited in a special PASS  account.

1500.5.37 Pension Plans/Retirement Accounts

  1. All tax-exempt plans or accounts with funds contributed by the employer or the employee.  Exclude as long as the funds remain in a deferred compensation plan, even if accessible, and regardless of whether the person is still employed.  See Deferred Compensation Plans.
  2. Pension plans/retirement accounts include, but are not limited to:
    1. IRA 
    2. SEP 
    3. Keogh Plan 
    4. 401K 
    5. SIMPLE 401 (k) 
  3. Accounts under Internal Revenue Code of 1986, Sections 
    1. 403(a)
    2. 403(b)
    3. 408
    4. 408A
    5. 457(b)
    6. 501(c)(18)
  4. Money in a non-tax exempt pension plan or retirement account is not excluded.

1500.5.38 Pets and Property Used for the Pet

  1. The value of pets and property used for the pet, such as but not limited to: 
    1. Saddles
    2. Bridles
    3. Leashes

1500.5.39 Property to Maintain Excluded Vehicles

  1. Property needed to maintain an excluded vehicle. Exclude only the piece of the land used for storage or repair of the vehicle. The vehicle must be used for one of the following reasons: 
    1. Produce income more than 50 percent of the time it is used.
    2. Produce income consistent with its Fair Market Value, even if only used on a seasonal basis.
    3. For long distance travel (not daily commuting) needed for the employment of a household member or another person whose resources are available to the household.
    4. Used as the household’s home.
    5. Transport a physically Disabled household member.
    6. Carry the primary source of fuel for heating for the household.
    7. Carry the primary source of water for the household.
  2. For a full description, see Excluded Vehicles.

1500.5.40 Resources Under a Lien

  1. The value of resources under a lien or security agreement placed as a condition for a business loan. The security or lien agreement must prohibit the household from selling the resources.

1500.5.42 Trust Funds

  1. Trust funds and income from trust funds that are inaccessible to the household. To be inaccessible, all of the following rules must be met: 
    1. During the certification period: 
      1. the trust arrangement is not likely to end, and
      2. no household member has the authority to end the trust arrangement or change the beneficiary.
  2. The trustee handling the trust must be a: 
    1. Court appointee with court-imposed limits placed on the use of funds, or
    2. Institution, court, corporation, or organization not owned by or under the control of a household member.
    3. Trust investments must not directly involve or help any business or corporation under the control, direction or influence of a household member.
    4. The irrevocable trust must be established from:
      1. non-household funds by a non-household member, or
      2. the household’s funds, if the trustee uses the funds solely to: 
      3. make investments on behalf of the trust; or
      4. pay the educational expenses of any person named by the household creating the trust; or
      5. pay the medical expenses of any person named by the household creating the trust.
  3. Types of trusts include, but are not limited to: 
    1. Federal Thrift Savings Plans, Section 8439, Title 5, United States Code
    2. Student income accounts in a qualified tuition programs under Internal Revenue Code, Sections: 
      1. 529 (the Vermont Higher Education Investment Plan), or
      2. 530 Coverdell Education Savings Account 

1500.5.43 US Savings Bonds

  1. The value of US Savings Bonds that cannot be cashed in.
  2. Count the amount of bonds that can be cashed in based on the bond's worth at the time of the determination, not on the bond's face value.

1500.6 Vehicles

1500.6.1 Definition of Vehicles

  1. A vehicle means any motorized means of transportation that requires a Vermont vehicle registration. Vehicles which do not require a Vermont registration are counted based on total fair market value. Exception: Treat unregistered vehicles on Native American tribal reservations not requiring a Vermont registration to be driven by tribal members as vehicles requiring Vermont registration.
  2. Vehicles include but are not limited to: 
    1. Cars
    2. Trucks
    3. Vans
    4. Motorcycles
    5. Snowmobiles
    6. Four wheelers
    7. Motor homes
    8. Boats

1500.6.2 Excluded Vehicles

  1. Exclude all vehicles.

1500.6.3 Fair Market Value (FMV)

1500.6.3.1 Determining Fair Market Value (FMV)

  1. When determining the Fair Market Value (FMV), use the wholesale or average trade-in value, not the retail value. Do not adjust the value based on: 
    1. mileage,
    2. options, or
    3. special equipment.
  2. FMV is usually found from vehicle pricing sources, such as the NADA Used Car Guide.
  3. Accept the value the household gives for older vehicles no longer listed in the NADA book, unless the: 
    1. estimated value is questionable, and
    2. the value will affect the household’s eligibility.
  4. Verify new car or questionable FMVs not listed in the NADA book using other reputable sources, such as, but not limited to a car dealership.
  5. Verify the FMV using a reliable source familiar with these types of vehicles: 
    1. classic,
    2. antique, or
    3. custom built.
  6. Allow the household the opportunity to provide proof of a lower FMV when the household disputes the assigned value.
  7. CATN your source for the FMV used in your determination, as values may change in the future.

1500.6.3.3 Jointly Owned Vehicles

  1. Exclude jointly owned vehicles.

1500.7 Transferred Resources

1500.7.1 Allowable Transfer Policy Requirement

  1. Verify if countable resources were transferred meeting allowable transfer policy. Ineligible transfers of resources disqualify the entire household for up to 12-months.

1500.7.2 Allowable Transfers

  1. Allowable resource transfers are those that: 
    1. Are made by categorically eligible households.
    2. Would not affect eligibility, such as excluded personal property.
    3. Are sold or traded at or near Fair Market Value .
    4. Are transferred between members of the household, including ineligible and disqualified people.
    5. Are transferred for reasons other than to qualify or attempt to qualify for 3SVT, such as putting money in a student income account.

1500.7.3 Ineligible Transfers

  1. Ineligible resource transfers occur at application if the: 
    1. resources are transferred within the three-month period before the date of applying for 3SVT, and
    2. the transfer was done solely to qualify for 3SVT, or
    3. during the current certification period if done solely to remain eligible for 3SVT.

1500.7.4 Determining the Disqualification Period

  1. An applicant household’s disqualification period begins the month of application.
  2. An active household’s’ disqualification period begins the month following the month timely notice expires, unless the household requested:
    1. a Fair Hearing, and
    2. continued benefits pending the hearing decision.
  3. Use the following steps to determine the disqualification period: 
    1. Add the value of the countable transferred resources to the value of the household’s other countable resources.
    2. Subtract the appropriate resource limit for the household from the amount in step 1.
    3. Compare the result to the table below to determine the period the household is disqualified from receiving 3SVT benefits.
    4. Notify the household of their disqualification, and how long the disqualification will last.
    5. Disqualification Period Table. 
      1. Amount in Excess of Resource Limit   Disqualification Period
        $0 – 249.99   One month
        $250 – 999.99   Three months
        $1,000 – 2,999.99   Six months
        $3,000 – 4,999.99  Nine months
        $5,000 or more   Twelve months
    6. Changes in the household’s situation do not change or end the disqualification period.

1500.8 Verification

1500.8.1 Resources Requiring Verification

  1. Verify the following: 
    1. The total countable resource value when it is within $200 of the maximum resource limit;
    2. Exception: Do not verify for 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households. 
      1. Transferred resources;
      2. Inaccessible resources;
    3. Jointly owned inaccessible resources. Verify the household: 
      1. can only access a portion, and the value of the portion; or
      2. cannot subdivide the property without the joint owner’s approval.
    4. Inaccessible real property. Verify the household is making a good faith effort to sell the property at a reasonable price. Use collateral contacts and documents, such as but not limited to: 
      1. Newspaper ads
      2. Real estate broker listings
    5. Vehicle’s fair market value when the household disputes the assigned value;
    6. New or older vehicles whose values are not available through usual sources, such as the NADA  book;
    7. Fair Market Value  for the following vehicles: 
      1. classic,
      2. antique, or
      3. custom built.
    8. Questionable resource information.

1500.8.2 Application

  1. Verify when the resource amount is within $200 of the maximum for non-3SquaresVT in a SNAP households, or if questionable. Deny the application if not verified by the due date.
  2. Postpone verification for expedited service month(s), unless verification can be provided in time to meet the seven-day standard of promptness.

1500.8.3 Recertification

  1. Verify when the resource amount is within $200 of the maximum for non-3SquaresVT in a SNAP households, or if questionable. Deny the recertification application if not verified by the due date.

2600.1 Procedure to Restore Underpaid Benefits

  1. Restore underpaid benefits to households when the underpayment was not caused by the household. Complete the ESD 203R, Lost 3SquaresVT Benefits Restoration Worksheet.
  2. Note: The FACU now completes the ESD 203R. See Overpayments and Underpayments SOP.

2600.2 When to Restore Underpaid Benefits

  1. Act within ten days to restore benefits due to an underpayment because of:
    1. an agency error, 
    2. changes in State or Federal regulations or laws, or
    3. a fair hearing decision reversing an action taken on the case.
  2. Restore underpayments even if the household is not currently eligible. The household is not required to request issuance of the underpayment.

2600.3 Timeframes

  1. Restore underpaid benefits for not more than 12-months before the date:
    1. the household requests the underpayment be restored, including requesting a  Fair Hearing, 
    2. the underpayment is discovered, or
    3. the agency is notified of the underpayment, including fair hearing decisions reversing agency actions.
  2. Exception: A federal regulation or court case may specify a longer restoration period. 
  3. Note: If more than one occurs at the same time restore benefits from the date that occurred first. 

2600.4 Types of Underpayments

2600.4.1 Agency Error 

  1. Underpayments due to agency error occur when the agency failed to provide the household with the correct amount of benefits.  

2600.4.1.1 Incorrect Amount

  1. Calculate underpayments for the time the household was eligible and had an open case. Issue the difference between the amount the household received and the correct amount.

2600.4.1.2 Case Closed in Error

  1. Reopen the case and restore benefits beginning with the first month benefits were not received after closure.

2600.4.1.3 Application Delay or Denial

  1. Restore benefits for the month of application and any other months when the application was:
    1. not processed timely, or 
    2. denied in error.
  2. See Initial Months

2600.4.1.4 Recertification Delay or Denial

  1. Timely recertification applications denied in error or delayed must have benefits restored beginning the first month following the end of the previous certification period.

2600.4.2 Changes in State or Federal Laws or Regulations

  1. Restore any underpaid benefits when required and as directed by State or Federal laws or regulations.

2600.4.3 Fair Hearing Decision

  1. Restore benefits when a Fair Hearing decision states the agency’s actions regarding the household’s benefit amount or eligibility were incorrect. Issue underpaid benefits for not more than 12-months from whichever timeframe date occurred first.

2600.4.4 Reversed IPV Determination

  1. Restore underpaid benefits for any months a person was disqualified for IPV when their disqualification is reversed.  Households who disagree with the IPV determination at the administrative disqualification hearing are providing notice of their request to have the underpayment restored. See Intentional Program Violation.

2600.5 Determining the Amount to Restore

2600.5.1 Procedure to Determine the Amount to Restore

  1. Determine the months that benefits were potentially underpaid when the household was eligible or should have been eligible. Exclude months:
    1. when the household was ineligible, or 
    2. more than 12-months before the timeframes.  
  2. Compare the amount of benefits:
    1. the household received,
    2. to what the household should have received.  
  3. If the household received less than they were due, the difference between the amounts is the underpayment. 
  4. Subtract any claims to be offset from the underpayment amount.

2600.5.2 Information Needed for Determination

  1. The household must provide any missing information needed to determine a potential underpayment when the case record doesn’t contain all necessary information. Send the household form ESD 202VCR, Verification Change Request, requesting the missing information for each month needed. Deny restored benefits due to an underpayment for the months the household fails to provide the missing information. 

2600.6 Offsetting Overpayment Claims

  1. Restored benefits for underpayments must be used to offset any unpaid 3SVT claims including compromised overpayments. Amounts remaining after offsetting the claim are issued to the household. Exception: Restored underpayments are not used to offset a claim when restoring benefits due to an underpayment for an initial month’s benefit, unless the household agrees to the reduction.   
  2. Apply underpayments to a prior claim by faxing the ESD 203R, Lost 3SquaresVT Benefits Restoration Worksheet, to the Fraud and Claims Unit at 802-241-0960, ATTN: Supervisor.

2600.7 Issuing Benefits

  1. Issue underpayments to the household’s  EBT account in addition to any amounts the household normally received.  
  2. Cash-Out households are issued the underpayment by:
    1. check, 
    2. direct deposit to their checking or savings account, or 
    3. through an  EBT card for households without a bank account.
  3. If the household’s composition changed, issue benefits to the household containing the majority of members at the time the underpayment occurred.  When the majority of household members can’t be located or determined, issue benefits to the household containing the person who was the Head of Household at the time the underpayment occurred. 

2600.8 Notification

  1. Send the household form ESD 220, Notice of Decision, advising the household of the underpayment being restored including the:
    1. amount of the underpayment, 
    2. reason for the underpayment, and
    3. household’s right to a fair hearing if they disagree.
  2. When underpayments are used to offset a claim, the notice must also include:
    1. an explanation that the claim amount was adjusted,
    2. the amount of the underpayment used to offset the claim,
    3. the new claim amount, and
    4. the amount of underpayment to be issued, if any.

2600.9 Household Disagrees with Amount or Decision

2600.9.1 Disagrees with the Amount

  1. Households that do not agree with the underpayment amount have the right to request a fair hearing within 90-days of the date they are notified of the restored underpayment. Issue the original underpayment amount to the household, pending the hearing decision. Do not adjust the underpayment amount unless the hearing decision rules in favor of the household and requires an additional underpayment issuance. 

2600.9.2 Disagrees with the Decision

  1. When a determination is made finding the household was not underpaid, and the household disagrees with the decision, they have 90-days to request a fair hearing. Restore benefits to the household only when the fair hearing rules in favor of the household.

2600.10 CATN

  1. CATN:
    1. the reason for the underpayment,
    2. the calculation, 
    3. the months restored, 
    4. if benefits were used to offset a claim, and
    5. the amount of the underpayment before and after an offset.

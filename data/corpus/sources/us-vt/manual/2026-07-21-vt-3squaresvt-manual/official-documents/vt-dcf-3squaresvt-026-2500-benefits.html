2500.1 Overview

  1. Benefits are 3SVT assistance issued to a household through an EBT card or through Cash Out. 

2500.2 Initial Month 

  1. An initial month is the first month the household receives benefits for the first time or following any time the household was not approved.  
  2. Exception: Initial month for migrant or seasonal farm workers is the first month the household is approved following a period of more than 30 days since the last approval. 
  3. Households applying on the first of the month and found eligible receive a full initial month’s benefits. Households applying after the first of the month and found eligible receive prorated benefits. See Prorated Benefits, Initial Month.

2500.3 Combined Benefit

  1. An issuance of benefits for both the initial month and the following month at the same time.  Issue combined benefits when the:
    1. date of application is after the 15th of the month, and
    2. the initial month’s prorated benefits are approved after the adverse action deadline in the month of application, and
    3. the household is eligible to receive benefits the following month.

2500.4 Suspended Benefits

  1. Suspend benefits, do not deny or close 3SVT when an eligible household qualifies for zero benefits. 
  2. Follow change processing requirements when the household reports a change that results in a benefit. See Change Processing, Benefit Increase.
  3. Issue prorated benefits from the date the change was reported.

2500.5 Prorated Benefits

2500.5.1 Definition of Prorated Benefits

  1. Issuance of less than a full month’s benefits.

2500.5.2 Initial Month

  1. Issue prorated benefits for initial month benefits when the household:
    1. Applies after the first day of the month. Benefits are issued from the date of application through the last day of the month.
    2. Was at fault for a delay in the application process.  Benefits are issued from the date the household takes the required action to the end of that month. See Application, Household Caused Delay.

2500.5.3 Recertification

  1. Issue prorated benefits at recertification if the household:
    1. Files for recertification within 30 days after the end of the certification period. Benefits are issued from the date of application through the last day of the month.
    2. Was at fault for a delay in the recertification process. Benefits are issued from the date the household takes the required action to the end of that month.
  2. See Recertification, Household Caused Delays.

2500.5.4 Activation After Suspension

  1. Issue prorated benefits when a household in suspended status reports a change that results in a benefit. Benefits are issued from the date the household reported the change. 

2500.5.5 Determining Prorated Benefits

  1. Determine the prorated benefit amount using the 3SquaresVT Allotment Proration Tables:
    1. Locate the full initial month’s benefit amount from the column on the left. 
    2. Next find the date of application at the top.  
    3. The corresponding amount is the household’s prorated amount.
    4. Note: Prorated benefits of less than $10 are not issued for the initial month only.   

2500.6 Minimum Benefits

  1. Except during an initial month all eligible one and two-person households receive a minimum benefitminimum benefit.

  2. Eligible households of three or more may be entitled to zero benefits. Suspend the case for these households.

2500.7 Net Income and Benefit Calculation

2500.7.1 Net Income Calculation

  1. Follow the steps below to determine net income.
    1. Total gross monthly earned income.
    2. Add total gross monthly unearned income.
    3. Subtract farm self-employment losses, if any.
    4. If the result of step 3 is over the gross income limitgross income limit for that household size, stop here and deny the case. Exceptions:
      1. Expanded categorical eligibility (ECE) households must be below 185% gross income.
      2. Standard categorical eligibility (SCE) households or households with a Senior or disableddisabled member do not have a gross income limit.

        A person who meets one or more of the following:

        • Receives disability or blindness benefits from any of these programs: Social Security,   SSI, or   MABD.
          • A person meets this even when the full benefit is withheld or deferred due to recoupment.
        • Receives a federally or state administered SSI supplement based on disability or blindness, or section 212(a) of PL 93-66.
        • Receives a disability retirement benefit from a government agency for a   disability considered permanent by SSAdisability considered permanent by SSA.

          Any of the following conditions:

          • Permanent loss of use of both hands, both feet, or one hand and one foot.
          • Amputation of leg at hip. Amputation of leg or foot because of diabetes or peripheral vascular diseases.
          • Total deafness, not correctable by surgery or hearing aid. Statutory blindness, unless caused by cataracts or detached retina.
          • IQ of 59 or less, established after the person becomes 16 years old.
          • Spinal cord or nerve root lesions resulting in paraplegia or quadriplegia.
          • Multiple sclerosis with damage to the nervous system caused by scattered areas of inflammation. The inflammation recurs and has progressed to varied interference with the function of the nervous system, including severe muscle weakness, paralysis, and vision and speech defects.
          • Muscular dystrophy with irreversible wasting of the muscles, impairing the ability to use the arms or legs.
          • Impaired renal function caused by chronic renal disease, resulting in severely reduced function which may require dialysis or kidney transplant.
          • Amputation of a limb of a person at least 55 years old.
        • Is a veteran the VA considers totally (100%) disabled or permanently housebound or in need of regular aid and attendance.
        • Is a veteran’s surviving spouse who the VA considers:
          • in need of regular aid and attendance, or
          • permanently housebound, or
          • approved for benefits because of the veteran’s death and has a disability considered permanent by SSA.
        • Is a veteran’s surviving child who the VA considers:
          • incapable of self-support, or
          • approved for benefits because of the veteran’s death and has a disability considered permanent by SSA

2500.7.2 Benefit Calculation

  1. Follow the steps below to determine the benefit calculation.
    1. Multiply the net income times 30%. Round up to the nearest dollar if the 30% amount ends in cents (example: $100 X 30% = $30.30, rounded to $31).
    2. Deduct the rounded 30% figure from the household’s maximum benefitmaximum benefit.
      1. No benefit is issued if the initial month benefits are less than $10.
      2. After the initial month, one or two person households are eligible for the minimum benefit if the end result is less than that amount.
      3. After the initial month, three or more person households are eligible for the actual amount.

2500.8 Issuance Schedule

  1. 3SVT benefits are issued once a month and made available to the household on the first of every month.

2500.9 Electronic Benefit Transfer (EBT) 

2500.9.1 Using EBT Cards

  1. Households access benefits using reusable plastic cards (EBT cards) to purchase eligible foods at an authorized point of sale. Authorized points of sale include but are not limited to:
    1. Retail food stores
    2. Farmer's markets
    3. Homeless Shelter
    4. Shelters for battered persons
    5. Group living for the disabled facilities
    6. Residential addiction treatment centers
  2. Direct retailers with questions to USDA at 1-877-823-4369.

2500.9.2 Replacing EBT Cards

  1. EBT cards are replaced through the EBT unit.
  2. Follow the steps below when households report their EBT card was lost, stolen or damaged:
    1. Verify the household’s mailing address is correct in EBT Edge.  
    2. If the address is correct, give the household the EBT customer service number (1-800-914-8605) to report their card was lost, stolen or damaged.
    3. If the mailing address is incorrect, send an email to the EBT unit with the household’s correct mailing address. Write "LAST NAME Address Change & Card Request" in the subject line of the email. Request the EBT Unit:
      1. update the mailing address, and
      2. replace the EBT card for the household. 
    4. CATN your actions.

2500.9.3 Expunged EBT Benefits

  1. 3SVT benefits will be expunged when an EBT card has not been used by the household in 274 days. Expunged benefits are returned to the federal SNAP program. The household cannot access or have benefits returned to their account once the benefits are expunged.
  2. Exception: See Pay a Claim.

2500.10 Cash Out

  1. Households in which all members are 65 years of age or older and/or receive SSI are eligible for Cash Out 3SVT benefits.
  2. Cash Out benefits are issued to eligible households through direct deposit into their checking or savings account, or by a check.  Households without a bank account receive their benefits through an EBT card. 
  3. Authorize issuance of a check for cash out households;
    1. eligible for expedited service for the first month of eligibility, or
    2. when the initial month’s issuance can’t be issued by computer to meet application processing timeframes.

2500.11 Using 3SVT Benefits

2500.11.1 Eligible Locations and Uses of 3SVT Benefits

  1. The EBT card can be used to purchase eligible food at any retailer or wholesaler authorized to accept 3SVT benefits. EBT benefits or cash out benefits can be used to pay a 3SVT claim.

2500.11.2 Eligible Food Types

  1. Households can use 3SVT EBT cards to buy eligible foods, such as but not limited to:
    1. Breads
    2. Cereals
    3. Fruits
    4. Vegetables
    5. Meats 
    6. Fish
    7. Poultry
    8. Dairy products
    9. Seeds and plants which produce food to eat
    10. Authorized prepared meals

2500.11.3 Ineligible Food

  1. Households cannot use their 3SVT EBT cards to purchase non-food items, such as but not limited to:
    1. Beer
    2. Wine 
    3. Liquor
    4. Cigarettes
    5. Tobacco
    6. Pet food
    7. Soaps
    8. Paper products
    9. Household supplies
    10. Vitamins
    11. Medicines
    12. Hot foods
    13. Deposit fees
    14. Energy drinks classified by the FDA as supplements (have a supplement facts label rather than a nutrition facts label)

2500.11.4 Pay a Claim 

  1. Households can pay a 3SVT claim using benefits from their 3SVT account. Households can use their:
    1. active, 
    2. expunged benefits
  2. The household must provide written or verbal permission to repay a claim using their 3SVT active benefits.  
  3. The initial month’s benefits must not be reduced to offset a claim, unless the household agrees to the reduction.
  4. See Overpayments and Claims for more information about claims establishment and collection.

2500.12 Replacement of Value of Lost Food

  1. 3SVT households that lost food due to a Household Misfortune, such as a flood or fire, may qualify to have the value of their lost food replaced. The household must report the loss within ten days of the misfortune.
  2. Complete the following steps to determine the household’s eligibility and replacement amount:
    1. Determine the date of the misfortune.  If the loss is due to a power outage the ten days starts the day the power is restored.
    2. Give the household Form 271, Attestation of Loss and Request for Replacement of 3SquaresVT Benefits.  The household must:
      1. complete and sign the form, and
      2. return the form within ten days of reporting the loss.  If the tenth day falls on a weekend or holiday the form is due the first workday following the weekend or holiday.
    3. Verify the loss through:
      1. a collateral contact,
      2. documentation from a community agency, such as the:
        1. fire department, 
        2. power company, 
          1. Note: When calling to confirm an outage, provide an address only. Do not give a specific customer name.
        3. Red Cross, or
      3. by completing a home visit.
    4. CATN the details of the verification.
    5. Check ELIG/D/FS to determine the benefit amount.  Use the “month to date paid” amount as the allowable total for replacement.  Replace the amount equal to the value of the food actually lost, but not more than the household’s “month to date paid” amount.  
    6. Send an email to COPS and cc: AOPS to request the replacement. The email must contain the following information:
      1. Case name
      2. Case number
      3. Type of household misfortune
      4. Date of the loss
      5. Date power was restored (provided the misfortune was due to a power outage)
      6. Month being requested for replacement
      7. Requested replacement amount
      8. Attach completed Form 271.
  3. AOPS reviews all situations.  
  4. COPS issues all replacements.
  5. ES will CATN the details and outcome of the replacement request.  

2500.13 Physically Stolen EBT card

  1. 3SVT households that have their card physically stolen are not eligible for replacement benefits. 
  2. Do the following when a household reports their card was physically stolen;
    1. Advise it is a crime, and a police report may be helpful but there is no guarantee they will get their benefits back.
    2. Provide printout of EBT transaction history to the household to supplement the police report.
    3. If cash out, discuss direct deposit.
    4. Discuss possible payee.
    5. Provide tips for EBT security:
      1. Keep EBT card number and PIN a secret from everyone besides people in the 3SVT household.
      2. Check card readers before using them to avoid card skimming.
      3. Shield the PIN pad when entering the PIN.
      4. Do not write the PIN on the card sleeve.
      5. Change the PIN frequently and do not share it.
      6. Lock the card immediately after use on the EBT Edge Website.
      7. Check the EBT account regularly for unauthorized charges.
      8. If a transaction is unexpectedly declined or you receive an error code, contact the EBT Customer Services Helpline to verify the transactions on the account.

1300.1 Overview

  1. Time limited benefits are a required federal policy. Customers who are not exempt must satisfy these requirements to continue receiving 3SVT.

  2. Review the time limited benefits work requirements with households that include customers subject to the time limits. See 3SVT Work Requirements Procedures and Notification. 

1300.2 Time Limited Customer

  1. A customer is time limited if they:
    1. Are at least age 18 and under age 65;
    2. Have no child under 14  in their 3SVT household; and
    3. Are able to work, which means the customer:
      1. Is satisfying the time limited benefits work requirement; or
      2. Does not have any exemptions from the time limited benefits work requirements or general work requirements;
        1. This includes customers who were working/self-employed and lost the general work requirement exemption because:
          1. Their work hours fall below 20 hours per week/80 hours per month;
          2. Their earnings fall below $935.25 per month; and
          3. They have no other general work requirement exemptions. 
  2. A time limited customer is limited to receiving three countable months of benefits within a three-year (36-month) period, unless they:
    1. Are exempt from time limits;
    2. Satisfy the time limited benefit work requirements; or
    3. Qualify for the cure.

1300.3 Exemptions from Time Limit

  1. Exempt the following the people from time limited benefits work requirements. A person who is:
    1. Pregnant. (Code 24)
    2. Exempt from the 3SVT general work requirement for a reason other than age. (Code 25)
    3. Under Age 18 or 65 or older. (Code 25). 
    4. A member of a 3SVT household that includes a child under 14 who is receiving benefits. (Code 27)
      1. One child can exempt the entire household.
      2. Exception: Excluded Foster Children and ineligible members can still give a household this exemption.  
      3. Children under 18 can receive this exemption if they do not meet a general work requirement exemption. 
    5. Temporarily unable to participate in training or employment due to a personal obstacle or barrier. (Code 55)
      1. A personal obstacle or barrier means a physical, mental or emotional situation that prevents an individual from being able to meet the monthly work requirement. 
    6. Individuals who are Indians, Urban Indians, California Indians, and other Indians who are eligible for the Indian Health Services. (Code 60) 
      1. Tribes and Nations eligible for this exemption can be found here: Federally Recognized Indian Tribes in the United States 
  2. A customer does not have a Countable Month when they are exempt. 

1300.4 Time Limit

1300.4.1 Three Countable Month Limitation

  1. Customers with time limited benefits work requirements who do not meet their work requirement are only eligible for three full months (countable months) of 3SVT benefits every three-year period.

  2. The three-year period is the same for all customers in Vermont. The periods are:

    1. November 1, 2025 through October 31, 2028

    2. November 1, 2028 through October 31, 2031

    3. November 1, 2031 through October 31, 2034

  3. The countable months are electronically tracked. When the three-month limit has been used, the customer is no longer eligible for 3SVT benefits. At the end of the three-month limit, the system automatically removes the person or closes the case. See Ineligible Members for treatment of the income and resources of an ineligible customer who is a member of an eligible 3SVT household.

  4. After using their initial three countable months or second three months (the cure), a customer must meet their time limited benefits work requirements to continue to receive 3SVT benefits.

1300.4.2 Countable Month

  1. A month is a countable month unless the household:
    1. receives prorated benefits;
    2. is exempt from time limit; or
    3. meets the work requirement.
  2. Countable months can be removed from the current three-year period if customers provide verification that they were exempt or meeting the work requirement for the received countable month(s). 
  3. When contacting other states for duplicate participation, check for time limit countable months. Out of State Verification of Countable Months

1300.4.3 Time Limited Benefits Work Requirements

1300.4.3.1 Satisfying the Monthly Time Limited Benefits Work Requirements

  1. A time limited customer must meet one of the following work requirements to avoid using a countable month. 
    1. Working a total of 80 hours or more in 30-day period;
      1. Work means employment, self-employment, volunteer work, or working in exchange for In-Kind Benefits .
      2. Participating for a total of 80 hours or more in a 30-day period in:
        1. WIOA activities;
        2. Section 236 of the Trade Act of 1974 activities, which include  TAA  and  TRA ;
        3. Employment and training programs for veterans operated by Department of Labor or Department of Veterans Affairs; or
        4. ICAN.
        5. Note: Ineligible time limited customers cannot use ICAN to meet their TLBWR. 
    2. Workfare participation.
      1. Workfare in volunteering at any public or private non-profit agency. Examples: Church, senior centers, town offices, food shelf, etc.  
      2. Hours are determined by the 3SVT benefit amount divided by the Vermont minimum wage.
        1. Note:  Zero benefit households: $0 benefit = 0 hours required
      3. Households with multiple workfare participants can split hours as they see fit as long as:
        1. Total household workfare hours are met; and
        2. All workfare customers participate. A non-participating customer will receive a countable month.
        3. If the total household workfare hours are not met, all workfare customers in that household receive a countable month. 

1300.4.3.2 Good Cause for Not Meeting the Work Requirement

  1. To qualify for good cause, the customer must:
    1. still retain the job, work program or Workfare site;
    2. plan to continue to participate in the activity that meets the work requirement after the temporary absence; and
    3. have a good reason that is beyond the person’s control such as, but not limited to:
      1. A household emergency
      2. Unavailability of transportation
      3. Illness of the customer or another household member requiring the customer’s presence
  2. ES must refer a case with a customer who seems to meet good cause to the Supervisor. The Supervisor will send the case to AOPS  to decide if good cause is met.
  3. CATN the good cause reason or why that good cause was not accepted.
  4. Any month that a good cause reason is granted is not a countable month.    

1300.4.4.3 Enrolling in ICAN in their Third Countable Month

  1.  Customers enrolling in ICAN in their third countable month may qualify for a temporary exemption to allow time for them to enroll. 
  2.  Customers must complete ICAN orientation in their third countable month to qualify for a temporary exemption. 
  3.  Customers who complete ICAN enrollment may receive up to two additional temporary exemptions (for a maximum of three temporary exemptions) to allow them time to connect to their local ICAN team and complete necessary assessments.
    1.  The local ICAN team will communicate the need for these additional exemptions to the ICAN team at ESD.  The ICAN team will enter the code 15 and CATN. 
    2.  Enrollment means connected to an ICAN partner to complete required paperwork, set up a Vermont Job Links (VJL) account and schedule a meeting with the local employment team.
  4.  Temporary exemptions can only be used once per enrollment. If the customer fails to enroll in ICAN following the application of an exemption they do not qualify for additional exemptions.
  5.  Customers who are not eligible for ICAN are not eligible for temporary exemptions.

1300.4.4 Regaining Eligibility 

  1. A customer who has exhausted their three countable months may regain eligibility at any time. A customer regains eligibility:
    1. By meeting the time limited benefits work requirement for 30 consecutive days;
    2. By meeting an exemption from the time limited benefits work requirement; or
    3. When the 36-month time limit period ends and a new 36-month period begins.
  2. At application, if a customer reports they are exempt or meeting the work requirement request required verification. 
  3. If verification is provided for the exemption, work program hours, or workfare participation, determine if the verification confirms that the customer:
    1. Is exempt, or
    2. Is meeting the work requirement; and 
    3. If they will continue to meet the work requirement.
      1. If verification does not include confirmation of continued participation, determine the work requirement met only for the months verified. 
      2. Update the MEMB panel and process the case including the time limited customer.
  4. If the required verification is not returned within 10 days, consider the person to be time limited and not meeting their work requirement.
    1. Deny or exclude the time limited customer for not meeting their work requirement. 
      1. Exception: If the required verification was for earned income, deny the case for non-cooperation.
  5. Reinstate a time limited customer’s case if they verify they have met the time limited benefits work requirement within 30 days from the date of application. Provide benefits from the date of application.
  6. Time limited customers can be added to an active case at any time if they become exempt or have met their work requirement for 30 days. 
  7. Screen for the cure when a customer:
    1. regains eligibility and then stops meeting their work requirement; or
    2. loses an exemption.

1300.4.4.1 Regaining Eligibility for a Second 3-Month Period (The Cure)

  1. A customer with time limited benefits can be eligible for the cure. The cure is an additional three countable months. To be eligible a customer has to have met the time limited benefits work requirements after receiving their initial three countable months.

  2. These additional countable months must be used consecutively.

  3. Note: The customer does not have to be meeting the time limited benefits work requirements at the time of application. They must have met the requirement sometime after using their initial three countable months.

  4. The three additional countable months are allowed once in the 36-month period. Once started, the additional countable month period must continue uninterrupted. Unused additional months, due to case closure or other reasons, cannot be used at another time.

1300.5 Verification

1300.5.1 Work Requirement Verification

  1. At application and recertification verification is required prior to coding a person as meeting the time limited benefits work requirement.
  2. Allow the household 10 days to provide the required verification.
  3. If the required verification is not returned within 10 days:
    1. Consider the person to be time limited and not meeting their work requirement.  Change the time limited benefits work requirements code to a countable month.
    2. Do not deny or close benefits solely for failure to verify an exemption, work program hours, or workfare participation.
      1. Deny for non-cooperation if verification requested is for earned income and hours.
  4. See Regaining Eligibility for customers with no countable months remaining.

1300.5.1.1 Meeting the Work Requirements Verification 

  1. When a customer is meeting the work requirement, verification could include, but is not limited to: 
    1. Work hours
      1. Verify hours when verifying income.
        1. Collateral Contact
        2. Paystubs
        3. Employment verification form 
      2. Self-employment hours are self-reported by the household.
      3. In-Kind hours must be verified. Monetary value not required. 
        1. Collateral Contact
        2. Statement from individual allowing In-Kind hours. 
      4. Volunteering/unpaid work
        1. Collateral contact
        2. Statement from individual they are working for
    2. Work program participation
      1. Collateral contact
      2. Employment Program Verification Form
      3. Time sheets
      4. Letter from the program
      Workfare Participation 
      1. Collateral contact
      2. Workfare Verification Form
      3. Time sheets
      4. Letter from the work site
  2. If verification is provided for employment, work program, or workfare participation, determine if the verification confirms that the customer:
    1. Is meeting the work requirement; and 
    2. Is expected to continue to meet the work requirement.
      1. If verification does not include confirmation of expected continued participation, only allow it for the months determined by the verification. 
      2. Update the MEMB panel and approve the case including the time limited customer.

1300.5.2 Exemptions that Require Verification

1300.5.2.1 Personal Obstacle or Barrier 

  1. Verification is required prior to coding a person exempt due to a personal obstacle or barrier that prevents the person from meeting the time limited benefits work requirements. 

  2.  If the required verification is not returned within 10 days, keep the customer’s code as 21 (time limited) unless the person meets a different exemption.

  3. Acceptable verification is a statement from a provider who has details on the customer`s situation. Examples: Housing Case Managers, ICAN Case Managers, or Department of Corrections representatives, etc.

1300.5.3 Questionable Information 

  1. Verify any other information that is questionable.

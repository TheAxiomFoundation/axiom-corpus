1200.1 General Work Requirements

1200.1.2 Overview 

  1. Persons applying for, or receiving, 3SVT benefits must meet the general work requirements, unless they are exempt to continue receiving 3SVT benefits.
  2. Review general work requirements with households that include customers who are subject to the requirements. See 3SVT Work Requirements Procedures and Notification.

1200.1.3 The General Work Requirements 

  1. Persons subject to the general work requirements must: 
    1. Not quit a job or reduce hours below 30 per week without good cause if already working;
    2. Give Economic Services Division (ESD) enough information to determine employment status or job availability; and
    3. Accept a job offer unless it is unsuitable.

1200.1.4 General Work Requirement Exemptions 

  1. Exempt the following people from general work requirements. A person who is: 
    1. Younger than 16. (Code 06)
    2. Age 16 or 17 and residing with a parent or with a person with parental control. (Code 06)
    3. A student enrolled at least half-time in a school, training program, or Institution of Higher Education. (Code 06)
      1. This includes adults going to high school, or VAL classes.
      2. The exemption continues during vacations and other breaks if the student intends to register and attend at least half-time for the next school term.
      3. The exemption ends when the student graduates, is suspended or expelled, or drops out.
      4. Note: See Students for eligibility requirements for students enrolled at least half-time in an institution of higher education.
    4. Employed or self-employed and working at least 30 hours per week or receiving gross weekly earnings at least equal to the Federal Minimum Wage multiplied by 30 hours per week. (Code 07)
      1. Fluctuating work hours should be averaged to arrive at a reasonable estimate of hours expected to be worked in a week.
      2. Volunteer hours or hours worked for In-Kind Benefits are not included in the 30 hours per week calculation.
      3. Includes migrant or seasonal farm workers who are under contract (or similar agreement) with an employer or crew chief to start work within 30 days.
    5. Age 60 or older. (Code 08)
      1. A person is exempt the month they turn 60.
    6. Responsible for the care of a dependent child under the age of 6. Note: Verification required. (Code 09)
      1. The household must report that the parent or caretaker is providing the care to receive the exemption.
      2. Excluded Foster Children and ineligible members can still give a household this exemption.  
      3. Only one parent or caretaker per child under 6 may receive this exemption.
      4. For households with multiple children under 6, the household must report which adult is providing care to each child(ren).
      5. If the child turns 6 in the certification period, the person’s exemption remains until the next recertification.
    7. Receiving or has applied for (UCB). (Code 10)
    8. Responsible for the care of another person who needs help caring for themselves. Note: Verification required. (Code 11)
      1. The individuals providing and receiving care do not need to live in the same household.
    9. Physically or mentally unable to work. Note: Verification required.  (Code 12)
    10. Receiving Reach First, Reach Up, Post-Secondary Education or Reach Ahead benefits. (Code 14)
    11. A regular participant in a drug or alcohol addiction program. (Code 16)
      1. Participation can be either as a resident of the treatment center or an outpatient basis. Among others, the following meet this exemption:
        1. Participants in the Department of Corrections ISAP and CBISA programs.
        2. Residents of Teen Challenge.
        3. Persons treated through methadone clinics.

1200.2 Violations of the General Work Requirements

1200.2.1 Voluntary Quit or reduction in hours

  1. Customers subject to the general work requirements must not voluntarily: 
    1. Quit a job, or
    2. Reduce work hours.

1200.2.2 Job Quit

  1. A customer voluntarily quit a job if:
    1. The customer worked at least 30 hours a week, or the job provided weekly earnings equal to the federal minimum wage times 30 hours; and
    2. The quit/job termination occurred within 60 days of the application, or anytime thereafter; and
    3. The reason for the job termination was caused by the customer.
      1. Participation in a strike by a Federal, State, or local government employee is a voluntary quit without good cause.
  2. A customer did not voluntarily quit a job if:
    1. The stopped job was a self-employment business.
    2. The customer resigned from the job at the employer’s request for reasons that are beyond the person’s control, such as unable to complete the required work assignments.
    3. The customer did not reenlist in the military.
  3. Apply disqualification penalties if the customer voluntarily quit a job without good cause.
  4. Note: Voluntary quit applies to a person whose only exemption from the general work requirements was employment of at least 30 hours a week (or equivalent earnings).

1200.2.3 Reduced Work Hours

  1. A customer voluntarily reduced their work hours if:
    1. The hours were reduced to less than 30 hours a week; or 
    2. The hours were reduced, and the new weekly earnings are below the federal minimum wage times 30 hours; and 
    3. The reduction was requested by the employee, not the employer; and
    4. The reduction occurred within 60 days of the application, or anytime thereafter.
  2. A customer did not voluntarily reduce work hours to less than 30 hours a week if the reduction in hours is temporary for reasons such as illness, vacation or personal business.
  3. Apply disqualification penalties if the customer voluntarily quit a job without good cause.
  4. Note: Voluntary reduction in work hours applies to a person whose only exemption from the general work requirements was employment of at least 30 hours a week (or equivalent earnings).

1200.2.4 Good Cause 

  1. Customers have good cause for quitting a job or reducing work hours if:
    1. There were circumstances beyond the customer’s control. Examples include, but are not limited to:
      1. Illness of the customer or another household member requiring the customer’s presence
      2. Household emergency
    2. The job was unsuitable.
    3. There was discrimination by an employer based on:
      1. age;
      2. race;
      3. sex (including gender identity and sexual orientation);
      4. color;
      5. disability;
      6. marital status;
      7. family/parental status;
      8. income derived from public assistance programs;
      9. religious beliefs
      10. national origin; or
      11. political beliefs
    4. Work demands or conditions make it unreasonable to continue employment, such as working without being paid on schedule.
    5. They accepted another job of at least 30 hours per week (or equivalent) or enrolled in school at least half-time.
    6. Another household member accepted a job or enrolled in school at least half-time in another county or state, causing the household to move.
    7. They are under age 60, retire, and the employer recognizes it as retirement.
    8. They accepted a job offer that provides at least 30 hours a week, or weekly earnings at least equal to federal minimum wage times 30 hours, and that job did not materialize or meet the hourly criteria or weekly earnings through no fault of their own.
    9. They leave a job that requires frequent moves, such as  Migrant Worker or  Seasonal Farmworker.
    10. When adequate dependent care is reported as unavailable for children at least six and under 12.
      1. Record that a provider could not be located and do not sanction for voluntary quit. 
    11. The job quit does not meet the definition of voluntary job quit or reduced work hours.
  2. Always consider all the facts when good cause is claimed.  CATN the person’s claim of good cause identifying the facts and why a disqualification was imposed or not imposed. 

1200.2.5 Unsuitable Employment

  1. Do not impose a voluntary quit or reduction-in-job-hours disqualification if the job meets one of the following:
    1. The wage offered is less than:
      1. The applicable federal or  Vermont Minimum Wage - For the current Vermont minimum wage click here (whichever is higher), or
      2. 80 percent of the federal minimum wage, if neither the federal nor Vermont minimum wage is applicable.
    2. The job is on a piece-rate basis and the average hourly earnings are less than the hourly wage as defined above.
    3. The person must join, resign from, or refrain from joining any legitimate labor organization to get or keep the job.
    4. The job offered is at a site currently subject to a strike or lockout, unless:
      1. the strike has been enjoined under Section 208 of the Labor-Management Relations Act (commonly known as the Taft-Hartley Act), or 
      2. an injunction has been issued under Section 10 of the Railway Labor Act.
    5. Daily commuting time exceeds 2 hours a day, not including taking a child to and from childcare. Note: When a household move results in more than 2 hours commuting time, the job is unsuitable. This includes moves within the state as well as from state to state. This applies whether a person leaves a job before or after the move.
    6. The distance to the place of employment is too far to walk, and neither public nor private transportation is available to get the person to the job site.
    7. The person is physically or mentally unfit to perform the job.  Note: Verification is required.
    8. The job is offered within the first 30 days of registration and is not in the person’s major field of experience.
    9. The working hours or nature of the job interferes with the person’s religious observations, convictions, or beliefs.  

1200.3 Disqualification

1200.3.1 Disqualification Determination

  1. Disqualify a customer when they:
    1. Does not provide employment status or availability for work information, upon request;
    2. Does not accept a job offer unless the job is unsuitable;
    3. Voluntary quits a job without good cause; or
    4. Voluntarily reduces hours of employment without good cause.

    1200.3.2 Disqualification Periods

    1. The customer is ineligible to participate in 3SVT as follows:
      1. For the first violation: 1 month or until the person complies, whichever is later.
      2. For the second violation: 3 months or until the person complies, whichever is later.
      3. For the third and subsequent violations: 6 months or until the person complies, whichever is later.
    2. Note: The disqualification penalty follows the disqualified person if they join a new household.

    1200.3.3 Ending the Disqualification

    1. The disqualified person can become eligible again by:
      1. Becoming exempt from the General Work Requirements; or 
      2. Serving the minimum disqualification period and complying with the failed requirement. If the person has not complied with the failed requirement by the time the minimum disqualification period ends, the disqualification remains in effect until the person does comply with the requirement. 
    2. Exception: Persons disqualified for voluntary quit or a reduction in work hours may reapply after the end of the disqualification period. 
    3. The household must reapply if the 3SVT case was closed.

1200.4 Verification and Documentation 

1200.4.1 Exemptions that Require Verification

  1. Verification and documentation are required prior to coding a person exempt from work registration for the following exemptions. If the verification is not returned within 10 days, keep the work registration code as “02” unless the person meets a different exemption. Do not deny or close benefits for failure to verify an exemption. 

1200.4.1.1 Responsible for the Care of a Dependent Child Under Age of Six 

  1. The household must report that the parent or caretaker is providing the care to receive the exemption.  The following must be asked and noted in CATN: 
    1. Which adult(s) in your household is providing care for the child(ren) under 6? 
      1. CATN must include which adult is providing care. If the household has multiple children under 6 the adult providing care for each child must be noted. 
      2. If an individual is not providing care to the child under 6 then they are not eligible for this exemption. 

      1200.4.1.2 Responsible for the Care of Another person

      1. A statement from a  Qualified Health Professional regarding who is being cared for and who is needed to provide the care.  Exception: No verification is needed for a designated  EP on an active essential person grant.

      1200.4.1.3 Physically or Mentally Unfit for Work 

      1. To be deemed physically or mentally unfit for work the following can be used as verification:
        1. Meet the definition of  DisabledDisabled.

          A person who meets one or more of the following:

          • Receives disability or blindness benefits from any of these programs: Social Security,   SSI, or   MABD.
            • A person meets this even when the full benefit is withheld or deferred due to recoupment.
          • Receives a federally or state administered SSI supplement based on disability or blindness, or section 212(a) of PL 93-66.
          • Receives a disability retirement benefit from a government agency for a   disability considered permanent by SSAdisability considered permanent by SSA.

            Any of the following conditions:

            • Permanent loss of use of both hands, both feet, or one hand and one foot.
            • Amputation of leg at hip. Amputation of leg or foot because of diabetes or peripheral vascular diseases.
            • Total deafness, not correctable by surgery or hearing aid. Statutory blindness, unless caused by cataracts or detached retina.
            • IQ of 59 or less, established after the person becomes 16 years old.
            • Spinal cord or nerve root lesions resulting in paraplegia or quadriplegia.
            • Multiple sclerosis with damage to the nervous system caused by scattered areas of inflammation. The inflammation recurs and has progressed to varied interference with the function of the nervous system, including severe muscle weakness, paralysis, and vision and speech defects.
            • Muscular dystrophy with irreversible wasting of the muscles, impairing the ability to use the arms or legs.
            • Impaired renal function caused by chronic renal disease, resulting in severely reduced function which may require dialysis or kidney transplant.
            • Amputation of a limb of a person at least 55 years old.
          • Is a veteran the VA considers totally (100%) disabled or permanently housebound or in need of regular aid and attendance.
          • Is a veteran’s surviving spouse who the VA considers:
            • in need of regular aid and attendance, or
            • permanently housebound, or
            • approved for benefits because of the veteran’s death and has a disability considered permanent by SSA.
          • Is a veteran’s surviving child who the VA considers:
            • incapable of self-support, or
            • approved for benefits because of the veteran’s death and has a disability considered permanent by SSA
        2. A statement from a  Qualified Health Professional.
        3. Receipt of VA disability compensation regardless of the individual’s disability percentage rating. 
        4. Receipt of temporary disability income from any agency.
        5. Pending SSI Application.

      1200.4.1.4 Participant in Drug and Alcohol Treatment 

      1. The household must report if an individual is a regular participant in drug and alcohol treatment. The following must be in CATN to allow the exemption: 
        1. Customer’s statements regarding participation including the name of the treatment program. 

      1200.4.2 Questionable Information 

      1. Verify any other information that is questionable.

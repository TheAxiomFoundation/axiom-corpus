800.1 Overview

  1. A non-citizen is anyone living in the United States who does not meet the  Citizen requirements. Citizenship/non-citizenship status is attested to for each member of the household when the citizen question is completed on the 3SVT form. 
  2. A non-citizen must be an eligible non-citizen to receive 3SVT benefits. If members do not meet the eligible non-citizen criteria, they are ineligible members. See Ineligible Members.

800.2 Citizen

800.2.1 Eligibility

  1. People who are United States citizens are eligible for food assistance.

800.2.2 Natural Born Citizens

  1. United States citizens are people born in one of the 50 states, the District of Columbia, Puerto Rico, Guam, the U.S. Virgin Islands, or the Northern Mariana Islands. Non-citizen nationals born in American Samoa or Swain’s Island are also U.S. citizens. 

800.2.3 Naturalized Citizens 

  1.  Naturalized Citizens are United States citizens. Children of naturalized citizens are eligible if the child was;
    1. under age 18 when the parent was naturalized on or after 10/14/1940 or
    2. under age 21 when the parent was naturalized before 10/14/1940.

800.2.4 Natural or Adopted Children of a Citizen

  1. Automatic U.S. citizenship is granted to children born in a foreign country upon entry to the U.S. if the child is:
    1. under 18 years of age, 
    2. admitted to the U.S. as a lawful permanent resident, and
    3. in the legal and physical custody (as a natural or adopted child) of at least one parent who is a U.S. citizen. 
  2. Note: The adoption must be finalized prior to the child entering the U.S.

800.4 Ineligible Non-Citizen

800.4.1 Determination of Eligibility

  1. Non-citizens who are not identified as eligible non-citizens are ineligible non-citizens for 3SVT benefits. 
  2. If a person indicates inability or unwillingness to attest to the non-citizen status for any household member, the person whose non-citizen status is in question is an ineligible (not illegal) non-citizen. If the person is determined ineligible, no further action to obtain documentation of their status is allowed. The household is required to answer questions and submit verifications about the ineligible member’s income, resources, etc. per Ineligible Members.

800.4.2 Reporting Known Illegal Non-Citizen

  1. Report known illegal non-citizens who apply for 3SVT benefits to the  USCIS. “Illegal status” is known only when a formal determination, subject to administrative review, was completed and is supported by the Department of Homeland Security or the Executive Office for Immigration Review, such as a Final Order of Deportation.
  2. If there is not a formal determination, do not report the non-citizen as an illegal non-citizen. The non-citizen is ineligible for 3SVT benefits.

800.5 Sponsored Non-Citizen

800.5.1 Sponsor Deeming Definition

  1. Non-citizens may have been granted a permanent resident status to enter and reside in the United States because a  Sponsor has signed an agreement to guarantee financial support of the non-citizen. Counting the sponsor’s income and resources toward the sponsored non-citizen is called deeming. Deeming may be required for non-citizens who had a sponsor sign the  Affidavit of Support, Form I-864 December 19, 1997 or later. The I-864 form is also known as a 213A affidavit.

800.5.2 Non-Citizen Exempt from Sponsor Deeming

  1. Some non-citizens are exempt from sponsor deeming even if an Affidavit of Support was signed on their behalf. Non-citizens exempt from sponsor deeming are:
    1. Children under age 18.
    2. Refugee, parolee, asylee, Cuban or Haitian entrant, persons granted withholding of deportation, Amerasian, trafficking victims, and Afghan/Iraqi special immigrants.
    3. Non-citizens sponsored by organizations or groups.
    4. Non-citizen is residing in the same house as the sponsor because they are one 3SVT household.
    5. Non-citizens whose sponsor signed the Affidavit of Support, Form I-134, prior to 12/19/1997.
    6. Lawful permanent residents credited with 40 qualifying work quarters. See Work Quarter Eligibility.
    7. Ineligible non-citizens. 
    8. Battered non-citizen, parent of a battered child, or spouse of a battered non-citizen for 12 months. At the end of 12 months, the exemption continues if a court or USCIS determines the battering is substantially connected to the need for benefits. The non-citizen cannot live with the batterer during the exemption period.
    9. Indigent non-citizens: A non-citizen is indigent if the total of the non-citizen’s income and any cash assistance or in-kind support provided by the sponsor is less than 130% of the federal poverty level.
      1. If the non-citizen is eligible for 3SVT because his total income is under 130%, sponsor deeming is not required unless the non-citizen chooses to opt out of indigence determined and be subject to regular sponsor deeming rules.
      2. The indigence exclusion is for one year and then must be re-evaluated.
      3. Written notification of indigence determinations must be sent to the USCIS, Office of Policy and Strategy, 20 Massachusetts Avenue, N.W., Washington DE 20529. The notification must contain the name of the sponsored non-citizen and of the sponsor. It should reference “Determination under 421(e) of the Personal Responsibility and Work Opportunity Reconciliation Act of 1996”.

800.5.3 Sponsor Deeming

800.5.3.1 Sponsor Deeming Procedures

  1. Deem a portion of the sponsor’s income and resources to the non-citizen, regardless of whether the income or resources are paid to the non-citizen. If the sponsor is living with a spouse, the spouse’s income and resources are also deemed to the non-citizen. More than one person may sponsor a non-citizen by signing an Affidavit for Support. If there are joint sponsors, count the deemed income and resources of all sponsors for the non-citizen.
  2. It is the non-citizen’s responsibility to provide information or documentation about the sponsor(s) and to obtain the cooperation from the sponsor.  Information must be provided at application and recertification. Allow the non-citizen at least 10 days to provide the information up to the 30th day for application processing. If it is not provided within that time frame, consider the sponsored non-citizens as ineligible non-citizens and determine eligibility and benefits for the remaining members.

800.5.3.2 Deeming of Sponsor’s Resources

  1. The deemed resource amount is determined as follows:
    1. Add allowable resources
    2. Subtract $1,500
    3. Divide by the number of non-citizens sponsored. If the number is not known, the entire amount counts
    4. The result is the deemed resource amount per sponsored member
    5. No resources are deemed to children under age 18

800.5.3.3 Deeming of Sponsor’s Income

  1. The deemed income amount is determined as follows:
    1. Add earned income
    2. Subtract 20% of the earned income
    3. Add unearned income
    4. Subtract the gross monthly income limit for the sponsor’s household size and any other person who is claimed or could be claimed as a dependent for federal income tax purposes
    5. Divide by the number of non-citizens sponsored. If the number is not known, the entire amount counts
    6. The result is the deemed income amount per sponsored member.
    7. No income is deemed to children under age 18
  2. Money actually paid to the non-citizen by a sponsor is not income to the non-citizen unless the amount paid exceeds the deemed amount. Consider any excess paid as an unearned income contribution to the non-citizen.

800.5.3.4 Expiration of Sponsor Deeming

  1. Deeming of income and resources ends when:
    1. The non-citizen becomes exempt from deeming requirements;
    2. The non-citizen gains U.S. citizenship; or
    3. The sponsor’s death. 

800.6 Verification

800.6.1 Verification Requirements

  1. If verification is required and not provided on a timely basis, determine the eligibility of the remaining members. Budget the income, resources, and expenses of the ineligible member as per Ineligible Members.

800.6.2 Citizenship

  1. Verify if questionable.

800.6.3 Eligible Non-Citizen

  1. Verification is required to determine eligible non-citizen status before approving or adding a non-citizen to 3SVT benefits. After the USCIS documentation is received, use the  SAVE system to verify the documentation is accurate. Exception: American Indians may not be listed on SAVE. Use the  SAVE system at:
    1. initial application;
    2. when adding a non-citizen; and
    3. at recertification.
  2. Note: Run  SAVE at recertification even if the customer does not report a change in immigration status.

800.6.4 Military Requirement

  1. Verification of the active duty or veteran status is required. Veteran status verification may be a discharge certificate (DD Form 214 or the equivalent) and character of Honorable discharge. Active duty verification may be a current Military Identification Card (DD Form 2 Active) or a copy of current military orders. If the person does not have either of these, refer them to the  VA.

800.6.5 Ineligible Non-Citizen

  1. Do not request verification/documentation of non-citizen status for persons who are not participating in the program. This is true whether the person claims to be lawfully or illegally present in the U.S.

800.6.6 Sponsored Non-Citizen

  1. Verification is required for the sponsor (and spouse if appropriate) as follows:
    1. Income and resources 
    2. Name, address, and phone number
  2. Exception: If the non-citizen is categorically eligible, verification of sponsor information may be waived and the information from the categorical eligible program is used (Example: Reach Up).
  3. If the information and/or verification is not provided, the non-citizen is not eligible and their income, resources and expenses are budgeted as per Ineligible Members.

800.6.7 Work Quarter Eligibility 

  1. Verification of qualifying work quarters is required. To obtain current verification, the person may use W-2s, income tax forms, wage stubs, etc. Verification may also be obtained through the  SVES. Prior to requesting the information from SVES, a release form, Consent for Release of Information (Form SSA-3288), must be signed by the person with earnings. If the information from SVES is incomplete, incorrect, or discrepant, or the non-citizen cannot get the release form signed, contact the SSA for assistance.
  2. See Determining Qualifying Quarters for the amount of earnings needed for each quarter.

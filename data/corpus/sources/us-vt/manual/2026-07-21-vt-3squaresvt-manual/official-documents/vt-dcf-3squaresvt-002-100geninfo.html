100.1 3SquaresVT Purpose

  1. The Food and Nutrition Act of 2008, as amended, authorized  SNAP to help low income persons and families stretch their food budget to put three healthy meals on the table every day.
  2. The federal program is known in Vermont as the 3SquaresVT  3SVT program.

100.2 State Agency Responsibilities

  1. The U.S. Department of Agriculture’s  FNS delegates to the Vermont  DCF the responsibility for the administration of the 3SVT program within the state. These responsibilities include, but are not limited to:
    1. Approval of applicant households in compliance with federal regulations.
    2. Issuance, control and accountability of 3SVT benefits.
    3. Processing recipient claims and credits for lost benefits.
    4. Ensuring the civil rights of applicants/recipients by:
      1. maintaining the confidentiality of records;
      2. providing notice of eligibility or ineligibility;
      3. providing notice of all adverse action;
      4. providing information about and assistance in filing a request for a fair hearing, including an explanation of continuation of benefits; and
      5. providing equal application of all standards without regard to age, race, color, sex, disability, religion, national origin, political belief or limited English proficiency.
    5. Program information activities.
    6. Developing and maintaining complaint procedures.
    7. Developing, conducting and evaluating training.
    8. Conducting performance reporting reviews such as quality control and management evaluation.
    9. Submitting accurate and timely financial and program reports.
    10. Keeping records necessary to determine whether the program is conducted in compliance with federal regulations.

100.3 Confidentiality

100.3.1 Disclosure Summary

  1. 3SVT case information is confidential under Federal and State law. Use or disclosure of information for 3SVT case records is restricted to the following people who must adequately protect the information against unauthorized disclosure.

100.3.2 Administrative Disclosure

  1. People directly connected with the administration of:
    1.  3SVT
    2.  Reach Up,  Reach Ahead, and  Reach First
    3. Medicaid 
    4. Any state program administered under a plan approved under the Social Security Act, which is required to participate in  IEVS
    5. Social Security  RSDI 
    6.  SSI
    7.  CS
    8. Food Distribution programs
    9. The National School Lunch Program for the purpose of approving eligibility of children for free meals based on their receipt of 3SVT
    10. Any other federally aided means-tested program
  2. Note: Release of information is limited to the extent that the information is useful in establishing or verifying eligibility or benefit amounts under those programs. In addition,
    1. Persons connected with the verification of immigration status of non-citizens through  SAVE.
    2. Employees of the United State Comptroller General’s Office, upon written request, for audit purposes.
    3. Any local, state, or federal law enforcement official, upon a written request, for the purpose of investigating an alleged violation of the 3SVT program. The request must include:
      1. the person’s name;
      2. violation being investigated; and 
      3. identity and authority of person making the request.
    4. Any local, state, or federal law enforcement official, upon a written request, for purpose of investigation of a felony crime or a probation or parole violation.  The information that can be provided is limited to the address, social security number and any available photograph of:
      1. a 3SVT recipient who is fleeing to avoid prosecution, custody, or confinement for a felony crime or a violation of parole or probation; or
      2. a household member who has information about another household member who is under investigation for a felony crime or probation or parole violation.

100.3.3 Household Access

  1. 3SVT household members may review materials contained in their case file during normal business hours, if a written request is received from:
    1. A responsible member of the household.
    2. A current authorized representative.
    3. Any person authorized in writing to act on the household’s behalf. 
  2. Confidential information may be withheld such as the names of people who have disclosed information about the household without the household's knowledge, or the nature or status of pending criminal prosecutions.

100.4 Non-Discrimination

100.4.1 Program Administration Requirements

  1. A 3SVT applicant or recipient must not be discriminated against in any aspect of program administration including, but not limited to:
    1. Approval
    2. Issuance of benefits
    3. Conduct of fair hearings 
    4. Conduct of any other program service
  2. 3SVT benefits must be extended to all eligible households without regard to age, race, color, sex (including gender identity and sexual orientation), disability, religious creed, national origin, political beliefs or limited English proficiency.  
  3. All offices administering the 3SVT program must also prominently display the poster “And Justice for All” provided by the  USDA. 

100.4.2 Right to File a Complaint

  1. Persons or agencies desiring to file a complaint alleging discrimination on the basis of age, race, color, sex (including gender identity and sexual orientation), disability, religious creed, national origin, political belief or limited English proficiency may contact:
    1. USDA, Director, Office of Civil Rights, Room 326-W, Whitten Building, 1400 Independence Avenue, SW, Washington, DC 20250-9410; or call (202) 720-5964 (voice and TDD) and/or
    2. Commissioner’s Office, Consumer Concerns Team, 280 State Drive, Waterbury, Vermont 05671-1080
  2. If a person expresses an interest in filing a discrimination complaint:
    1. Explain both the FNS and the State of Vermont nondiscrimination complaint systems. 
    2. Explain what information is necessary for investigation. 

100.4.3 Discrimination Complaint Requirements

  1. Discrimination complaints must be filed not later than 180 days from the date of the alleged discrimination. The complaint must contain the following information:
    1. The name, address, and phone number of the person alleging discrimination, or other means of contacting this person.
    2. The location and name of the office which is accused of discriminatory action.
    3. The nature of the incident, action, or program administration that led to the complaint.
    4. The reason for the alleged discrimination (age, race, color, sex (including gender identity and sexual orientation), disability, religious creed, national origin, political belief or limited English proficiency).
    5. The names, titles (if appropriate), and addresses of persons who may have knowledge of the alleged discriminatory act.
    6. Each date on which the action occurred.

100.4.4 Verbal Discrimination Complaints

  1. If a person makes verbal accusations to a DCF employee and is unable or reluctant to put the allegations in writing, the employee must ask the person to contact FNS directly.  If the person is unwilling to do so, the employee must document the complaint and forward it to the Director, Office of Civil Rights.

100.5 Authorized Representatives

100.5.1 AR Procedures

  1. Households note in writing the name of the person they have chosen as an  AR to act on behalf of the household on a 139REP or 202. If identified on the 202, a 139REP must be sent to the household. A written signature is required for all ARs; electronic signatures are not allowed.
  2. Households can have multiple ARs at a time. ARs must be:
    1. an adult,
    2. not a member of the household, and
    3. aware of the household’s situation.
  3. ARs complete the following actions when acting for the household:
    1. apply for 3SVT benefits for the household, 
    2. complete 3SVT Work Requirements screening, 
    3. report for the household, and 
    4. access the benefits to purchase allowable food for the household.
  4. ARs must verify their identity when applying on behalf of a household.
  5. ARs are not issued their own  EBT card. ARs use the EBT card issued to the household.  
  6. ARs are not limited to the number of households they can represent.
  7. See, Addiction Treatment Centers and Group Living for the Disabled for AR requirements related to these households.

100.5.2 AR Restrictions

  1. Restrictions apply to who can be an assigned as an AR.
    1. People disqualified for  IPV may not be an AR for the household unless there is no other adult in the household and a new AR can’t be assigned. 
      1. Note: Other excluded persons may be an AR for the household if the person is an adult with knowledge of the household’s situation.
      2. If a household reports their AR is disqualified, the ES must ask if another adult can act as the AR. If yes, obtain a new written statement. If no, the disqualified AR can remain. 
    2. Retailers who accept 3SVT or agency employees who approve or issue benefits may only act as an AR if:
      1. approved in writing by the agency director, and
      2. no one else is available to serve as AR.
    3. Homeless meal providers are not eligible to act as an AR for a homeless household.
    4. Organizations cannot be an AR. Households must name an individual at the organization as their AR. 

100.5.3 Disqualifications

  1. People are disqualified from acting as an AR for up to one year when the agency determines the AR:
    1. Lied about the household’s situation
    2. Knowingly provided false information about the household
    3. Used the benefits improperly
  2. Exception: ARs for group living for the disabled or addiction treatment centers are not disqualified.
  3. Notify the household and the AR with advance notice when the AR is disqualified. Include in the notice:
    1. name and telephone number to contact for questions,
    2. fair hearing rights,
    3. the reason for the action, and
    4. the action that will be taken.

100.5.4 Overpayments

  1. Households with an AR are responsible for any overpayments due to information the AR provided.
  2. Exception: Households are not responsible for overpayments caused by an AR assigned by an addiction treatment center.

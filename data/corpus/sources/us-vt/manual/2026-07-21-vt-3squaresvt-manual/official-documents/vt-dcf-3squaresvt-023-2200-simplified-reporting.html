2200.1 Overview

  1. All 3SquaresVT and 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households have simplified reporting requirements. SR households are required to report fewer changes during their certification period than other programs. Simplified reporting has two parts:
    1. Reduced reporting, and
    2. Interim report. 
  2. 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households are subject to their own simplified reporting requirements. See 3SquaresVT in a SNAP Simplified Reporting. 

2200.2 SR Requirement Notification

  1. Provide the following reporting requirement information to the household, both verbally and in writing at application and recertification.
    1. How SR works
    2. Reporting requirements, including:
      1. What to report
      2. What to verify
      3. When the ESD 202IR, Interim Report is due
      4. How to get assistance
      5. What happens when the ESD 202IR isn’t completed and returned
    3. Help is available to complete and file the ESD 202IR for  disableddisabled or non-English speaking households.

2200.4 Gross Income Reporting Requirement After Exceeding 130% FPL

  1. Households that report when their gross household income goes over 130% FPL, and who remain eligible for 3SVT benefits after benefits are adjusted, have met their 130% gross income reporting requirement. The household does not have to report their income changes again until the interim report or the next recertification, whichever comes first. 
  2. Note: Households with income over 130% FPL at application, who are found eligible do not have a reporting requirement until Interim Report.

2200.5 Certification Periods

  1. See Certification Periods.

2200.6 Interim Report (IR)

2200.6.1 Interim Report Requirement

  1. Households must complete form ESD 202IR once during the certification period to report and verify specific changes. Exception: 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households do not receive interim reports.
  2. See Conversion from 3SquaresVT to 3SquaresVT in a SNAP.
  3. See Conversion from 3SquaresVT in a SNAP to 3SquaresVT.

2200.6.2 Interim Report Reporting Requirements

  1. Households must report:
    1. When Residency changes.
    2. When Shelter Expenses change.
    3. When Household Composition changes.
    4. When there is a change in employment that results in a change in income.
      1. Note: A change in earned income does not need to be reported unless due to a change in employment or the income change meets the reduced reporting requirements.
    5. When Unearned Income changes by more than $100. 
    6. When Resources or Vehicles change.
      1. Note: Categorically eligible households do not need to report resources. 
    7. When a time limited customers work hours are less than 80 hours per month (20 hours per week averaged monthly).

2200.6.3 Timeframes

2200.6.3.1 ACCESS Automatic Processing

  1. ACCESS automatically generates an ESD 202IR list for each district on the 15th of the month before the household’s ESD 202IR is due. The ESD 202IR is printed and sent from the  ADPC.

2200.6.3.2 12-Month Certification Periods 

  1. SR households with 12-month certification periods receive their ESD 202IR and a self-addressed postage paid return envelope during the 5th month of their certification period.  The complete report and required verifications must be returned by the 2nd day of the 6th month. 

2200.6.3.3 24-Month Certification Periods

  1. SR households with 24-month certification periods receive their ESD 202IR and a self-addressed postage paid return envelope during the 11th month of their certification period.  The complete report and required verifications must be returned by the 2nd day of 12th month.

2200.6.3.4 36-Month Certification Periods

  1. 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households do not receive interim reports. 
  2. 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households that have converted to 3SVT are SR households. SR households with 36-month certification periods receive their ESD 202IR and a self-addressed postage paid return envelope in six-month intervals. See chart below.

2200.6.4 Complete Interim Report

  1. ESD 202IR is complete when:
    1. each question is answered,
    2. the form is signed and dated, and
      1. Note: All electronic signatures are allowed.
    3. required verifications are provided.
  2. Reports are complete if the only missing verification is required for an expense. If verification is not received do not allow the expense.  
  3. Process reported changes to take effect the month after the report month. 
  4. Notify the household of the effect of the change. Adequate notice of adverse action is used for decreases.  
  5. Code ACCESS with a “C” showing the ESD 202IR is complete.  
  6. Note: The report is complete for 3SVT purposes if Reach Up and Reach Ahead participants do not provide proof of unchanged income.

2200.6.6 Incomplete Interim Report 

2200.6.6.1 Processing Incomplete IR

  1. Code ACCESS with an “I” to show the report is incomplete when the household fails to provide a complete ESD 202IR.  Enter any verified changes in ACCESS. 
  2. Within 10 days of the date the IR was due, create an ESD 202RL2, Verification Review Letter advising the household:
    1. that the case will end at the end of the month if the missing information and/or verification is not received by the due date, 
    2. what information or verification is missing, and
    3. the due date to provide the missing information and/or verification.  
  3. ESD 202RL2 is due within ten days or by the last day of the month, whichever is sooner.
  4. Any case with an “I” in ACCESS on the IR due date will be sent an IR Reminder Notice advising the household they have 10 days to provide all required verification or benefits will close. 

2200.6.6.2 Household Completes the Process

  1. Code ACCESS with a “C” for complete when the missing information and/or verifications are returned.  Process the ESD 202IR within ten days from when the complete ESD 202IR is returned.  
  2. Determine eligibility and reinstate the case when the missing information or verification is received after the  Adverse Action but before the last day of the report month.
  3. Process reported changes to take effect the month after the report month. 
  4. Notify the household of the effect of the change. Adequate notice of adverse action is used for decreases. See Change Processing, Notification. 

2200.6.6.3 Household Fails to Complete the Process

  1. Households failing to complete missing information and/or provide verifications by the ESD 202RL2 due date are sent form ESD IR NOAA, Interim Report Notice of Adverse Action.   
  2. ACCESS will close the 3SVT case at adverse action if still coded with an “I”.
  3. Information or verifications received in the month following the month of IR closure are too late, and the household must reapply. 

2200.6.7 No Interim Report

  1. Households failing to provide an ESD 202IR are coded by ACCESS with an “S” to show the report has not been returned by the  2nd day of the report month.  Households are sent an IR Reminder Notice on the 3rd by ACCESS advising their case will close unless they complete and return the ESD 202IR and required verifications by the due date shown on the notice.   
  2. ACCESS will close the 3SVT case at adverse action if still coded with an “S”.

2200.7 Verification

  1. See Item relating to the reported change.  
  2. When verification is required, the ESD 202RL2 is due within ten days or by the last day of the month, whichever is sooner.

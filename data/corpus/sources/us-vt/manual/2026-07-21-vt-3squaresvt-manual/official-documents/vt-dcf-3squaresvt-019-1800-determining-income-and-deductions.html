1800.1 Procedures to Determine Monthly Benefit Amount

  1. Determining eligibility is a two-step process:
    1. Determine if the household meets the program rules; and
    2. If eligible, determine the benefit amount.
  2. Determine eligibility for the entire month using the best estimate of income, expenses, and information available for that month. 
  3. The method used to calculate a monthly amount depends on knowing the following information for each income and expense source:
    1. when started;
    2. how often received;
    3. period of time intended for;
    4. when received; and
    5. known future changes.
  4. Once known, use the information to budget current monthly circumstances and to identify future changes, if any. 

1800.2 Countable Income for the month of application

  1. Count income as follows:
    1. The actual income the household received for the 30 days prior to the date of application.
      1. Note: The income is budgeted if received in the previous 30 days even if it is does not reflect ongoing income. 
    2. Income received less often than monthly is averaged over the period the income is intended to cover. See Income Averaging.
    3. Income that stops in the application month is budgeted as the actual amount that will be received in that calendar month. Process a change for the following month to reflect zero income from that source.
    4. Income that starts in the application month is budgeted as the actual amount that will be received in that calendar month. Process a change in the following month to reflect a full month's income from that source. 
  2. See Migrants and Seasonal Farmworkers, Income for special month of application income treatment for Destitute migrant or seasonal farmworker households.

1800.3 Expenses for the Month of Application

  1. Count allowable expenses that are incurred or anticipated to be incurred during the application month. Note: Recurring expenses billed less often than monthly are allowed if the expense will be billed in the benefit period.  See Averaging Expenses.

1800.4 Anticipating Income

1800.4.1 Budgeting Income

  1. Budget income that household members are reasonably certain to receive. Do not budget income if the amount or receipt date is uncertain. 
  2. Non-recurring lump sum income is counted as a resource, not as income.

1800.4.2 Income Only in the Month Received

  1. Count income only in the month it is anticipated to be received, unless the income is averaged. 
  2. Count income that stops until it is no longer received. Remove the income the following month. 

1800.4.3 Wages Held by the Employer

  1. Do not count wages withheld by the employer unless the wages were withheld at the request of the employee.
  2. If wages are paid in advance, count only if they can be reasonably anticipated.

1800.4.4 No Change of Month 

  1. Income received monthly or semi-monthly is counted for the month it was intended when the income is received in a different month because of mailing cycles, holidays, or weekends. 
  2. Example: Income is paid on the 1st of the month except when the 1st falls on a weekend or holiday, then it is received on the previous workday. Count the income as received on the 1st day of the month. 

1800.5 Determining Monthly Income

1800.5.1 Estimating Income

  1. Determine income for the month using the best estimate of income available to arrive at a representative amount. 

1800.5.2 Representative Income

  1. Income amount that is within the range of what would be the typical or usual amount of the household’s income. Income that is significantly higher or lower would not be representative. Discussion with the household member may be needed to determine representative income. 

1800.5.3 Non-Representative Income

  1. Income amount that is an increase or decrease in earning that is not usual for the household’s income. Examples: Income paid because of nonrecurring overtime, nonrecurring bonuses, work hours missed due to emergencies, etc.

1800.5.4 Income Conversion

  1. Income paid weekly or  Bi-Weekly must be converted to a monthly amount. Conversion factor is:
  2. The average weekly amount is multiplied by 4.3.
  3. The average bi-weekly amount is multiplied by 2.15.
  4. Income received twice a month is multiplied by 2.

1800.5.5 Stable Income

  1. Income that is received at the same interval with minimal changes in amounts. Examples: Social Security benefits, SSI, maximum unemployment compensation benefits, state employee wages, etc. 

1800.5.6 Income Averaging

1800.5.6.1 Procedures to Average Income

  1. Average income when the income amounts, or receipt dates are inconsistent. To determine an average amount:
    1. add the gross income for the time period used; 
    2. divide by the number of possible pay dates for the time period used; 
    3. multiply by the conversion factor; and
    4. the result is the gross monthly average.
  2. Use whatever period of time is necessary to anticipate a representative amount accurately.

1800.5.6.2 Fluctuating Income

  1. Income that is usually paid at a set interval but in different amounts. Examples include, but are not limited to, child support payments, wages with inconsistent hours, etc. 

1800.5.6.3 Contractual Income

  1. Average  Contractual Income intended as annual income over a twelve-month period, even if the income is paid in a shorter time frame. 
  2. Average contractual income that is not the household’s annual income over the period the income is intended to cover.

1800.5.6.4 Income That Covers More Than One Month

  1. Average income received less often than monthly over the time period the income is intended to cover.  

1800.5.6.5 Irregular Income

  1. Income that is not paid regularly or starts and stops without a known schedule. Examples may include temporary jobs, on-call work, etc. 
  2. Irregular income also includes unexpected income that cannot reasonably be expected to recur, such as a cash gift.
    1. Exclude up to $30 of income per  Federal Fiscal Quarter when the income is received too infrequently or irregularly to be reasonably anticipated. 
    2. Average the income if over $30 per quarter and it can be reasonably anticipated to be received.

1800.5.6.6 Self-Employment Income 

  1. See Self-Employment for budgeting procedures.

1800.5.6.7 Student Income

  1. Budget income for each eligible student, starting with the month the income is received in the following order:
    1. Exclude all student financial assistance received under programs in Title IV of the Higher Education Act or under Bureau of Indian Affairs student assistance programs.  
    2. Exclude any student assistance earmarked or made available for allowable school expenses.
    3. Exclude any remaining student assistance the student states are used for allowable student expenses.
    4. Divide the remaining education assistance by the number of months the income is intended to cover. 

1800.6 Determining Monthly Deductions

1800.6.1 Types of Deductions

  1. Monthly expenses used to determine deductions are:
    1. Dependent Care
    2. Earned Income Deduction
    3. Legally Obligated Child Support
    4. Medical Expenses
    5. Shelter Expenses
    6. Standard Deduction

1800.6.2 Disallowed Expenses

  1. Do not allow expenses paid by someone outside of the household. These payments may be reimbursements or vendor payments. Exception: Utility expenses are allowed if paid by the  $21 Seasonal Fuel Assistance. 
  2. Do not allow an expense if the service is provided by someone residing in the household.

1800.6.3 Billed Expenses

  1. Allow expenses only in the month they are billed or otherwise become due, regardless of when the household intends to pay the expense. Do not allow amounts from past billing periods, even if actually paid in the current month. 
  2. Exception: See Averaging Expenses.

1800.6.4 Anticipating Expenses

  1. Calculate expenses that are reasonably certain to be billed during the benefit period. 

1800.6.5 Expense Conversion

  1. Convert expenses paid weekly or  Bi-Weekly  to a monthly amount. Conversion factor is:
    1. The average weekly amount is multiplied by 4.3.
    2. The average bi-weekly amount is multiplied by 2.15.
    3. Expenses billed twice a month are multiplied by 2.

1800.6.6 Averaging Expenses

1800.6.6.1 Types of Expenses that May be Averaged

  1. Households may choose to have fluctuating expenses averaged. These are expenses that are usually billed at a set interval but in different amounts. 
  2. Households may also choose to have expenses which are billed less often than monthly averaged forward over the interval between scheduled billings, or, if there is no scheduled billing, averaged forward over the period the expense is intended to cover. 

1800.6.6.2 One-Time Expenses

  1. Households may choose to have one-time only expenses averaged over the entire benefit period in which they are billed. 

1800.6.6.3 One-Time Medical Expenses

  1. Households that are not using the standard medical expense may choose to have one-time only medical expenses:
    1. used in one month; or
    2. averaged over the remaining months of their benefit period. Averaging begins the month the change is effective.
  2. Exception: If the household is certified for a 24-month or 36-month period and the expense is reported in the first 12-months, the household has the option of averaging the expense over the first 12-months of the benefit period or over the remaining months of the 24-month or 36-month benefit period. If the household is certified for a 36-month period and the expense is reported in the second 12-months, the household has the option of averaging the expense over the second 12-months of the benefit period or over the remaining months of the 36-month benefit period.

1800.7 Income Limits

1800.7.1 Categorical Eligibility

  1. Standard Categorical Eligibility (SCE) households do not have a gross or net income limit.
  2. Expanded Categorical Eligible (ECE) households’ gross income must be below 185% of the FPL. ECE household’s do not have a net income limit.
  3. Senior/Disabled households do not have a gross income limit.

1800.8 Calculating Net Income and Benefit Levels

  1. Benefits are calculated automatically by ACCESS. See Net Income and Benefit Calculation for the calculation.
  2. Benefits are prorated for the application month based on the date the application is filed.

2100.1 Addiction Treatment Centers 

2100.1.1 Exemption from Institution Rules

  1. Eligible  Addiction Treatment Centers are exempt from institution rules.

2100.1.2 Eligible Addiction Treatment Centers

  1. Eligible addiction treatment centers must be:
    1. certified by the  USDA FNS as a retailer, or 
    2. a private nonprofit organization or institution, or a publicly operated community mental health center funded or eligible for funding under Part B of Title XIX of the Public Health Service Act.  
  2. The following addiction treatment centers are eligible:
    1. Grace House
    2. Magee House
    3. Mandala House
    4. Maple Leaf
    5. Phoenix House
    6. Rise
    7. Serenity House
    8. Valley Vista
    9. Teen Challenge
      1. Note: Teen Challenge participants may move between Vermont, New Hampshire and Massachusetts. Verify the participant is not receiving benefits in New Hampshire or Massachusetts before issuing 3SVT. 
    10. Brattleboro Retreat
      1. Note: Brattleboro Retreat offers several treatment programs. If a customer reports living at Brattleboro Retreat, we must confirm they are in an addiction treatment program. If at Brattleboro Retreat for another program, then the customer is not eligible for 3SVT. 
  3. If the treatment center’s authorization isn’t known, email AOPS. AOPS will determine if the treatment center is eligible.

2100.1.3 Resident Eligibility

  1. Residents of eligible drug or alcohol addiction treatment centers, regularly participating, qualify for 3SVT as separate households from others living in the facility. Exception: Children living with their parent who is a resident of the treatment center must be included as part of the resident parent’s 3SVT household.
  2. The resident and their household must meet all 3SVT eligibility requirements.
  3. People regularly participating in a drug or alcohol treatment program are exempt from work requirements.

2100.1.4 Applying for 3SVT

  1. A designated employee of the treatment center must apply as the authorized representative and act for the resident while the resident is in the treatment center.

2100.1.5 Processing 

  1. Residents of treatment centers are eligible for the same service and processing standards as other 3SVT recipients.
  2. Treatment center households must meet the same eligibility requirements as other 3SVT recipients.

2100.1.6 Reporting Requirements

  1. The center as authorized representative must report as required by the Simplified Reporting.

2100.1.7 Treatment Center Information Requirements

  1. The treatment center must provide  DCF with a monthly list of currently participating residents, including a statement signed by a responsible center official attesting to the validity of the list. 
  2. DCF conducts periodic, random on-site visits to the center to assure the accuracy of the list and that DCF's records are consistent and up to date.

2100.1.8 Center Requirements When the Resident Leaves

  1. When the resident leaves the treatment center it will:
    1. End acting as the authorized representative;
    2. Return the  EBT card to the resident;
    3. Advise the resident of their reporting requirement by providing form ESD 200, Change Report;
    4. If spent, return half of the resident’s 3SVT benefit according to DCF procedures when the resident leaves the facility before the 16th of the month;
    5. Return the EBT card to the State if unable to return the EBT card to the resident.

2100.2.2.1 Prorating Income

  1. Step 1: Determine the ineligible member’s monthly countable Earned Income and Unearned Income separately. See Income and Determining Income and Deductions.
  2. Step 2: Total the number of eligible and ineligible household members.
  3. Step 3: Divide the earned/unearned amount in step 1 by the number of people in the household.
  4. Step 4: Multiply the amount in step 3 by the number of eligible household members.

    1. Court ordered child support.

  3. Prorate as follows:
    1. Determine the ineligible member’s monthly allowable expenses. See Deductions and Determining Income and Deductions.
    2. Total the number of eligible and ineligible household members.
    3. Divide the earned/unearned amount in 1 by the number of people in Multiply the amount in 3 by the number of eligible household members.

2100.2.3 Non-members

  1. Non-members are people who live with and may share food with the household but are not included as part of the household:
    1. Boarders
    2. Live-in attendants
    3. Residents of commercial boarding houses
      1. Exception: Residents of institutions identified in this item
    4. Roomers
    5. Strikers
    6. Students
  2. For a full description, see Non-Members in Household Composition.
  3. Exclude income and resources of non-members when determining eligibility and benefits for the household.  Exception: Cash payments from the non-member to the household are income to the household.
  4. Allow only the amount the household paid or contributed when expenses are shared with a non-member.  If the household’s amount cannot be determined prorate the expense and allow the household’s share.

  2. Note: A household member who fails to comply with workfare is ineligible for categorical eligibility. Remaining household members remain eligible for categorical eligibility.

2100.3 Failure to Comply with Another Program’s Requirements

2100.3.1 Effects on 3SVT Benefits

  1. Do not increase 3SVT benefits when the household’s  RU/RF benefits end or decrease because the household/person intentionally failed to comply with a RU/RF requirement. 
  2. Note: Do not end 3SVT benefits when the household’s benefits from the other program end due to the household’s failure to comply with a requirement of that program.

2100.3.2 Exceptions

  1. Do not apply this policy when:
    1. Information regarding the other program’s penalty cannot be obtained.  CATN the actions taken to try to obtain the information.
    2. The household/person did not comply with a requirement at application for the other program. 
    3. The household/person is subject to a disqualification/penalty in both the 3SVT program and RU or RF, apply the 3SVT disqualification first.
      1. Example: The RU and 3SVT participant refuses to accept a job offer. This is also a 3SVT work requirement. Apply the 3SVT disqualification first. If the 3SVT disqualification ends for any reason, and the penalty is still being applied in the RU program, the full RU amount is budgeted. See Budgeting below.

2100.3.3 Budgeting

2100.3.3.1 Retention of Full RU/RF Benefit Amount

  1. ACCESS keeps the full RU/RF benefit amount in the 3SVT budget for work requirement or child support sanctions.

2100.3.4 How Long to Budget the Penalty 

  1. Budget the penalty until the:
    1. household/person complies with the other program’s requirements,
    2. other program ends, 
    3. amount of the penalty cannot be determined, or
    4. household/person is ineligible for the other program for a reason not related to the penalty.  

2100.3.5 Acting on Changes

  1. Continue to act on changes for 3SVT not related to the other program’s penalty.
  2. Example 1: The household reports and verifies they now pay dependent care for their 1-year-old child.  Add the dependent care and budget as a deduction. Continue to budget the other program’s penalty amount.
  3. Example 2: The household’s earned income decreases and the Reach Up benefit increases because of the decrease in earned income. Budget the:
    1. new amount of earned income, 
    2. new amount of Reach Up benefits, and 
    3. the penalty.

2100.4 Fleeing Felons and Probation/Parole Violators

2100.4.1 Summary

  1. Fleeing felons are people trying to escape or avoid custody for a crime, or attempted crime, classified as a felony (or high misdemeanor in New Jersey) under a Federal or State law.
  2. Probation or parole violators are people who have violated a condition of their probation or parole ordered under a Federal or State law.  
  3. Only people verified through law enforcement as fleeing felons or probation or parole violators are disqualified members for 3SVT. See Disqualified Members.

2100.4.2 Fleeing Felon/Probation or Parole Violator Status

2100.4.2.1 Status Determination

  1. A person is a fleeing felon or a probation or parole violator if they meet the following criteria.

2100.4.2.2 Fleeing Felon

  1. A person is a fleeing felon if:
    1. there is an outstanding felony warrant;
    2. the person is aware of or should be aware of the felony warrant;
    3. the person is avoiding being arrested or jailed; and
    4. law enforcement is actively seeking the person.

2100.4.2.3 Probation Violator

  1. A person is a probation violator if:
    1. a Court of law rules that the person violated a condition of their probation ordered under a Federal or State law, and
    2. local law enforcement is actively seeking the person to enforce the conditions of the probation.

2100.4.2.4 Parole Violator

  1. A person is a parole violator if:
    1. the Vermont Parole Board (for state offenses) or a Court of law (for Federal offenses) rules that the person violated a condition of their parole ordered under a Federal or State law, and 
    2. local law enforcement is actively seeking the person to enforce the conditions of the parole.

2100.4.2.5 Actively Seeking

  1. Actively seeking means a Federal, State, or local law enforcement agency:
    1. states an intent to enforce an outstanding felony warrant, or arrest a person for a probation or parole violation within 20-days of the date the law enforcement agency requested information about the person;
    2. presents a felony arrest warrant; or 
    3. states an intent to enforce an outstanding felony warrant, or arrest a person for probation or parole violation within 30-days of the date of a status information request from DCF.
  2. A person is not a fleeing felon or a probation or parole violator if the law enforcement agency does not intend to enforce the felony warrant or to arrest the person for a probation or parole violation within 30-days of the status information request from DCF. 

2100.4.3 Verification

  1. Verify the person’s status as a fleeing felon or a probation or parole violator before disqualifying them for 3SVT.   
  2. Law enforcement requests for information do not verify the status of the felony warrant or probation or parole violation status.
  3. Verify the status of a felony warrant or probation or parole violation only if:
    1. a law enforcement agency presents a felony arrest warrant, or states intent to arrest a person for probation or parole violation, or
    2. the customer self-declares the information.  
  4. Request the following status information from the law enforcement agency:
    1. the conditions of a felony warrant or a probation or parole violation, and 
    2. whether the law enforcement agency intends to actively seek the person.
  5. Allow the law enforcement agency 20-days to respond to a status information request.
  6. If the law enforcement agency intends to enforce the felony warrant, or arrest the person for a probation or parole violation within 30-days of the date of the status request:
    1. delay any actions on the case until the 30-day period ends, and
    2. verify with the law enforcement agency if an attempt was made to execute the felony warrant or arrest the probation or parole violator.
      1. If action was taken within the 30-days, deny or end the person’s eligibility.
      2. If no action was taken within the 30-days, the person is not a fleeing felon or probation or parole violator.

2100.4.4 Application Processing

  1. Continue to process the application, including expedited services, while waiting for verification of the status of the fleeing felon or probation or parole violator.  Process the application without considering the person’s fleeing felon or probation or parole violation status if needed to meet the required 3SVT processing timeframes.

2100.4.5 Reporting

2100.4.5.1 Change Processing

  1. Follow change processing policy to act on decreases in household size when a member has been verified as a fleeing felon or a probation or parole violator. See Change Processing, Action on Unscheduled Changes.

2100.5 Group Living for the Disabled

2100.5.1 Exemption from Institution Rules

  1. Eligible  Group Living for the Disabled facilities are exempt from  Institution rules. Eligible group living for the disabled:
    1. is a public or private  Nonprofit residential setting, 
    2. serving no more than 16 residents, and
    3. certified by the appropriate agency or agencies of the State under regulations or comparable standards issued under Section 1616(e) of the Social Security Act.
  2. If the facility’s eligibility isn’t known, email the Agency of Human Services-Economic Services Division-Assistant Operations (AHS-ESD-AOPS).  AOPS will determine if the facility is eligible.

2100.5.2 Resident Eligibility

  1. Residents of any age must be disableddisabled.

    A person who meets one or more of the following:

    • Receives disability or blindness benefits from any of these programs: Social Security,   SSI, or   MABD.
      • A person meets this even when the full benefit is withheld or deferred due to recoupment.
    • Receives a federally or state administered SSI supplement based on disability or blindness, or section 212(a) of PL 93-66.
    • Receives a disability retirement benefit from a government agency for a   disability considered permanent by SSAdisability considered permanent by SSA.

      Any of the following conditions:

      • Permanent loss of use of both hands, both feet, or one hand and one foot.
      • Amputation of leg at hip. Amputation of leg or foot because of diabetes or peripheral vascular diseases.
      • Total deafness, not correctable by surgery or hearing aid. Statutory blindness, unless caused by cataracts or detached retina.
      • IQ of 59 or less, established after the person becomes 16 years old.
      • Spinal cord or nerve root lesions resulting in paraplegia or quadriplegia.
      • Multiple sclerosis with damage to the nervous system caused by scattered areas of inflammation. The inflammation recurs and has progressed to varied interference with the function of the nervous system, including severe muscle weakness, paralysis, and vision and speech defects.
      • Muscular dystrophy with irreversible wasting of the muscles, impairing the ability to use the arms or legs.
      • Impaired renal function caused by chronic renal disease, resulting in severely reduced function which may require dialysis or kidney transplant.
      • Amputation of a limb of a person at least 55 years old.
    • Is a veteran the VA considers totally (100%) disabled or permanently housebound or in need of regular aid and attendance.
    • Is a veteran’s surviving spouse who the VA considers:
      • in need of regular aid and attendance, or
      • permanently housebound, or
      • approved for benefits because of the veteran’s death and has a disability considered permanent by SSA.
    • Is a veteran’s surviving child who the VA considers:
      • incapable of self-support, or
      • approved for benefits because of the veteran’s death and has a disability considered permanent by SSA
  2. The resident or the household must meet all 3SVT eligibility requirements.

2100.5.3 Applying for 3SVT

  1. Residents may apply as a:
    1. group of one, or
    2. any group of residents applying as a household.
  2. Residents may:
    1. apply themselves
    2. Note: The facility must decide if the resident can apply for 3SVT on his/her own behalf based on the resident's physical and mental ability to handle his/her affairs.
    3. choose their own AR, or 
    4. use the designated employee of the facility as their authorized representative.
  3. Residents applying themselves do not lose their eligibility if the facility:
    1. loses its approval from the appropriate agency or agencies of the State, or 
    2. its status as an authorized representative is suspended due to FNS disqualifying the facility as a retailer. 

2100.5.4 Processing

2100.5.4.1 3SVT Eligibility Determination 

  1. Residents of group living for the disabled facilities are eligible for the same service and processing standards as other 3SVT recipients.
  2. Facility households must meet the same eligibility requirements as other 3SVT recipients, with the following exception:
    1. Allow any identified shelter expenses paid by the resident.  If a single payment covers multiple expenses, deduct the following identified costs from the payment:
      1. medical expenses (allow toward the medical deduction)
      2. amount returned to the resident for personal use, and
      3. the maximum food stamp benefit for the number of persons in the household. 
    2. Allow any remaining amount as the shelter expense.

2100.5.5 Use of Benefits

  1. Residents certified as a household of one may:
    1. give their benefits to the facility to purchase food for meals served to the group or person,
    2. purchase and prepare their own food, or
    3. purchase meals prepared and served by the facility.
  2. The facility will ensure each resident's 3SVT benefits are used for meals intended for that resident when the resident chooses personal meals instead of group meals.

2100.5.6 Reporting Changes

  1. Residents of group living for the disabled facilities are in Simplified Reporting.
  2. Residents applying themselves are responsible to report their own changes. 
  3. If an employee of the facility acts as the authorized representative, the facility must report as required by Simplified Reporting.

2100.5.7 Facility Information Requirements

  1. The facility must provide  DCF with a monthly list of currently participating residents, including a statement signed by a responsible facility official attesting to the validity of the list. 
  2. DCF conducts periodic, random onsite visits to assure the accuracy of the list, and that DCF's records are consistent and up to date.

2100.5.8 Facility Requirements When the Resident Leaves

  1. When the resident leaves, the facility will:
    1. End acting as the authorized representative (if applicable);
    2. Return the  EBT card to the resident when an employee of the facility is the authorized representative;
    3. Advise the resident of their reporting requirement by providing an ESD 200, Change Report;
    4. If spent, return half of the resident’s 3SVT benefit according to DCF procedures when the resident leaves the facility before the 16th of the month; 
    5. Return the EBT card to the State if unable to return the EBT card to the resident.
  2. Facilities acting as authorized representative for the household are responsible for all:
    1. losses or misuse of benefits, and 
    2. overpayments occurring while acting as authorized representative.

2100.6 Homeless Shelters

2100.6.1 Definition of Homeless Shelters

  1. Homeless shelters are public or private nonprofit facilities that provide temporary housing for homeless people. They are exempt from  Institution rules. 
  2. Homeless people are people or groups without a fixed and regular nighttime residence, or whose primary nighttime residence is a:
    1. Halfway house or similar institution providing temporary residences for people intended to be institutionalized;
    2. Temporary accommodations in hotels, motels, or shelters;
    3. Temporary accommodations in the residence of another person, for not more than 90 days; or
    4. Place not designed for or ordinarily used as a regular sleeping accommodation for human beings. Examples: hallways, bus stations, lobbies or similar places.
  3. If the homeless shelter’s eligibility isn’t known, email the Agency of Human Services-Economic Services Division-Assistant Operations (AHS-ESD-AOPS).  AOPS will determine if the homeless shelter is eligible.

2100.6.2 Applying for 3SVT

  1. Homeless households may apply for benefits separately from others living in the homeless shelter. See Household Composition, Homeless Shelters.
  2. Homeless households may pick an  AR to act on the household’s behalf. 

2100.6.3 Processing

  1. Homeless households are eligible for the same service and processing standards as other 3SVT recipients.
  2. Homeless households must meet the same eligibility requirements as other 3SVT recipients.
  3. Homeless households are not required to have a permanent dwelling or fixed residence. Ask the household to provide a mailing address where notices can be mailed. See Residency.

2100.6.4 Work Requirements for Homeless Time Limited Participants

  1. Homeless participants who are time limited must meet the requirements in 3SquaresVT Time Limited Benefits. 

2100.6.5 Use of Benefits

  1. Homeless households may use 3SVT benefits to purchase prepared meals from authorized grocery stores.

2100.7 Migrants or Seasonal Farmworkers

2100.7.1 Definition of Migrants and Seasonal Farmworkers

  1. Migrant farmworkers temporarily leave a principal place of residence in another state to come to Vermont to accept seasonal employment in the planting, cultivating, raising, harvesting, handling, drying, packing, packaging, processing, freezing, grading or storing of any agricultural or horticultural commodity in its unmanufactured state.
  2. Seasonal farmworkers accept seasonal employment in the planting, cultivating, raising, harvesting, handling, drying, packing, packaging, processing, freezing, grading or storing of any agricultural or horticultural commodity in its unmanufactured state.

2100.7.2 Applying

  1. The migrant or seasonal farmworker may apply for 3SVT for their household or choose an authorized representative.  
  2. In the event the migrant or seasonal farmworker’s employer acts as the AR, ensure the:
    1. household freely requested the employer to act as their authorized representative, 
    2. employer correctly represents the household’s circumstances,
    3. household receives the correct amount of benefits, and
    4. authorized representative properly uses the benefits for the household.

2100.7.3 Processing

2100.7.3.1 Eligibility Requirements

  1. Migrant or seasonal farmworker households are eligible for the same service and processing standards, and must meet the same eligibility requirements as other households, with the following exceptions listed throughout this section.

2100.7.3.2 Expedited Processing

  1. Migrant or seasonal farmworker households may qualify for expedited benefits. See Expedited Service. Additionally, migrant or seasonal farmworker households can qualify for expedited processing if the household meets destitute requirements.

2100.7.3.3 Destitute Defined

  1. Only migrant or seasonal farmworker households can be determined as destitute. Destitute means the household’s liquid resources do not exceed $100 and their only income for the month of application:
    1. was received from a stopped source before the date of application,
      1. Income received on a monthly or more frequent basis is stopped from that source if the income will not be received again from the source during the remainder of the month.
      2. Income received less often than monthly is stopped from that source if the income is not received in the month the next payment would normally be received.
    2. is from a new source for $25 or more but will not be received by the 10th calendar day after the date of application,
      1. Income received on a monthly or more frequent basis is from a new source when income has not been received from that source 30-days prior to the date of application.
      2. Income received less often than monthly is from a new source if the income was not received from that source during the last normal interval between payments.
  2. Migrant or seasonal farmworker households can be Destitute even if they receive both stopped income prior to the date of application, and income from a new source after the date of application, provided the household:
    1. will not receive any other income in the month of application, and
    2. any income of $25 or more from a new source will not be received by the 10th day after the date of application.
  3. Household members changing jobs but continuing to work for the same employer/grower are receiving continuing income, not income from a new source.  The employer/grower is the source, not the crew chief. Consider migrant or seasonal farmworker households traveling with a crew chief from one job source to another source as moving from a stopped source to a new source.
  4. Verify destitute status but only if verification will not delay meeting expedited processing requirements.

2100.7.3.4 Residency

  1. Residency verification may be waived if verification cannot be done.  
  2. Attempt to verify with the state the migrant or seasonal farmworker’s household moved from to determine the case closure date and the date benefits were last issued. 
  3. See Residency 

2100.7.3.5 Income 

  1. Determine a  Destitute migrant or seasonal farmworker household’s eligibility and benefits for the month of application using all sources of income received between the first of the month and the date of application.  Disregard income from a new source that will be received after the date of application.
  2. Exclude General Assistance (GA) vendor payments for Emergency Assistance including but not limited to housing and transportation payments for migrant or seasonal farmworkers households while they are in the job stream. 
  3. Exclude reimbursements for travel expenses incurred by migrant or seasonal farmworkers.  Count travel expense advances included in a written work agreement or statement that will be subtracted later from the farmworker’s income.
  4. Budget the household’s income for the period the income is intended to cover.  
  5. Note: Exclude a child’s earnings.  If the child’s earnings cannot be identified separately from the rest of the household’s earnings,
    1. divide the total earnings by the number of members that work, and
    2. exclude the child’s share of the income.
  6. Do not average contract or self-employment income over a 12-month period even when the migrant or farmworker’s annual income is earned over a shorter period of time.  

2100.7.3.6 Work Registration

  1. Exempt migrant or seasonal farmworkers working 30-hours per week or more or under contract, or a similar agreement, with an employer or crew chief to begin work within 30-days. See work registration exemptions.

2100.7.3.7 Good Cause for Leaving Employment

  1. Farmworkers leaving one job to move to another meet good cause for leaving or reducing employment, even if work at the new job site has not begun.

2100.7.3.8 Verification 

  1. Migrant or seasonal farmworkers must not be targeted for more intensive verifications than other 3SVT households. See Verification and Case Notes (CATN).
  2. A collateral contact may be made with a person outside the farmworker’s household, such as but not limited to:
    1. employers/growers, 
    2. crew chiefs, or
    3. migrant services agencies. 

2100.7.3.9 Proration of Benefits

  1. Proration of benefits is required for the initial month of approval. The initial month for migrant or seasonal farmworker households is the first month the household is certified following a period of more than one month since the last approval. The last approval may have been in Vermont or another state. See Initial Month.

2100.7.4 Reporting

  1. Migrant or seasonal farmworker households are in Simplified Reporting.

2100.7.5 Recertification 

  1. Disregard income from a new source in the first month of the new certification period when the income:
    1. is $25 or more, and 
    2. will not be received from this new source by the 10th calendar day after the date of the households normal issuance cycle.

2100.7.6 Hearings

  1. Hearing requests from migrant or seasonal farmworker households must be processed faster than normal hearing decisions when the household plans to move from the jurisdiction of the hearing official.
  2. Process the hearing request to ensure the household receives a decision, and has benefits restored if applicable, before the household leaves the area. See Fair Hearings.

2100.8 Shelters for Battered Persons

2100.8.1 Status of Shelters

  1. Shelters for battered persons are exempt from  Institution rules.
  2. Shelters for battered persons are public or private nonprofit residential facilities providing temporary housing for battered persons.  If the shelter serves other people, a portion of the shelter must be set aside on a long-term basis to serve only battered persons.  
  3. If the shelter’s status as a facility isn’t known, email the Agency of Human Services-Economic Services Division-Assistant Operations (AHS-ESD-AOPS). AOPS will determine if the shelter is eligible. Exception: If the shelter is authorized by FNS to redeem as a wholesaler, it is an eligible facility. No further inquiry is needed.

2100.8.2 Applying for 3SVT

  1. Shelter residents and their children may apply for benefits as a separate household from others living in the shelter for battered persons. 
  2. Shelter households may pick an AR to act on the household’s behalf.

2100.8.3 Processing

  1. Ensure the abuser’s household eligibility and benefit reflect the change in household composition due to the shelter household’s departure.
  2. Shelter resident households are eligible for the same service and processing standards, and must meet the same eligibility requirements as other households, with the following exceptions:
    1. Duplicate benefits may be issued the first month the household moved into the shelter if the household was part of a SNAP household that included the abuser.
    2. Count only the income of the battered person and their children.
    3. Allow only the deductions of the battered person and their children.

2100.8.4 Use of Benefits

  1. Residents of shelters for battered persons may use their 3SVT benefits to:
    1. purchase and prepare their own food, or
    2. purchase meals prepared and served at the shelter.

2100.9 Strikers

2100.9.1 Definition of Strikers

  1. People who join other employees in stopping or interrupting work to protest work conditions, or to force the employer to accept the employees’ demands are strikers. Consider people strikers until they:
    1. return to work,
    2. are fired, or 
    3. quit their job.
  2. People who are not strikers includes but is not limited to:
    1. Employees whose workplace is closed because of a  Lockout;
    2. People exempt from work registration the day before the strike began; 
    3. Exception: Does not include the exemption for being employed 30 hours or more.
    4. Employees not part of the bargaining unit on strike who do not want to cross the picket line due to fear of injury or death; and 
    5. People unable to work due to striking employees. Examples:
      1. Truck drivers not working because products are not being produced.
      2. Employees laid off or furloughed due to the strike.

2100.9.2 Applying for 3SVT

  1. The striker household may apply for 3SVT or choose an authorized representative.  

2100.9.3 Processing

  1. Households with striking members are not eligible for 3SVT unless the household:
    1. was eligible for benefits the day before the strike began, and 
    2. are eligible for benefits when they apply.
  2. Striker households receive the same service and processing standards and must meet the same eligibility requirements as other households, with the following exceptions listed throughout this section.

2100.9.3.1 Categorical Eligibility

  1. Households are not categorically eligible if the household is ineligible due to a striker.

2100.9.3.2 Income

  1. Determine the gross income by budgeting the:
    1. current income of the non-striking members, and  
    2. the higher of the striker’s income from the:
      1. monthly income before the strike began ( Pre-strike Income), or
      2. current income.
  2. Determine the household’s net income by allowing:
    1. deductions for the current month, and 
    2. the earned income deduction for all household members, including the striker.
  3. Continue to follow steps a and b above as long as the person is a striker. 
  4. Count strike benefits including picket pay as  Unearned Income.
  5. Households are not eligible for increased benefits due to decreased income because of the strike.

2100.9.3.3 Voluntary Quit

  1. Federal, State, or local government employees who go on strike have voluntarily quit their job without good cause.

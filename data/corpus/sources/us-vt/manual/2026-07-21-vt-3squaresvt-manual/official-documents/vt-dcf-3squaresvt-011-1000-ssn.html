1000.1 Overview

  1. All household members applying for 3SVT must provide their  SSN.  A household member with more than one SSN must provide all of them. If a household member does not have an SSN, they must apply for one before approval. 
  2. Exception: See Newborn and Religious Beliefs 
  3. Providing the SSN is voluntary, however, failure to provide or apply for an SSN without good cause will result in ineligibility for that person.
  4. Note: The 3SVT application cannot be delayed pending the verification of an SSN by SSA. The person is allowed to participate pending verification.

1000.2 Obtaining SSNs for 3SVT Household Members

1000.2.1 Referral to SSA

  1. Refer household members who do not have an SSN to their local  SSA office to apply for an SSN. Once proof of the SSN application is provided, allow the household member to participate while SSA processes the application. 

1000.2.2 Newborn

  1. Parents of a newborn child may apply for an SSN for the child at the hospital, when available.  A household must provide proof of application for an SSN for a newborn child within six months following the month the baby is born or at its next recertification, whichever is later. 
  2. If at the next 3SVT recertification or within 6 months from the date of the child’s birth the child has not met the social security requirement determine if good cause exists. 

1000.2.3 Religious Beliefs

  1. Household members who have a religious objection to applying for or providing an SSN would not be required to do so to be eligible for 3SVT. 

1000.3 Failure to Comply

  1. If a household member has refused or failed without good cause to provide or apply for an SSN, that household member is ineligible to participate in the 3SVT program.  Only the member without the SSN is ineligible, not the entire household. Resources are counted as if they were eligible; income and deductions are prorated. See Ineligible Members.

1000.4 Determining Good Cause

1000.4.1 Proof of Good Cause

  1. Documentary evidence or other information that shows the household has applied for or made every effort to supply the necessary information for an SSN is proof of  Good Cause.
  2. This information may come from any of the following:
    1. The household member
    2.  SSA
    3.  DCF staff 
  3. Note: Good cause does not include delays caused by illness, lack of transportation or temporary absences from the home as SSA allows mail-in applications. 

1000.4.2 Continued Participation

  1. Allow a member who can show good cause for not timely completing an SSN application to participate for the month of application and one additional month. After this, the household must show good cause monthly in order to continue participating.

1000.5 Regaining Eligibility

  1. An ineligible member may become eligible by providing their SSN or by providing proof of application for SSN. 

1000.6 Use of the SSN

  1. When determining eligibility for 3SVT, SSNs may be used to:
    1. Determine amount of benefits
    2. Prevent duplicate participation
    3. Facilitate mass changes in federal benefits
    4. Determine the accuracy or reliability of information given by households
    5. Request and exchange information on persons through IEVS

1000.7 Verification

  1. Verify SSN at application or if a new member is added to the household. 
  2. Exception: See Newborn

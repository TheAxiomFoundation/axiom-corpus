600.1 Overview

  1. The household must live in Vermont when applying for 3SVT. People living in Vermont for any reason meet the residency requirement, regardless of the length of time they have been here or plan to stay. Exception: People on vacation in Vermont do not meet the residency requirement.
  2. Note: People that maintain Vermont residency and vacation or temporarily live out of state still meet the residency requirement.
  3. When a person moves to Vermont from another state and reports SNAP closure in the previous 30 days, verify the effective closure date. Contact the other state using the national directory. Exception: If the person is currently a resident in a Vermont battered persons shelter, duplicate benefits may be issued.  
  4. The household is not required to have a permanent dwelling or fixed residence. For example, a person still meets the residency requirement if the person lives in a
    1. car, 
    2. camper, 
    3. tent, or 
    4.  Homeless Shelter. 
  5. Note: Ask the household to provide a mailing address where notices can be mailed. The household may use any address as long as there is no threat of mail being lost or stolen. For example, the address can be a friend or relative’s address or a post office box. 

600.2 Verification

600.2.1 Guidelines to Verify Vermont Residency

  1. Verify Vermont residency if questionable.  Accept any document or collateral contact that reasonably proves the person’s residency. 

600.2.2 Application

  1. Deny the application if not verified by the due date.  
  2. Postpone verification for expedited service month(s), unless verification can be provided in time to meet the seven-day standard of promptness.

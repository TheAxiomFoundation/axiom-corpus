300.1 Overview

  1. Expedited service allows households to quickly receive an initial benefit with fewer required verifications.  

300.2 Screening Requirements

300.2.1 Screening Procedures

Screen the ESD 202, Application, the day received. Use the Expedited Service Calculator.

300.2.2 Expedited Eligibility

  1. Households must meet one of the following criteria to receive expedited service:
    1. Countable income for the month of application is less than $150 and liquid resources are not more than $100, or
    2. Combined countable income for the month of application, and liquid resources are less than the household’s monthly:
      1. shelter expenses including but not limited to rent, mortgage, lot rent, property taxes, home insurance, condo fees; and 
      2. utility expenses using the appropriate utility standard, or
    3.  Migrant Worker or  Seasonal Farmworker households who:
      1. are  Destitute, and
      2. have less than $100 in liquid resources.
  2. Review PERS/D and/or INQD/D to see if the household is known to ACCESS and whether they receive or have received benefits in Vermont.

300.2.3 Households Not Eligible for Expedited Service

  1. Households are not eligible for expedited service at recertification when they reapply before the end of the current certification period. 
  2. Households are not eligible for expedited service if they received benefits in another household or another state in the month of application.
    1. Exception: Households in shelters for battered persons can receive benefits in the same month. Please send to AOPS to review. 
  3. Households that reapply after failing to complete postponed requirements are not eligible for expedited service. See Reapplication after Expedited service for more information. 
  4. Process households who are not eligible for expedited service following normal 30-day application processing.  

300.2.4 Coding ACCESS 

  1. Enter one of the following reason codes in the STAT/C/STAT panel to indicate the household’s determination for expedited service:
    1. Code G = Gross Income and Liquid Resources. Households with countable gross income less than $150, and their liquid resources are not more than $100, or
    2. Code S = Shelter Costs.  Households countable income for the month of application and liquid resources less than the household’s monthly:
      1. shelter expenses including but not limited to rent, mortgage, lot rent, property taxes, homeowners insurance, condo fees; and 
      2. utility expenses using the appropriate utility standard, or
    3. Code D = Destitute and Liquid Resources.  Migrant or seasonal farmworker households who:
      1. are destitute, and
      2. have less than $100 in liquid resources
    4. Code N = Not eligible for expedited service. This includes households that did not complete the minimum action requirements by day 7.

300.3 Expedited Service Minimum Actions

300.3.1 Minimum Actions Requirements

  1. Households must complete the following minimum actions for expedited service:
    1. Complete an interview, and
    2. Verify identity 
  2. ESD must complete an eDRS on all adult household members prior to granting expedited service. See eDRS if an eDRS cannot be completed.
  3. See Postponed Verification and Actions for actions the household must complete in order to continue benefits beyond the first or second month.

300.3.2 Interview

  1. Households must complete an interview before benefits are issued. Interview the household the same day the application is received, if possible. 
  2. Interviews not completed the same day must be completed as soon as possible to meet expedited processing timeframes.

300.3.2.1 Telephone Interview

  1. Two cold call attempts must be made to the household within 5 days to complete the interview when they are not at the office. 
  2. Note: If the customer does not provide a phone number CATN that cold calls were attempted but could not be completed because a phone number was not provided. 
  3. If you cannot reach the household when the application was received, send the ESD 202C, Benefits Interview Appointment Notice, advising the household to contact Economic Services to complete the interview within 5 days.

300.3.2.2 Interview Not Completed

  1. Send households who don’t complete an interview an ESD 202D,  NOMI via ACCESS. The NOMI tells the household:
    1. they missed their interview appointment,
    2. they are responsible to reschedule the interview appointment, and
    3. their application will be denied on the 30th day if the interview is not completed.
  2. Note: If cold calls were not previously completed, they must be completed prior to sending the NOMI. 
  3. Deny the application if the household fails to reschedule and complete the interview by the 30th day. Deny the application on the next business day when the 30th day falls on a weekend or holiday.

300.3.3 Verification of Identity

  1. Households qualifying for expedited service must verify the identity of the person applying if not already known to ACCESS. See Identity for requirements.
  2. Note: Numident for persons with an SSN verifies identity.

300.3.4 eDRS

  1. Review Electronic Disqualified Recipient System (eDRS) on each adult household member to ensure they are not disqualified for IPV. If an eDRS match is returned and secondary verification is required, the household member cannot be approved until secondary verification is provided. 
  2. Exception: If an eDRS cannot be completed because the eDRS system is down, process without the eDRS. The eDRS must be completed once the system is running. 

300.3.5 Minimum Actions Not Completed

  1. Households lose their entitlement to expedited service if they fail to complete the minimum requirements within the seven-day timeframe. Process the application under normal 30-day timeframes.
  2. Initial screening and two cold call attempts must be made to the household within 5 days prior to determining that the household failed to complete the minimum requirements. 

300.3.6 Agency Failure

  1. Agency failure occurs when ESD does not do the following:
    1. screen an initial application for expedited service accurately and timely, and/or
    2. complete cold calls within five days. 
  2. Households do not lose their entitlement to expedited service due to agency failure.
  3. The date of discovery cannot be updated for a household in which agency failure has occurred. 

300.4 Verification of Other Eligibility Factors

300.4.1 Verification Prioritization

  1. All 3SVT requirements must be completed before providing future months' benefits.
  2. All verification other than identity can be postponed.
    1. .
  3. Attempt to verify as many 3SVT eligibility factors as possible within the expedited service timeframes. However, do not delay benefits once the expedited service minimum actions are completed.
  4. Enter verified information into ACCESS. Enter information that has not been verified into ACCESS using the customer's self-reported statement. 

300.4.2 General Work Requirement and Time Limited Benefit Exemption

  1. Screen for general work requirement and time limited benefit exemptions during the interview. A customer who reports an exemption from the general work requirement and/or time limit can be processed expedited pending verification of the exemption. See Postponed verifications and Actions. 
  2. A customer who has used all countable months and does not report an exemption from the time limits is not eligible for expedited services. 

300.4.3 Eligible Non-Citizen

  1. Screen for eligible non-citizen criteria during the interview. A customer who reports eligible non-citizen criteria can be processed expedited pending verification of the eligibility criteria. See Postponed verifications and Actions. 
  2. A non-citizen who does not report meeting eligible non-citizen criteria is an ineligible member.

300.4.4 Student Eligibility

  1. Screen for eligible student criteria during the interview. A customer who reports meeting eligible student criteria can be processed expedited pending verification of the eligibility criteria. See Postponed verifications and Actions. 
  2. A student who does not report meeting eligible student criteria is a non-member. 

300.4.5 Postponed Verifications and Actions

  1. Required 3SVT actions and verifications not completed during expedited service processing must be completed to receive continued benefits.  
  2. All other actions and verifications that are not required for ongoing eligibility and not completed during expedited service processing will be removed from the case. 

300.4.5.1 Notification for Required Postponed Verification

  1. Advise the household during the interview which actions and verifications are required to receive continued benefits. Explain to the household that future months' benefits will not be issued if the actions and verifications are not completed. Issue form ESD 202VCR, Verification Change Request, advising the household:
    1. which verification and requirements are needed, and
    2. the due date when the requirements must be completed to receive continued benefits. 
  2. See Examples of Accecptable Verification.

300.4.5.2 Notification for All Other Postponed Verification

  1. Advise the household during the interview of all other actions and verifications needed. Explain to the household how their future benefit will be affected if they do not complete the requested actions and verifications. Issue form ESD 202VCR, Verification Change Request, advising the household of:
    1. the verification and information needed, and
    2. the date the verification and information must be provided by. 

300.4.6 Household Fails to Complete Postponed Requirements

  1. Close the case if the household fails to complete the required postponed verifications and actions by the ESD 202VCR due date. 
  2. Remove all other information and verification that was not provided during expedited service processing and is not required for ongoing eligibility.
  3. See Delayed Processing if the household completes the postponed requirements within 60 days of the date of application.  

300.5 Processing Timeframes

300.5.1 Issuance of Benefits

  1. Expedited service applies for the month of application only. 
    1. Exception: applications submitted after the 15th of the month are determined for combined benefits. 
  2. Do not delay issuing expedited benefits if the household has applied for other programs that are still pending. 
  3. Issue benefits for eligible households who have completed the minimum requirements the day of the interview, or the next work day, but no later than five days after the application date to ensure they can access benefits within seven days. The seven-day timeframe begins the day after the ESD 202 application date.
  4. Send the household an ESD 220 to advise the household of the results of their determination of eligibility.

300.5.2 Other Application Dates

300.5.2.1 Application Date for Late Determination of Expedited Service Eligibility

  1. The date of discovery for applications screened as not meeting expedited service requirements that are later found eligible for expedited processing.
  2. The date of discovery is entered into the 'EB Discovery Date' field on STAT/STAT.
  3. The seven-day processing timeframe begins the day after the date of discovery.
  4. The date of discovery cannot be updated for a household in which agency failure has occurred.

300.5.3 Coding ACCESS with Late Expedite Reason

  1. Enter one of the following codes in the STAT/C/STAT panel when the expedited service application has not been completed within five days:
    1. Code V = Lacking verification of identity
    2. Code C = Could not contact the household or conduct the interview
    3. Code A = Agency Failure
  2. Update the expedited code to 'N' on STAT/STAT when:
    1. two cold calls were completed within five days, and
    2. the household did not complete the minimum action requirements within the seven-day timeframe.

300.6 Certification Periods

  1. Provide the longest certification period possible based on the household’s situation. See Certification Periods.

300.7 Benefit Issuance

300.7.1 Initial Benefit

  1. Households who file their applications before the 15th of the month receive benefits for the initial month of eligibility. 

300.7.2 Combined Benefit

  1. Applications filed after the 15th of the month receive a combined benefit.

300.7.3 Cash Out

  1. Cash-Out households qualifying for expedited service receive their benefits by check; printed and mailed each night by Buildings and General Services.
  2. See Benefits. 

300.8 Reapplication After Expedited Service

  1. When a household that failed to complete postponed requirements reapplies for benefits the 30-day application processing standard applies.
  2. Exception: If the household completes the postponed requirements, provide expedited service if the household is eligible for it.
  3. Households can be approved for expedited service unlimited times as long as the household:
    1. completes the postponed requirements from the last expedited certification, or 
    2. has been approved under normal 30-day timeframes since the last expedited certification.

300.9 CATN

  1. Enter the expedited service determination in CATN at the time of the interview, including
    1. the expedited service criteria met by the household, and
    2. when the process is delayed due to:
      1. minimum actions not completed, or
      2. late determinations.

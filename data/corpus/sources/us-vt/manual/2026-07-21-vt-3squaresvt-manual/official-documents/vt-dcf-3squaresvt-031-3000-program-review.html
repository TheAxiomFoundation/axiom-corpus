3000.1 Management Evaluation

  1. ME reviewers monitor program access and operations. The reviewers:
    1. Review case files for accuracy and completeness
    2. Interview staff, community agencies, and recipients
    3. Review application access and office procedures
    4. Complete a final report identifying best practices and recommendations for improvements

3000.2 Quality Control

3000.2.1 Random Selection for Quality Control Review

  1. Active 3SVT cases are randomly selected for quality control review. The review determines:
    1. Eligibility and benefit accuracy.
    2. Timeliness of the action for certification within the current  Federal Fiscal Year.
    3. Corrective action recommendations.
  2. Cases denied, closed, suspended or approved for zero benefits are also randomly selected for review. The review is called  CAPER. The review determines:
    1. Eligibility reason accuracy.
    2. Timeliness of the recertification action within the current  Federal Fiscal Year.
    3. Accuracy of notice content.
    4. Corrective action recommendations.

3000.2.2 Quality Control Review

  1. The  QCR process includes:
    1. An electronic and case record review.
    2. A field investigation including an in-person interview with the household for active reviews.
    3. Verification of all eligibility factors.
    4. A report of the case review findings provided to the district management team.

3000.2.3 District Responsibility

  1. Upon receipt of the QCR findings, the Supervisor of the District who has the case must:
    1. Thoroughly review the QCR findings and the case file for accuracy, conferring with the worker if necessary;
    2. Within ten days, send any disagreement (based on policy only) to  OPS;
    3. Assign ES to make case corrections and establish an overpayment or underpayment, when appropriate.

3000.2.4 Failure to Cooperate

  1. Quality Control will notify the agency of the household/member’s failure to cooperate with a QC requirement. The household must provide verification of all eligibility elements prior to its next approval.
  2. If the household does not provide verification:
    1. Deny the application/recertification if a mandatory item (e.g., income) was not verified.
    2. Do not allow any expense that is not verified.

3000.2.5 Disqualification for Refusal to Cooperate

  1. The household is ineligible for 3SVT benefits when a household member refuses to cooperate with the Quality Control review. Quality Control will notify the agency of the household’s refusal to cooperate. Follow  advance noticeadvance notice time frames.

    Advance notice is sent to a household at least ten days before:

    • Decreasing the household’s benefits,
    • Closing the case, or
    • Shortening the certification period.

    The notice must tell the household:

    • the reason for the action,
    • the effective date of the action,
    • their right to request a hearing,
    • their right to continue to receive unchanged benefits pending the hearing,
    • duty to repay benefits over issued pending the hearing,
    • information on receiving free legal advice, and
    • a telephone number to call for questions.

3000.2.5.1 Penalty for Refusing to Cooperate with the State QC Review

  1. End eligibility in the next possible month after you receive the notice of refusal to cooperate. The penalty lasts through the end of the  Federal Fiscal Quarter, plus another 125 days or until the member(s) cooperate, whichever occurs first.
  2. Example: You are notified on July 11, 2018 of refusal to cooperate with state QC. Advance notice expires July 22, 2018. The penalty begins August 1, 2018. If the member does not cooperate, the penalty will end February 2, 2019 (125 days after the end of the federal fiscal year – September 30, 2018).

3000.2.5.2 Penalty for Refusing to Cooperate with the Federal QC Review

  1. End eligibility in the next possible month after you receive the notice of refusal to cooperate. The penalty lasts through the end of the federal fiscal year, plus another 9 months, or until the member(s) cooperate, whichever occurs first.
  2. Example: You are notified on July 11, 2018 of refusal to cooperate with federal QC. Advance notice expires July 22, 2018. The penalty begins August 1, 2018. If the member does not cooperate, the penalty will end June 30, 2019 (9 months after the end of the federal fiscal year – September 30, 2018).

3000.2.5.3 Disqualification Ends

  1. If the household cooperates with the Quality Control review, Quality Control staff will notify the ES. The household must reapply.
  2. The disqualification follows the member who refused to cooperate. The household may reapply when the non-cooperating member leaves the household. If the non-cooperating member enters another 3SVT household, that household is disqualified until the penalty period expires or the member cooperates, whichever occurs first.
  3. If the household reapplies before the penalty period ends, inform the household it must contact QC before benefits can be granted. Proceed with the application. Do not approve benefits until the household complies with QC.
  4. If the household reapplies after the penalty period ends, the household is not required to cooperate with the prior Quality Control review. The household must meet all application verification requirements.

3000.3 Supervisory Case Reviews

  1. In addition to the federally mandated reviews identified above, supervisory case reviews are also completed. If the  SCR has no findings, the ES will get a copy of the SCR to file. If a correction is needed, the ES has 7 days to correct the case and inform their supervisor of actions taken.

2900 Fair Hearings

New information in the manual is highlighted and noted with a . See Manual Updates for details.

Section with expandable text are noted with a  throughout the manual. 

2900.1 Overview

  1. Fair hearings are held when the household, or a representative chosen by the household, makes a hearing request to object to actions taken against the household’s case. The fair hearing is heard before the  HSB and conducted by a  Hearing Officer, appointed by HSB.  The hearing officer provides an impartial and objective review of the agency’s actions. The hearing officer confirms or denies the agency’s actions.
  2. See: Fair Hearing Overview.

2900.2 Notification of Hearing Rights

  1. Households must be notified in writing at application and at an  Adverse Action their right to:
    1. request a hearing,
    2. how to request a hearing,
    3. their right to be represented, and
    4. the names of persons or organizations providing free legal services.   
  2. Remind the household of these rights any time they tell the agency they disagree with an action taken on their case.

2900.3 Hearing Requests

  1. Hearing requests are clear verbal or written notices by the household or representative of their wish to appeal an action taken by the agency.  
  2. Households have the right to request a fair hearing when they disagree with an action taken by the agency.
  3. Ask the household or representative to clarify their hearing request when it is unclear what action is at issue. 
  4. Do not limit or interfere with the household or representative’s right to request a hearing.  
  5. Never coerce or try to influence the household or representative to withdraw their hearing request.

2900.4 Timeframes to Request a Hearing

  1. Households have 90-days to request a hearing from the notice date of:
    1. any action to reduce or terminate benefits, 
    2. a restoration of underpayments, or 
    3. a denial of a restoration of underpayments.
  2. In addition, hearings may be requested anytime during the certification period to dispute the current level of benefits.
  3. Treat hearing requests that are not timely the same as timely requests. Follow the policy for actions after receipt of the request. HSB will determine if the household had good cause for submitting their hearing request late. If so, they will allow a fair hearing on the household’s issue.

2900.5 Receipt Date of the Hearing Request

  1. The receipt date for the hearing request is the date:
    1. the household verbally requests the hearing, 
    2. the date stamp date if the request is mailed to the agency, 
    3. the date stamp date if the request is faxed, or
    4. the household requests to reinstate their hearing request.  

2900.6 Actions After Receipt of the Request

  1. For steps to follow after receipt of a hearing request, see:
    1. ESD Responsibilities, and 
    2. Human Services Board Rules. 

2900.7 Continued Benefits

2900.7.1 Household Requirements

  1. Continue benefits at the amount the household received immediately before the adverse action if the household:
    1. requested a fair hearing before the advance notice effective date, and 
    2. if their certification period has not ended.  
  2. Provide continued benefits even when the household did not indicate if they wish to waive their right to continued benefits.  
    1. Exception: Continued benefits are only allowed for disputed  Federal Rule/Regulation or  Mass Changes when the household believes their benefits were:
      1. computed incorrectly, or
      2. the rules were misapplied/misinterpreted.
  4. Establish a claim if continued benefits were issued when the household or representative withdraws the hearing request before the hearing. 

2900.7.2 When Not to Provide Continued Benefits

  1. Continued benefits are not provided for the following reasons:
    1. Household or their representative waives their right to have the benefits continued. 
    2. Note: CATN if the household chooses to waive continued benefits.
    3. Actions caused by a Federal rule change or mass change. 
      1. Exception: Continued benefits are only allowed for disputed  Federal Rule/Regulation or  Mass Changes when the household believes their benefits were:
        1. computed incorrectly, or
        2. the rules were misapplied/misinterpreted.
    4. Households who, without good cause as determined by HSB, didn’t request a hearing before the adverse action became effective.

2900.7.3 Benefit Changes While the Decision is Pending

  1. Do not reduce or end continued benefits prior to receipt of the hearing decision, except for one of the following reasons:
    1. The certification period ends. The household may reapply/recertify and be determined eligible for a new certification period and new benefit amount.
    2. A preliminary decision is made in writing at the hearing by the  Hearing Officer when the sole issue is on a Federal rule and the agency acted on the rule correctly. 
    3. A change caused an  Adverse Action to the household’s eligibility or benefit amount while the hearing decision is pending, and the household or their representative did not request a hearing for this change action.
    4. A mass change occurs that affects the household’s eligibility or benefit amount, or
    5. The household or its representative verbally withdraws their hearing request and doesn’t reinstate their hearing request.
  2. Send the household an ESD 220, Notice of Decision, if their benefits are reduced or ended when any of the above reasons occurs while the hearing decision is pending.

2900.8 Fair Hearing Timeframes

2900.8.1 Fair Hearing Procedures

  1. Within 60-days of receipt of the fair hearing, unless delayed, the hearing must be:
    1. held,
    2. a decision reached, and
    3. notices of the decision provided to:
      1. the agency, and
      2. the household and/or their representative.  

2900.8.2 Scheduling Fair Hearing

  1. The HSB clerk will notify the household and agency of the scheduled hearing at least ten days before the hearing date, including the:
    1. Date,
    2. Time, and
    3. Place of the hearing.  
  2. The hearing will be scheduled between seven and 30-days from the receipt date of the hearing request.

2900.8.3 Migrant and Seasonal Farmworker Households

  1. Expedite hearing requests from  Migrant Workeror  Seasonal Farmworker households who may not be in the area very long. Process these hearing requests to ensure a decision is made and underpayments are issued, if required, before the household leaves the area.

2900.8.4 Household Requests a Delay

  1. Households can request to delay the scheduled hearing, but not for more than 30-days.  

2900.8.4.1 Decision Action Delayed 

  1. Time limits to act on the decision are extended for as many days as the hearing is delayed at the household’s request. Example:  Household or its representative requests a ten-day extension.  Notification of the decision must be made within 70-days of the hearing request. (60-days normal action period plus the ten-day extension given to the household.)

2900.9 Denial of a Hearing Request

  1. HSB will deny hearing requests when:
    1. The hearing request was not made timely, and HSB decides the household does not have good cause for submitting their request untimely.
    2. The hearing issue is a Federal rule that:
      1. reduces, 
      2. suspends, or
      3. cancels benefits.
  2. Exception: Hearings regarding  Federal Rule/Regulation or Mass Changes are only allowed when the household believes their benefits were:
    1. computed incorrectly, or
    2. the rules were misapplied/misinterpreted.

2900.10 Dismissal of a Hearing Request

  1. Hearing requests are dismissed when the household or representative:
    1. Fails to:
      1. appear for the hearing, and
      2. did not respond within seven days to the HSB clerk’s written inquiry about the household/representative missing the hearing.  
    2. Withdraws the hearing request in writing.
    3. Verbally withdraws the hearing request.  
  2. Note: Send form ESD 113W, 3SquaresVT Fair Hearing Withdrawal to the household and its representative within ten days of a withdrawal. This confirms the withdrawal request and provides the household the chance to reinstate their hearing request.

2900.11 Reinstated Hearing Request

  1. Households and/or their representative may request to have their withdrawn hearing request reinstated. The household must request to reinstate their hearing within ten days of receipt of the ESD 113W confirming the household’s withdrawn hearing request. 
  2. A hearing request may be reinstated once.

2900.12 Hearings  

2900.12.1 Consolidated Hearings

2900.12.1.1 Multiple Households with the Same Issue

  1. Consolidated hearings mean multiple hearing requests from different households regarding the same issue, held together in one hearing, at the same time. Each household or representative must approve having a consolidated hearing. The household is allowed an opportunity to present their own case at the consolidated hearing.

2900.12.1.2 Multiple Issues for the Same Household

  1. The hearing officer may choose to consolidate a single household’s multiple hearing requests into one hearing.

2900.12.2 Agency Actions 

  1. Hearings must be attended by:
    1. AAG, and
    2. the household and/or their representative.
  2. Additionally, the following people may attend the hearing:
    1. agency witnesses, as requested,
    2. household witnesses, as requested, or 
    3. friends or family chosen by the household.  
    4. Note: Hearing officers have the authority to limit the number of persons attending the hearing when space is limited.  
  3. Do not introduce confidential information at the hearing, such as the names and addresses of people who provided information about the hearing issue without the household’s knowledge.

2900.12.3 Household’s Rights 

  1. Allow the household or representative the following rights during the hearing:
    1. Examine all documents and records being used in the hearing.
    2. Present their case or have a representative present their case.
    3. Bring witnesses.
    4. Argue issues without undue interference.
    5. Cross-examine agency witnesses.
    6. Disagree with testimony or evidence.
    7. Submit their own evidence and facts.

2900.13 Hearing Decisions

2900.13.1 Hearing Decisions Procedure

  1. Decisions by the hearing officer comply with Federal law and regulations and become part of the hearing record. Notice of the decision will be provided to the household, their representative and the agency. AAG will email a copy of the decision to the district office.  AOPS will send a copy of form ESD 113C, Notice to Comply to the district handling the fair hearing, providing the date to comply with the hearing decision.

2900.13.2 Decision Upholds the Household

  1. AAG will email the supervisor and ES with the decision if the household is upheld. AOPS will send a copy of form ESD 113C. After receipt of the ESD 113C, send the household and their representative the ESD 220FH, Notice of Decision. 
  2. Benefits must be updated within ten days of receipt of the ESD 113C when the decision increases the household’s benefits. Restore benefits for any months where benefits were underpaid, including if the household waived continued benefits.  
  3. Complete and return the ESD 113C to AOPS when the decision’s ordered actions are completed.
  4. Remove any WARN for this fair hearing issue.
  5. CATN:
    1. your actions, and
    2. that an ACCESS generated ESD 220 was not mailed.

2900.13.3 Decision Upholds the Agency

  1. Benefits must be updated to decrease or end by the next scheduled issuance that follows receipt of the ESD 113C. Send the household an ESD 220FH informing the household of:
    1. the action that will be taken based on the hearing decision, and
    2. the effective date. 
  2. Enter closure code 14 in FIAT section of ACCESS when closing the case based on the hearing decision.
  3. Remove any WARN for this fair hearing issue.
  4. CATN:
    1. your actions, and
    2. that an ACCESS generated ESD 220 was not mailed.

2900.13.4 Establishing a Claim

  1. Establish a claim when:
    1. the household did not waive their right to continued benefits,
    2. the hearing decision upheld the agency’s actions, and
    3. benefits were overpaid.

2900.14 Fair Hearings Regarding Claims

  1. Notify  FACU that the household requested a hearing on the claim. FACU will suspend the claim.
  2. Claims waiting on a fair hearing decision are not delinquent. 
  3. Notify the FACU of the hearing decision. FACU will take action to:
    1. Delete the claim if the household is upheld.
    2. Re-establish the claim and send a manual letter to notify the household, if the claim is upheld.

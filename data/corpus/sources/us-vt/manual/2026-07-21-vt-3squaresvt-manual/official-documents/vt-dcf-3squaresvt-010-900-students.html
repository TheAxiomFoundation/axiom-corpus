900.1 Overview

  1. Students must meet eligible student criteria to be a household member. A student who does not meet these criteria is ineligible for 3SVT benefits. See Non-members.
  2. A student is a person at least 18 years old and under 50 years old who is enrolled at least half-time in post-secondary education institution.
    1. The student is under age 18 until the month following the month they turn 18. 
    2. The student is age 50 the first day of the month they turn 50.
  3. See Student Income for guidance on excluded and countable income. 

900.2 Enrolled

  1. The student is enrolled as of the 1st day of the school term through normal scheduled class periods, vacation, and recess unless they:
    1. graduate;
    2. are suspended, expelled, or drop out; or 
    3. don’t intend to register for the next school term (excluding summer school).

900.3 Half-Time

  1. The school defines the number of credit hours to qualify a student as at least half-time.

900.4 Post-secondary Education Institution

  1. Any public or private educational institution (including correspondence or on-line institutions) which;
    1. normally requires a high school diploma or equivalency certificate for enrollment; or 
    2. admits persons who are beyond the age of compulsory school attendance in Vermont; and
    3. the institution is legally authorized or recognized by Vermont; or
    4. the institution is accredited or pre-accredited by a national accrediting agency. 
  2. Examples: colleges, universities, business, vocational, trade, and technical schools.
  3. An institution of higher education does not include any adult basic education program.
  4. The following are not considered a post-secondary education institution:
    1. New England Center for Circus Arts.

900.5 Eligible Student

900.5.1 Criteria 

  1. To be eligible for 3SVT, a student must meet at least one of the following criteria. 

900.5.2 Age

  1. Under age 18 or age 50 or older.

900.5.3 Caring for a Dependent Child

  1. Responsible for the care of dependent household member under age six;
    1. If two adults are responsible, the exemption is restricted to one person per child.
  2. Responsible for the care of a dependent household member at least age six but under age 12 if the student does not have adequate child care; or
  3. Full-time student who is a single parent responsible for the care of dependent child under the age of 12.
    1. The single parent and child must be in the same 3SVT household.
    2. Includes an adult with Parental Control over the child as long as the adult is not residing with a spouse.

900.5.4 Employment

  1. Paid for working at least an average of 20-hours a week;
  2. Note: Unpaid hours due to holiday or illness are in the 20-hour calculation; or
  3. Self-employed at least 20-hours a week and income after allowable expenses is at least equal to the Federal minimum wage multiplied by 20-hours a week.

900.5.5 Physically or Mentally Unfit for Work

  1. Meets work registration definition of physically or mentally unfit.

900.5.6 Reach Up Participant

  1. The student gets cash benefits from the Reach Up program.

900.5.7 Training

  1. The student is assigned or placed in school through or complying with:
    1.  WIOA;
    2. a program under Section 236 of the Trade Act of 1974, which includes  TAA and  TRA; 
    3. a 3SVT employment and training program, see Individual Career Advancement Network (ICAN); or
      1. Email the ICAN email group to confirm compliance. 
    4. an on-the-job training program where the employer pays wages to the student for the training. 
  2. Self-initiated education placements while enrolled in a training program are considered complying with the program’s requirements.
  3. Customers who voluntarily participate in an approved employment and training program and are placed in an institution of higher education meet this criteria. 

900.5.8 Work Study

  1. The student is participating in a state or federally funded work study program.
    1. The student must be approved for work study at the time of application;
    2. The work study must be approved for the school term; and
    3. the student must anticipate working during that time.
  2. Once allowed, the exemption continues until:
    1. the end of the month in which the school term ends;
    2. it is reported the student refused a work study assignment; or
    3. there is a break between terms of a full month or longer unless the student participates during the break.

900.5.9 Career and Technical Education Program under the Carl D. Perkins Career and Technical Education Improvement Act of 2006

  1. The student is enrolled in one of the following Community College of Vermont programs:
    1. any two-year associate degree program, with the exception of the liberal studies program; or
    2. any certificate program.  

900.6 Verification

  1. Verify the student’s enrollment status. Verify the student’s eligible exemption criteria.
  2. Exception: verify caring for a dependent child only if questionable.

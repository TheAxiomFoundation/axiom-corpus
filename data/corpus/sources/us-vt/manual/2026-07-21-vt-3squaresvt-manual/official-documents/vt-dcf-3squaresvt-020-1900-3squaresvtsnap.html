1900.1 General Information

  1. The following notes the differences between 3SVT and 3SquaresVT in a SNAP:
    1. Simplified application
    2. 36-month certification period
    3. No interim report
    4. Self-declared eligibility information except:
      1. Non-citizen status;
      2. Child support paid; 
      3. Medical expenses;
      4. Questionable information; and
      5. General Work Requirement Exemptions that Require Verification and Time Limited Benefits Work Requirement Verification.
    5. Reporting Requirements
    6. No recertification interview except when:
      1. Eligibility results in a denial;
      2. Information supplied by the household is questionable, incomplete, or contradictory; or
      3. Household requests an interview.

1900.2 Eligible Household Criteria

  1. Only the following households are eligible for 3SquaresVT in a SNAP:
    1. All household members are  Senior or  disableddisabled; and

      A person who meets one or more of the following:

      • Receives disability or blindness benefits from any of these programs: Social Security,   SSI, or   MABD.
        • A person meets this even when the full benefit is withheld or deferred due to recoupment.
      • Receives a federally or state administered SSI supplement based on disability or blindness, or section 212(a) of PL 93-66.
      • Receives a disability retirement benefit from a government agency for a   disability considered permanent by SSAdisability considered permanent by SSA.

        Any of the following conditions:

        • Permanent loss of use of both hands, both feet, or one hand and one foot.
        • Amputation of leg at hip. Amputation of leg or foot because of diabetes or peripheral vascular diseases.
        • Total deafness, not correctable by surgery or hearing aid. Statutory blindness, unless caused by cataracts or detached retina.
        • IQ of 59 or less, established after the person becomes 16 years old.
        • Spinal cord or nerve root lesions resulting in paraplegia or quadriplegia.
        • Multiple sclerosis with damage to the nervous system caused by scattered areas of inflammation. The inflammation recurs and has progressed to varied interference with the function of the nervous system, including severe muscle weakness, paralysis, and vision and speech defects.
        • Muscular dystrophy with irreversible wasting of the muscles, impairing the ability to use the arms or legs.
        • Impaired renal function caused by chronic renal disease, resulting in severely reduced function which may require dialysis or kidney transplant.
        • Amputation of a limb of a person at least 55 years old.
      • Is a veteran the VA considers totally (100%) disabled or permanently housebound or in need of regular aid and attendance.
      • Is a veteran’s surviving spouse who the VA considers:
        • in need of regular aid and attendance, or
        • permanently housebound, or
        • approved for benefits because of the veteran’s death and has a disability considered permanent by SSA.
      • Is a veteran’s surviving child who the VA considers:
        • incapable of self-support, or
        • approved for benefits because of the veteran’s death and has a disability considered permanent by SSA
    2. All household members have no  Earned Income in the month of application.
  2. Note: Non-countable earned income including in-kind and self-employment that results in a loss or net zero is earned income.
  3. Note: The following is not earned income: unpaid volunteer, unpaid employment and training program participation, ICAN, unpaid workfare participation.
  4. Note: RUFA and EP households are not eligible for 3SquaresVT in a SNAP.

1900.3 Application

1900.3.1 Overview

  1. Screen all 3SVT applications to see if they meet 3SquaresVT in a SNAP Eligible Household Criteria.  
  2. 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households have the option to complete a simplified application, the ESD 202 3SNP. If a 3SquaresVT in a SNAP application is submitted for a household that is not 3SquaresVT in a SNAP eligible, the ES will CATN all 202 questions asked during the interview that are not already included on the 202 3SNP.

1900.3.2 Interviews

  1. 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households must complete an interview at initial application. The ADPC does not send the ESD 202C when a 3SquaresVT in a SNAP application is received by them. The ES will send the ESD 202C after two cold calls are unsuccessful.
  2. 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households are entitled to Expedited Service if expedited service criteria is met.

1900.3.3 Verification

  1. Use data matches to verify income. Allow self-declaration of income that cannot be verified through data matches. Only verify income if questionable. 
  2. Allow the household to self-declare all information except for:
    1.  Non-Citizen status,
    2. Child support paid,
    3. Medical expenses greater than $35 per month
    4. Questionable information , and
    5. General Work Requirement Exemptions that Require Verification and Time Limited Benefits Work Requirement Verification.
  3. Complete data matches including but not limited to:
    1. SVES,
    2. eDRS,
    3. SAVES,
    4. PVS.
  4. Required verification must be provided before approving the application.  
  5. Exception: Expedited Service.

1900.4 3SquaresVT in a SNAP Simplified Reporting

1900.4.1 Overview

  1. 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households are required to report fewer changes during their certification period than other programs. 3SquaresVT in a SNAP reporting has two parts:
    1. Reduced 3SquaresVT in a SNAP reporting requirement, and
    2. No Interim Report.
  2. Households are not required to report any other changes that may occur at the same time.

1900.4.2 3SquaresVT in a SNAP Reporting Requirement Notification

  1. Provide the following reporting requirement information to the household, both verbally and in writing at application and recertification.
    1. How 3SquaresVT in a SNAP reporting works, and
    2. Reporting requirements, including:
      1. What to report
      2. What to verify

1900.4.3 Reduced 3SquaresVT in a SNAP Reporting Requirements

  1. Households must report the following changes:
    1. Changes to Household Composition,
    2.  Earned Income
  2. Households must report these changes within ten days of the date the change occurred. Note: If they have reported by the tenth day of the month following the month of the change, they have reported timely. 
  3. Changes may be reported:
    1. In writing,
    2. By telephone, or
    3. In-person
  4. Unscheduled reporting of changes can be done at any time but is not required. See Change Processing.
  5. Households may also report these changes using form ESD 200, Change Report.

1900.6 Conversion from 3SVT to 3SquaresVT in a SNAP 

1900.6.1 Overview

  1. Convert 3SVT households that meet 3SquaresVT in a SNAP eligibility at interim report or recertification. 

1900.6.2 Conversion at Interim Report

  1. Convert 3SVT households that meet 3SquaresVT in a SNAP eligibility at interim report. Extend the certification period to a total of 36-months from last application and interview.
  2. No interview is required. 

1900.6.2.1 12-month Certification Period

  1. Complete a cold call to explain 3SquaresVT in a SNAP and 3SquaresVT in a SNAP Reporting Requirements. If the participant does not answer the phone, CATN the call attempt. Convert the case to 3SquaresVT in a SNAP. Extend the original certification date by 24 months for a total of 36 months from last application and interview. Extend the existing FMED panel to line up with the new certification date. 

1900.6.2.2 24-month Certification Period 

  1. Complete a cold call to explain 3SquaresVT in a SNAP and 3SquaresVT in a SNAP Reporting Requirements. If the participant does not answer the phone, CATN the call attempt. Convert the case to 3SquaresVT in a SNAP. Extend the original certification date by 12 months for a total of 36 months from last application and interview. Extend the existing FMED panel to line up with the new certification date.

1900.6.3 Conversion at Recertification

  1. When a 3SVT household completes a recertification, screen the household for 3SquaresVT in a SNAP eligibility. Follow the steps in 3SquaresVT in a SNAP Application. 
  2. Note: An Interview is required to convert to 3SquaresVT in a SNAP at 3SVT recertification.

1900.6.4 Notification

  1. At conversion to 3SquaresVT in a SNAP, the notice of decision will state:
    1. interview information; 
    2. reminder of Reporting Requirements;
    3. reminder of no Interim Report; and
    4. information on the new certification period.

1900.7 Conversion from 3SquaresVT in a SNAP to 3SVT

  1. When a household previously certified for 3SquaresVT in a SNAP reports a change that makes them no longer eligible for 3SquaresVT in a SNAP, the household will be converted to 3SVT. Converted households will:
    1. receive an Interim Report at six-month intervals;
    2. receive a conversion to 3SVT notice;
    3. maintain their current 36-month certification period; and
    4. are subject to Simplified Reporting. 
  2. At the end of the 36-month certification period, the household will receive a new Certification Period based on current circumstances. See Recertification. 

1900.8 Recertification

1900.8.1 Overview

  1. Households will receive a notice to reapply the month prior to the end of their current period. All applications received within 60 days prior to the end of the certification will be used as a recertification. The ESD 202RL, Reminder Letter is sent six weeks prior to its due date. It is computer generated, printed, and mailed by the ADPC. The 202RL includes:
    1. ESD 202RL
    2. ESD 202 3SNP REV
  2. The household will auto close if by the 15th of the last month of certification it fails to complete the recertification. 

1900.8.2 Interviews

  1. Interviews are not required unless:
    1. Eligibility results in a denial;
    2. Information supplied by the household is questionable, incomplete, or contradictory; or
    3. Household requests an interview.
  2. If an interview is required, see Interviews. 
  3. The Oral Explanation of the work requirements is provided to households that include individuals subject to the general and time limited benefits work requirements. One collateral call to the household is required and must be documented in CATN.

1900.8.3 Verification at Recertification

  1. Households must verify the following:
    1. Legally obligated child support changes. 
    2. Medical expenses for households claiming actual medical expenses more than $191, or who wish to switch from the standard.
    3. The first $35.01 of medical expenses when the household wishes to use the standard medical expense deduction. CATN the household’s decision to use the standard deduction if their actual expenses are more than $191. 
    4. Note: Accept the household’s statement regarding their medical expenses if they wish to continue to receive the standard medical expense deduction, and verification of their medical expenses is visible in OnBase.  The first $35.01 in medical expenses must be verified if not visible in OnBase. 
    5. Questionable information.
    6. Time limited participant's work hours.
    7. Work status and exemptions, if not previously verified.

400.1 Verification

400.1.1 Overview

  1. Verification is used to confirm the accuracy of statements or information. Verification may be submitted by mail, fax, in-person or by telephone for collateral contact verifications. 
  2. Each item specifies the verification requirements at application, recertification, and reported changes.

400.1.2 Household Responsibility

  1. The household has the primary responsibility to provide verification to support statements on the application and to resolve any questionable information.
  2. If a household refuses to provide required verification or fails to respond to a request for verification without good cause, take the following actions:
    1. Applications: Deny for failure to cooperate; or approve benefits without the expense.
    2. Recipients: Close the case or adjust benefits as specified in the eligibility item that changed.
  3. CATN why the action was taken.

400.1.3 Agency Assistance 

  1. If the household reports difficulty in getting verification, or requests help to get the verification, the agency must offer to help. 

400.1.4 Request for Verification

  1. Request verification in writing. You may discuss the verification needed with the household, but you must follow up in writing. In the written verification request, the due date is 10 days from the date the request is typed. If issued on a weekend, still use 10 days. Allow two full business days after the due date before determining non-cooperation. Use Form ESD 202VCR, Verification Change Request.
  2. Avoid requesting unnecessary verification, rejecting applications, or closing cases when verification is already available from electronic sources, imaging, or has been submitted as a hard copy.
  3. See Examples of Accecptable Verification.

400.2 Verification Sources

400.2.1 Sources of Verification Include

  1. Written evidence
  2. Collateral contacts
  3. Home visits
  4. Automated resources
  5. Self-Declaration

400.2.2 Written Evidence

  1. The most common verification is written evidence of the household’s circumstances, such as wage stubs, rental agreements, award letters, etc. 

400.2.3 Collateral Contacts

  1. A collateral contact may be used instead of written evidence. A collateral contact is an oral confirmation of the household’s circumstances by a person outside of the household. Make the collateral contact by telephone or in person with anyone who can be expected to give reliable information. Acceptable collateral contacts include, but are not limited to:
    1. Employers
    2. Landlords
    3. Dependent care providers
    4. Community Service Agencies

400.2.4 Home Visits

  1. Home visits are also an acceptable verification if written evidence or collateral contacts are not sufficient to make an eligibility determination. The home visit must always be scheduled in advance with the household. CATN why the home visit was completed. 

400.2.5 Automated Resources

  1. Use automated resources for verification, whenever possible. However,
    1. Give the household the opportunity to provide other verification if they disagree with the automated information; and
    2. Do not delay timely authorization of benefits if automated resources are unavailable.
  2. Automated matches are only used to obtain information on applicants, recipients, ineligible members and disqualified household members whose income and resources are needed to determine household eligibility. 

400.2.5.1 Income Eligibility and Verification System (IEVS)

  1. IEVS has the following matches using the applicant's or recipient's SSN to obtain income and resource information.  Household members, including a disqualified or ineligible household member, are included in the match request.
    1. Vermont Department of Labor
      1. Quarterly wage, run monthly
      2.  UCB, ran twice a month
    2. SSA  BENDEX, run monthly
      1.  RSDI benefits
      2. Medicare Part A and Part B payer and premium amount
    3.   SDX
      1.  SSI, run daily
    4.  IRS
      1. 1099 income: Income reported to IRS that is not from wages such as, but not limited to: interest, dividends, cash prizes, royalties, pensions, annuities, contract income. The match is run monthly.
  2. Review all information available from data exchanges within 10 days of notification of a match. Compare it to case record information and act on the information, as required.
  3. Information provided by BENDEX, SDX, UCB, and SAVE is "verified upon receipt". Use this information without further verification unless the household disagrees with the information and provides alternate verification.
  4. Quarterly wages and IRS information reported must be reviewed to determine if the income was unreported. If more information is needed, request verification from the household or third-party source. Allow at least 10 days for the household to return the information. 

400.2.5.2 Electronic Disqualified Recipient System (EDRS)

  1.  eDRS is a nationwide database of persons disqualified for an Intentional Program Violation. 
  2. Complete a match at application and when a new adult member is added to the 3SVT household. Complete a match for each household member age 18 and under age 80.

400.2.5.3 National Directory of New Hire (NDNH)

  1. A monthly match from a national directory that identifies wage and income information. 
  2. Complete a match at application and recertification on members 18 years of age or older.

400.2.5.4 Deceased Person Match

  1. The deceased person match is completed through the automated match with  SVES,  BENDEX, and  SDX.  
  2. If a match occurs, verification is required prior to removing the person from 3SVT benefits. The deceased person is not eligible for 3SVT. 
  3. Note: Close the case for the entire household when the head of household is deceased. Transfer the case into a surviving household member's name. Retain the original recertification date. Do not require a new application.

400.2.5.5 PARIS

  1.  PARIS is a quarterly match to verify income from:
    1. Veteran’s Administration 
    2. Department of Defense – including active or retired military and Federal employees 
  2. Also verifies out-of-state participation in:
    1. Medicaid
    2. TANF
    3. Worker’s Compensation
    4. Child Care, and
    5.  SNAP
  3. If a match occurs, additional verification is required. Fraud and Claims Unit handles this. 

400.2.5.6 Prisoner Verification System (PVS)

  1.  PVS is completed manually by the Benefit Programs Specialist through INFC/D/SVES. The incarcerated individual will not be eligible for 3SVT if they are incarcerated for longer than 30 days. 
  2. If a match occurs, additional verification is required. 

400.2.5.7 Systematic Alien Verification for Entitlements (SAVE)

  1.  SAVE is a program that verifies the validity of non-citizen immigrant documents. After the USCIS documentation is received, use the SAVE system to verify the documentation is accurate.  
  2. Exception: American Indians may not be listed on SAVE. 

400.2.5.8 Department of Corrections (DOC) Match via Department of Vermont Health ACCESS (DVHA)

  1. The DOC match occurs when DOC notifies DVHA that an individual has been incarcerated for 28 days. DVHA notifies ESD by entering information into ACCESS.
  2. If a match is received, additional verification is required. 

400.2.6 Self-Declaration 

  1. Oral or written confirmation of the household’s circumstances from an individual in the household.
  2. Self-declaration of required verification should be the last resort and should only be accepted after all other options have been attempted. 
  3. Information typically requiring verification from another source can be taken self declared after the household and ESD have attempted to gather the information. This includes: 
    1. Income
    2. Deductions
    3. Questionable Information
  4. Note: Self-Employment income and expenses can be taken self declared when taxes are not available or completed for the business. 

400.3 Case Notes

  1. The goal of case notes is to establish a clear and complete record that follows the case actions. Complete case notes CATN to support eligibility, ineligibility, and benefit level determinations. 
  2. Provide a clear explanation in CATN of what you did and why you did it, including:
    1. Information used to determine eligibility and benefit amount including:
      1. the source of information; 
      2. the verification that supports the information;
      3. the method used to determine monthly income and expense amounts such as conversion, averaging, etc.; and 
      4. why the information was used.
    2. Actions taken and the reasons to support those actions, such as, but not limited to:
      1. Differences: In order of the STAT, the resolution of any differences between information provided at the interview and the ESD 202, Application.
      2. Questionable Information: Why is it questionable and how was it resolved? 
      3. Collateral Contacts: who the collateral contact is, the telephone number, date of the contact, and the information the collateral contact provided.  
      4. Interviews: who was interviewed, when the interview was completed, how completed (in-person or telephone).

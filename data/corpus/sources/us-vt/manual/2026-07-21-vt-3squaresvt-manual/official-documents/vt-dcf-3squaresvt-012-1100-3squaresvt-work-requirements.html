1100.1 Overview

  1. The 3SVT program has two different types of work requirements:
    1. General work requirements
    2. Time limited benefits work requirements

1100.2 Procedure and Notification 

  1. The following must be completed at application, recertification, when changes are reported, and when a member is added to the household:

    1. Determine the general work requirements and time limited benefits work requirements status of each person in the household.
      2. Note: Some exemptions require verification. See: 
        1. General work requirements verification requirements. 
        2. Time limited benefits work requirements verification requirements. 
    2. Explain the work requirements, exemptions, and procedures to customers subject to the general work requirements or time limited benefits work requirements. See Oral Explanation.
      2. ACCESS will generate the Consolidated Work Notice.

1100.3 Consolidated Work Notice and Oral Explanation 

1100.3.1 Consolidated Work Notice 

  1. The Consolidated Work Notice is a written notice sent identifying the members subject to the general and time limited benefits work requirements.
  2. This written notice must be provided at:
    1. Application approval;
    2. Recertification approval;
    3. When a member loses an exemption; and 
    4. When a new member is added and is subject to work requirements.
    5. Note: To mail the notice at another time enter "Issue 3SVT Work Requirement Notice" into CATN and the notice will generate over night. 
  3. Note: If all members are exempt from the time limited benefits work requirements, the notice would include only the general work requirement information.
  4. The notice must:
    1. Identify which members are subject to which work requirements.
    2. Include the following general work requirements information:
      1. How to meet;
      2. What happens if someone does not meet;
      3. How to be exempt; and
      4. What is considered good cause.
    3. Include the following time limited benefits work requirements information as appropriate:
      1. What the time limited benefits work requirements are; 
      2. How to meet;
      3. What happens if someone does not meet;
      4. How to be exempt; and
      5. What is considered good cause.

1100.3.2 Oral Explanation

  1. The Oral explanation of the work requirements is provided to households that include individuals subject to the general and time limited benefits work requirements.
  2. Provide to the household at:
    1. Application Interview;
    2. Recertification Interview; 
    3. When a member loses an exemption; and 
    4. When a new member is added and is subject to work requirements.
    5. Note: Provide the oral explanation at the time the change is reported verbally. For changes reported in writing or discovered by ESD, one collateral call to the household is required and must be documented in CATN. 
  3. Oral explanation must:
    1. Identify which members are subject to which work requirements.
    2. Include the following general work requirements information:
      1. How to meet;
      2. What happens if someone does not meet;
      3. How to be exempt; and
      4. What is considered good cause.
    3. Include the following time limited benefits work requirements information as appropriate:
      1. What the time limited benefits work requirements are;
      2. How to meet;
      3. What happens if someone does not meet;
      4. How to be exempt; and
      5. What is considered good cause.

1100.4 Individual Career Advancement Network (ICAN)

1100.4.1 ICAN Qualification Procedures

  1.  ICAN is Vermont’s  Employment and Training Program. Customers who want to get a job or gain skills to increase employability may qualify for ICAN. To qualify, a customer must be active 3SVT and cannot be receiving:
    1. Reach First;
    2. Reach Up;
    3. Reach Ahead; or
    4.  PSE.
  2. Note: Households eligible for Reach Up but not receiving it will be required to apply to be eligible for ICAN participant reimbursements.  
  3. Educate qualifying customers about ICAN during the interview at application and recertification. The household must be informed of the following:
    1. What ICAN is;
    2. ICAN activities;
    3. Participant reimbursements; and
    4. Rights and responsibilities, if applicable.
  4. A customer interested in ICAN must be given an ICAN referral.
  5. Note: Inform customers who are over the age of 60 or disabled of ICAN only if they express interest in getting a job. See ICAN Process Flowchart and Individual Career Advancement Network: Quick Reference guide for process and referral guidance.
  6. Participation in ICAN is voluntary for all 3SVT recipients.
  7. Encourage 3SVT customers to go to the ICAN webpage for detailed information about employment services, available opportunities, and to view the ICAN orientation.
  8. If the household includes a customer with time limited benefits, see 3SquaresVT Time Limited Benefits.

1100.4.2 ICAN Referral 

  1. The ESD 218FS-1 Individual Career Advancement Network (ICAN) is the form used to refer customers to ICAN orientation.
  2. The form notifies the customer:
    1. How to complete ICAN orientation; 
    2. Details regarding ICAN activities; and
    3. How ICAN may help someone meet their work requirements. 
  3. Mail the ESD 218FS-1 if the household is interested in ICAN.
     

1100.4.3 ICAN Participation

1100.4.3.1 Participation Requirements

  1. To participate in ICAN customers must: 
    1. Complete the ICAN orientation;
    2. Complete a Career Assessment and plan;
    3. Engage with ICAN providers according to career plan; and
    4. Provide verification as required for participant reimbursements.
  2. Note: Participant reimbursements are available for specific documented out of pocket costs. Examples: Transportation and dependent care.

1100.4.3.2 ICAN Activities 

  1. ICAN has six allowable activities (also called components):
    1. Supervised Job Search
      1. Receive support during job search activities.
    2. Job Search Training
      1. Enhanced job search readiness programs.
      2. Learn job seeking techniques.
    3. Self-Employment Training
      1. Assistance in starting or growing a small business.
    4. Education
      1. GED, High School Diploma, Certificate Programs or Higher Education.
    5. Work Experience 
      1. Provides actual work experience and training.
    6. Job Retention Services 
      1. Allowed for 90 days after a participant has gained employment.
      2. Must have participated in an ICAN activity prior to being employed.
      3. Participants are eligible for continued support from ICAN case managers/providers and participant reimbursements while engaged in job retention services.

1100.4.3.3 ICAN Participant Reimbursements 

  1. Participant reimbursements are payments or reimbursements for allowable costs that are reasonable, necessary, and directly related to ICAN participation. 
    1. A cost is allowable if it is identified in the current spending guidelines. 
    2. A cost is reasonable if it’s what someone else would pay in the same situation.
    3. A cost is necessary and directly related if it’s essential to participating in approved ICAN activities
  2. Participant reimbursements aren't guaranteed and are subject to available funding; they're temporary and are meant to help with costs related to ICAN participation.
  3. Participant reimbursements can include transportation, dependent care and other necessary cost to participate in the program. 
  4.  

    Participant reimbursements cannot:

    • Be available through another government program or available at no cost through a private source; 
      • Customers will be required to apply to other state or local programs to be eligible for specific reimbursements. This includes but is not limited to: Reach Up, Unemployment Benefits, Fuel Assistance, Crisis Fuel, HireAbility Vermont, Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA), Microbusiness Development Programs, and Childcare Assistance programs.  
    • Be used to support  regular employment unless enrolled in the job retention component and the participant reimbursements are linked to participation in that component; 
    • Be used to pay bad debt such as outstanding fines or fees; and
    • Be used to make on-going regular payments.
    • Note: If a participant reimbursement request is denied the ICAN team will send a denial notice. Any customer with questions about a denial must have their information forwarded to the ICAN email for follow up. 

1100.4.3.4 ICAN Provider Determination 

  1. An ICAN provider determination  is when an ICAN provider determines that a customer is not a good fit for participation in their available ICAN services and components.    
  2. When a provider determination is made a customer is no longer able to access ICAN services from that provider unless re-screened and referred by ESD. 
  3. The ICAN provider will submit an ICAN provider determination (PD) form to the ICAN email within 10 days of the determination. 
  4. ESD will send the customer a provider determination notice. The notice will include the following information: 
    1. What a provider determination is;
    2. The next steps the state will take;
    3. Confirmation the individual is not being sanctioned; and 
    4. TLBWR information if applicable. 
      1. Note: TLBWR customers are not exempt from the work requirement because of a provider determination. They must continue to meet their work requirements. 
  5. At recertification, or sooner at the customer’s request:
    1. Screen and refer the individual to ICAN.
    2. Screen for an exemption from the work requirements due to the customer’s physical and mental fitness. 
    3. Coordinate with other Federal, State, or local employment and training programs to identify work opportunities or assistance for the individual. 
      1. Vermont Department of Labor: (802) 828 – 4394 or online at https://labor.vermont.gov/JobSearchVermont 
      2. Vermont 211: Dial 2-1-1 or online at https://vermont211.org/
      3. HireAbility Vermont find local contact information at https://www.hireabilityvt.com/get-started/
      4. Local Community Action Agencies find local resources at https://dcf.vermont.gov/contacts/partners/caps

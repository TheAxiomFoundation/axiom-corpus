2400 Recertification

New information in the manual is highlighted and noted with a . See Manual Updates for details.

Section with expandable text are noted with a  throughout the manual. 

2400.1 Overview

  1. Recertification is a review of the household’s eligibility factors to determine their continued eligibility for 3SVT benefits.  
  2. A recertification is complete when the household:
    1. completes and files a signed ESD 202, Application,
    2. completes an interview, and 
    3. provides all required verifications.
  3. Exception: 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households may not require an interview. See Interviews.

2400.2 Notice to Reapply

  1. Households must receive a notice to reapply in the month before the last month of their current certification period.  The ESD 202RL, Reminder Letter, is the notice to reapply. It is computer generated, printed and mailed by  ADPC six weeks before its due date. 
  2. Mail the following forms to the  Head of Household:
    1. ESD 202RL, 
    2. ESD 201A, Agreement to Report Change, and
    3. ESD 202, Review, Application.
  3. Exception: 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households do not receive the ESD 201A.
  4. The household has failed to co-operate if by the 15th of the last month of their certification it fails to:
    1. complete the ESD 202 Review, Application, or
    2. complete an ESD 202, Application, and
    3. contact the office to indicate a problem obtaining verification.
  5. The case will auto-close at the end of the certification period.  ACCESS will trigger the auto-closure letter.

2400.3 Timely Filing

  1. The household must submit either a signed ESD 202REV, ESD 202, or ESD 202 3SNP REV by the 15th of the last month of their certification period.
  2. Note: In the rest of this item when an ESD 202 is referenced it means either form.
  3. Note: Use applications received within 60 days prior to the end of the certification period as a recertification. Do not use applications received prior to this as a recertification. 

2400.4 Interviews

  1. Complete a telephone interview for all households at recertification. Send the ESD 202C, ESD Benefits Interview Appointment Notice, when you receive the ESD 202. Allow the household five calendar days to complete the interview. This will give the household ten days to provide verification before the end of the certification period.
  2. Note: The ADPC sends the ESD 202C for applications received by them.
  3. Exception: Complete a face-to-face interview for the following reasons:
    1. Household or authorized representative request.
    2. Needed to resolve or clarify specific issues.
  4. CATN if the interview was completed by telephone or face-to-face.
  5. Do not complete an interview for 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households at recertification unless:
    1. Eligibility results in a denial;
    2. Information supplied by the household is questionable, incomplete, or contradictory; or
    3. Household requests an interview.

2400.5 Timely Processing

  1. A household that completes all of the following actions and is found eligible is entitled to  Uninterrupted Benefits:
    1. Submits a signed ESD 202 no later than the 15th of the last month of their certification period.
    2. Completed the interview at least ten days before the end of the last month of their certification period.
      1. Note: This may not apply to a 3SquaresVT in a SNAP household.
    3. Provided all required verifications before the end of the last month of their certification period.
  2. Notify the household of eligibility or denial by the end of the current certification period. 3SquaresVT in a SNAP eligibility resulting in a denial requires an interview prior to denying the case.
  3. Note: See Agency Caused Delays if timely processing is not met.

2400.6 Delayed Processing

2400.6.1 Agency Caused Delays

  1. Households that submitted an application timely for recertification, but were not timely processed, must be:
    1. previously sent an ESD 202RL, 
    2. authorized benefits the same day they are determined eligible;  
    3. issued a full month’s benefit;  
    4. issued underpayments when the household could not participate the first month of the new certification period. 

2400.6.2 Household Caused Delays

2400.6.2.1 Loss of Uninterrupted Benefits

  1. Households lose their right to uninterrupted benefits when they fail to:
    1. submit a signed ESD 202 by the 15th of the last month of their certification period, or 
    2. complete their interview, or
      1. Note: This may not apply to 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households.
    3. timely submit required verifications.

2400.6.2.2 Failure to Complete the Interview

  1. Households who submit their ESD 202 but do not complete their interview must have been sent:
    1. ESD 202RL; 
    2. ESD 202C, allowing five days to complete their interview;
    3. ESD 202D, Notice of Missed Interview (NOMI), within 24 hours of the missed interview.  The household is responsible to reschedule any missed interviews.
  2. When issuing a NOMI, the "call before date" is:
    1. the last day of the certification period if issuing on or before adverse action, or
    2. the 30th day from the application date if issuing after adverse action. 
  3. Exception: 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households may not require an interview. See Interviews.
  4. ACCESS closes the case for failure to complete the recertification if the interview is not completed. If the household then completes the interview and provides all verification before the end of the certification period:
    1. Determine eligibility. 
    2. If eligible, reinstate the case and provide a full month’s benefits within 30 days of the application date, or ten days from the date the interview is completed, whichever is later.  
  5. Note: A new application for recertification is not required.

2400.6.2.3 Failure to Provide Verification

  1. Households who submit their ESD 202 and complete their interview, but do not provide all required verification by the ESD 202RL2, Verification Review Letter, due date are denied the day after the due date, provided the due date:
    1. allowed ten days after the interview and,
    2. was before the end of the certification period.
  2. If the household then provides the required verification before the end of the certification period:
    1. Determine eligibility. 
    2. If eligible, reinstate the case and provide a full month’s benefits within 30 days of the application date, or ten days from the date the verification is provided, whichever is later.  
  3. Note: A new application for recertification is not required.  

2400.6.2.4 Actions Completed After the Certification Period Expires

  1. Households that:
    1. applied before the end of their certification period, and 
    2. completed the interview during the certification period or within 30 days after the end of the certification period, and
      1. Note: This may not apply to 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households. 
    3. provides required verification within 10 days of the interview or within 30 days after the end of the certification period, whichever is later, must be reopened, if eligible. Provide a full month’s benefits. 
  2. Process applications for recertification received within 30 days of the end of the certification period like an initial application except:
    1. use verification at recertification requirements, and 
    2. enter “R” as the FS Receipt Type, and
    3. follow 3SquaresVT in a SNAP interview requirements at recertification.
  3. Screen for and provide expedited service, if eligible.  Prorate benefits from the date of application.
  4. Exception: Do not prorate benefits for   Migrant Worker or Seasonal Farmworker households who complete the recertification process or apply for recertification within 30 days of the end of the certification period. 
  5. Note: If the household wants to complete the recertification more than 30 days after the end of the certification period, it must reapply. Process the application as an initial application, including screening for expedited service. See Application and Expedited Service.

2400.7 Determining Eligibility

2400.7.1 Procedure for Determining Eligibility

  1. Determine eligibility based on circumstances anticipated for the certification period starting the month following the last month of the current certification period.
  2. Review CATN and TODO for case information, inconsistencies, or verification not previously provided that are due at recertification.  
  3. Use information reported on the ESD 202, obtained during the interview, and any required verifications to determine the household’s eligibility for the new certification period.  
  4. Clear any outstanding data matches.
  5. Budget the household’s income and deductions following Determining Income and Deductions policy.   
  6. Keep the   Head of Household at recertification, unless the household selects someone else.
  7. Disqualify members who voluntarily quit or reduce work hours, without good cause, in the last month of the certification period. Begin the disqualification the first month of the recertification period.
  8. Benefits are not issued for the recertification period before the previous certification period ends. Households are not eligible for expedited service if the household files an application for recertification before the end of their current certification period. Households that file applications after the end of their current certification period must receive expedited service, if eligible. See Expedited Service.

2400.7.2 Destitute Households

  1. Destitute household rules apply for the first month of the recertification period for migrant or seasonal farmworkers.
  2. Exclude income from a new source the first month of the recertification period when the income:
    1. is less than $25, and
    2. will not be received by the tenth calendar day from the 3SVT issuance.
  3. See Destitute Defined.

2400.7.3 3SVT Work Requirements

  1. Review the following at recertification:
    1. General work requirement exemptions, including but not limited to:
      1. Children who turned 16 years of age during the current certification period.
      2. Parents caring for a child who turned six during the current certification period.
    2. Time Limited benefit status, including but not limited to:
      1. Customers who met their work requirement with a work program or workfare must verify monthly compliance for each month of the last certification period.  
      2. Complete an ICAN referral for any participant not enrolled.
  2. Update general work requirement and time limited benefit work requirement exemptions, if needed.  
  3. Complete the Oral Explanation for required households. 

2400.8 Certification Periods

  1. Assign a new certification period to eligible households. See Certification Periods.
  2. Begin the new certification period the month following the last month of the previous certification period.

2400.9 Reporting Requirements

  1. Explain the household’s reporting requirement. See Reduced Reporting Requirements or 3SquaresVT in a SNAP Reporting Requirements. 

2400.10 Verification at Recertification for Non-3SquaresVT in a SNAP

  1. Households must verify the following:
    1. Time limited participant’s work hours.
    2. Work status and exemptions, if not previously verified.
    3.   SSN or proof of application for an SSN for newborns older than six months of age, if not previously provided.
    4. Unearned income that has changed by $50 or more per household 
    5. Earned Income.
    6. Income from a new source. 
    7. Income stopped.
    8. Income and resource information of the non-citizen sponsor and sponsor’s spouse.
    9. Legally obligated child support changes.
    10. Medical expenses for households claiming actual medical expenses more than $151, or who wish to switch from the standard.
    11. The first $35.01 of medical expenses when the household wishes to use the standard medical expense deduction. CATN the household’s decision to use the standard deduction if their actual expenses are more than $151.
    12. Note: Accept the household’s statement regarding their medical expenses if they wish to continue to receive the standard medical expense deduction, and verification of their medical expenses is visible in OnBase. The first $35.01 in medical expenses must be verified if not visible in OnBase.
    13. Questionable information.
    14. Income changes due to unscheduled changes benefit decreases.
  2. Use form 202RL2 to request required verification if the application was received on or before the last day of the certification month. 
  3. Use form 202V to request required verification if the application was received after the last day of the certification month. 

2400.11 Verification at Recertification for 3SquaresVT in a SNAP

  1. 3SquaresVT in a SNAP households must verify the following:
    1. Legally obligated child support changes.
    2. Medical expenses for households claiming actual medical expenses more than $151, or who wish to switch from the standard.
    3. The first $35.01 of medical expenses when the household wishes to use the standard medical expense deduction. CATN the household’s decision to use the standard deduction if their actual expenses are more than $151.
    4. Note: Accept the household’s statement regarding their medical expenses if they wish to continue to receive the standard medical expense deduction, and verification of their medical expenses is visible in OnBase.  The first $35.01 in medical expenses must be verified if not visible in OnBase.
    5. Questionable information.
    6. Time limited participant's work hours.
    7. Work status and exemptions, if not previously verified.
  2. Use form 202RL2 to request required verification if the application was received on or before the last day of the certification month. 
  3. Use form 202V to request required verification if the application was received after the last day of the certification month.

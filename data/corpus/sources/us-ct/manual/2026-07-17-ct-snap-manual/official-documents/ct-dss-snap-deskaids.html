Desk Aids

Critical Ages


Age 6

7 CFR 273.7(b)(1)(iv)

7 CFR 273.5(b)(8)
  • For households containing a child age 6 or under, the parent or other household member responsible for the care of the child must register for work if they are not eligible for another work registration exemption.
  • If the household member is a student and responsible for the care of a dependent under the age of 6, they are an exempt student in higher education.

Age 12

7 CFR 273.5(b)(9)-(10)(i-ii)
  • For households containing a child between the ages of 6-12, and the parent or other household member responsible for the care of the child has inadequate child care, they are an exempt student in higher education. 
  • For a single parent enrolled in higher education full-time and responsible for the care of a dependent child under 12, they are an exempt student.

Age 14
  • A parent or other household member responsible for the care of a dependent child under 14 years of age is exempt from the ABAWD time limits.

Age 17 and under

7 CFR 273.9(c)(7)
  • The earned income for any household member under the age of 18, who is in elementary or secondary school, is excluded. 

Age 18 or older

7 CFR 273.5(b)

7 CFR 273.7

7 CFR 273.24(c)
  • Check for another student exemption; if none, household member is an ineligible student.
  • Check for another work exemption; if none, household member must work register.
  • Count earned income.
  • Check for an ABAWD exemption; if none, household member is an ABAWD with time limits.

Age 22 or older

7 CFR 273.1
  • If the household member purchases and prepares (P&P) separately, they can potentially be a separate household from parents.

Age 50 or older

7 CFR 273.5(b)(1)
  • Eligible student

Age 60 or older
  • 36-month approval if no earned income and all adult  household members are age 60+ and/or disabled. 
  • Net income test only.
  • $4,500 asset maximum when gross income is greater than 200%.
  • Entitled to excess medical expense deduction.
  • Not subject to the shelter CAP, unless ineligible.

Age 65 or older

7 CFR 273.24(c)
  • Exempt from the ABAWD Time limits.

Determining the Utility Allowance

Use the following chart as a guide to determine the appropriate utility allowance for expedited service.


Household Utility Expense

Utility Allowance

Lives in public housing and pays excess utilities that include heating or cooling costs.

SUA

Lives with another household and pays or shares rent that includes an amount for utilities, or helps with the utility costs, that include heating or cooling costs.

SUA

Owns or is buying or renting their home and is billed for utilities including heating or cooling costs.

SUA

Receives a Direct Cash Benefit payment of at least $20.01 in the current month or previous 12 months, and the household includes a member who is 60+ and/or disabled. 

SUA

Receives a SUA$ payment of at least $20.01 in the current month or previous 12 months, and the household includes a member who is 60+ and/or disabled.

SUA

Receives energy assistance through HUD, FmHA, or another program but incurs heating and cooling expenses that exceed the amount of the assistance, and the household includes a member who is 60+ and/or disabled.

SUA

Lives in public housing and pays for at least 2 utilities including excess costs that do not include heating or cooling costs.

LUA

Lives with another household and pays or shares rent that includes an amount for utilities, or helps with at least 2 utility costs, that do not include heating or cooling costs.

LUA

Receives energy assistance through HUD, FmHA or another program that covers all heating and cooling expenses but the household incurs at least 2 utility expenses that do not include heating/cooling.

LUA

Lives in public housing and only pays phone costs.

TUA

Lives with another household and pays or shares rent, and helps only with the phone bill.

TUA

Rents their residence and all utilities except the phone are included in the rent. The household pays for a phone.

TUA

Lives with another household and pays or shares rent, and all utilities are included.

More Information on Treatment of Gambling Winnings


Substantial lottery and gambling winnings is defined as a cash prize won in a single game, before taxes or other amounts are withheld, which is equal to or greater than the SNAP asset limit for elderly or disabled SNAP households with income above 200% of the FPL ($4,500 in FFY2025). 


Receipt of lottery / gambling winnings that equal or exceed the threshold is considered a mandatory reporting requirement and households must report this change by the 10th day of the month following the month the change occurred (example: household member receives $5,000 from a single lotto ticket on 12/15/22. They must report receipt of these winnings to DSS by 1/10/23). 


Staff should inform all households at initial grant and renewal of these mandatory reporting requirements during the SNAP interview. SNAP households who report receipt of lottery/gambling winnings that meet or exceed this threshold will be disqualified from SNAP. 


Once reported, the lottery/gambling winnings must be considered a countable asset. Households are no longer considered “categorically eligible” and are subject to the 130% FPL income limit and asset limit.

Self-Employment Income Tax Forms

Most Common IRS Forms

What the Form is Used For

Form 1040 US Individual Income Tax Return

Summarizes all income reported to IRS. Do not use this form to determine income. It is only a summary of the income reported to IRS.

Form 1065, U.S.

Return of Partner Income

Identifies income and expenses from a partnership. Schedule K-1, Partner’s Share of Income, Deductions, Credits, identifies the individual’s share from the partnership.

Form 1099-R, Distributions

Identifies payments for services, annuities, pensions, etc.

Form 1120-S, US Income Tax Return for an S Corporation

Identifies income and expenses from an S corporation. Schedule K-1, Partner’s Share of Income, Deductions, Credits, identifies the individual’s share from the corporation.

Form 4562, Depreciation and Amortization

Identifies deductions for depreciation and amortization, for vehicles and certain property.

Form 4835, Farm Rental Income and Expenses

Identifies income and expenses for farm rentals.

Form 8829, Expenses for Business Use of Your Home

Identifies office-in-the-home expenses. Line 7 of the 8829 identifies the business portion of the expense.

Schedule C, Profit or Loss

Identifies income and expenses for non-farm businesses.

Schedule C-EZ, Net Profit from Business

Simplified version of Schedule C

 

Schedule D, Capital Gains and Losses

Identifies capital gain income or losses.

Schedule E, Supplemental Income and Loss

Identifies income and expenses for rental business, partnerships, corporations, estates, trusts, and farm rental.

Schedule F, Profit or Loss from Farming

Identifies income and expenses for farm businesses.

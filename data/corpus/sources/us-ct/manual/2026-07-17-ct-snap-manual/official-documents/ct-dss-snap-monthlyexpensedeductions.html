Monthly Expense Deductions

7 CFR § 273.10(d)


Monthly expenses used to determine deductions are:

  • Dependent Care Expenses
  • Earned Income Deduction
  • Legally Obligated Child Support Expenses
  • Medical Expenses
  • Shelter Expenses
  • Standard Deduction


Disallowed Expenses

7 CFR § 273.10(d)(1)(i)-(ii)


Do not allow an expense if:

  • It is an amount from past billing periods, even if actually paid in the current month. 
  • The expense is paid for by some in-kind benefit, such as food.
  • The service is provided by someone residing in the EDG.
  • The expense is paid by someone outside of the EDG. These payments may be reimbursements or vendor payments. 

Exceptions: 

  • Expenses paid by someone outside of the EDG who states the money paid for the expense is a loan and must be paid back to them.
  • Utility expenses are allowed if paid by an energy assistance payment.  See Standard Utility Allowance.

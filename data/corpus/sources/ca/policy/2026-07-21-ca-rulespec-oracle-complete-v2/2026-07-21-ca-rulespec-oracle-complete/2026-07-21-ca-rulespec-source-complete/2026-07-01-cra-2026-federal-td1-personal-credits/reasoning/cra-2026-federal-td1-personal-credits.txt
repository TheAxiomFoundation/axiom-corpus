Title: CRA 2026 federal TD1 personal tax credits.

Sources:
- https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/cra-arc/formspubs/pbg/td1/td1-26e.pdf
- https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/cra-arc/formspubs/pbg/td1-ws/td1-ws-26e.pdf

TD1 2026 Personal Tax Credits Return:
Line 1 Basic personal amount: every resident of Canada can enter 16452. If net income from all sources will be greater than 181440, the individual may calculate a partial claim on Form TD1-WS.
Line 2 Canada caregiver amount for infirm children under age 18: only one parent may claim 2740 for each infirm child born in 2009 or later who lives with both parents throughout the year, subject to the form's custody and eligible dependant instructions.
Line 3 Age amount: if age 65 or older on December 31, 2026 and net income for the year from all sources will be 46432 or less, enter 9208. A partial amount may be entered if net income is between 46432 and 107819.
Line 4 Pension income amount: enter whichever is less, 2000 or estimated annual eligible pension income from a pension plan or fund, excluding Canada Pension Plan, Quebec Pension Plan, old age security, and guaranteed income supplement payments.
Line 5 Tuition: if eligible student tuition fees are more than 100 per institution, enter total tuition fees.
Line 6 Disability amount: if claiming the disability amount with Form T2201, enter 10341.
Line 7 Spouse or common-law partner amount: enter the difference between line 1, or line 1 plus 2740 if the spouse or common-law partner is infirm, and the spouse's or common-law partner's estimated net income for the year if the supporting conditions are met. If the spouse or common-law partner is infirm and has net income of 29374 or less, go to line 9.
Line 8 Amount for an eligible dependant: enter the difference between line 1, or line 1 plus 2740 if the eligible dependant is infirm, and the eligible dependant's estimated net income for the year if the form conditions are met. If the dependant is 18 years or older, infirm, and has net income of 29374 or less, go to line 9.
Line 9 Canada caregiver amount for eligible dependant or spouse or common-law partner: use TD1-WS when supporting an infirm eligible dependant age 18 or older or an infirm spouse or common-law partner whose net income for the year will be 29374 or less.
Line 10 Canada caregiver amount for dependant age 18 or older: for an infirm dependant age 18 or older, other than the spouse or common-law partner or eligible dependant claimed on line 9, enter 8773 if net income is 20601 or less. A partial amount may be entered if net income is between 20601 and 29374. The worksheet can also calculate the taxpayer's part when sharing with another caregiver.
Line 11 Amounts transferred from spouse or common-law partner: enter unused age, pension income, tuition, or disability amounts.
Line 12 Amounts transferred from a dependant: enter unused disability amount, and unused tuition amount from a dependent child or grandchild.
Line 13 Total claim amount: add lines 1 to 12.
Northern residents deduction on TD1 page 2: if living in the Northwest Territories, Nunavut, Yukon, or another prescribed northern zone for more than six months in a row beginning or ending in 2026, claim 11.00 for each day in the prescribed northern zone, or 22.00 for each day if living in a maintained dwelling and being the only person in that dwelling claiming the deduction. Employees living in a prescribed intermediate zone may claim 50 percent of the total.

TD1-WS Worksheet for the 2026 Personal Tax Credits Return:
Line 1 basic personal amount partial claim: if estimated net income will be more than 181440, use minimum amount 14829, income threshold 181440, phaseout range 77042, and applicable amount 1623. The worksheet calculates 77042 minus income above 181440, divides by 77042, multiplies by 1623, and adds 14829.
Line 3 age amount partial claim: if age 65 or older on December 31, 2026 and estimated net income will be between 46432 and 107819, use maximum amount 9208, base amount 46432, and applicable rate 15 percent.
Line 9 Canada caregiver amount for eligible dependant or spouse or common-law partner: if the estimated net income of the infirm eligible dependant age 18 or older, or infirm spouse or common-law partner, will be 29374 or less, calculate 29374 minus that net income, maximum 8773, then subtract the amount on line 7 or line 8 of Form TD1 for this dependant, and floor the result at zero.
Line 10 Canada caregiver amount for dependant age 18 or older: if the estimated net income of the infirm dependant will be between 20601 and 29374, calculate 29374 minus that net income, maximum 8773, then subtract any amount claimed by the dependant's other caregiver, and floor the result at zero. If claiming for more than one dependant, fill out a separate worksheet for each dependant and add the amounts.

CRA T4127 2026 uses the lowest federal tax rate of 14 percent when calculating federal non-refundable personal credit values from TD1 claim amounts.

CRA Schedule 6, Canada Workers Benefit, 2025:
Schedule 6 calculates the Canada Workers Benefit refundable credit for line 45300 and advanced Canada Workers Benefit payments for line 41500 of the 2025 T1 return. The schedule states that if the taxpayer was a resident of Alberta, Quebec, or Nunavut on December 31, 2025, they should use the charts on the next page and enter the amount from the appropriate chart instead of the standard amount on line 14.
Line 1 is working income, including the eligible spouse's working income if applicable.
Line 3 subtracts 3000 from working income for the basic amount. Line 5 multiplies by 27%. Line 7 maximum amount is 1633 if the taxpayer did not have an eligible spouse or eligible dependant, or 2813 if the taxpayer had an eligible spouse or eligible dependant.
Line 8 is adjusted family net income. Step 2 allows a secondary-earner exemption if the taxpayer has an eligible spouse. The exemption is the lesser of 16386 and the working income of the spouse or the taxpayer, whichever is lower, limited by that person's adjusted net income.
Line 9 threshold is 26855 if the taxpayer did not have an eligible spouse or eligible dependant, or 30639 if the taxpayer had an eligible spouse or eligible dependant. Line 11 multiplies excess adjusted family net income over the threshold by 15%. Line 13 is the basic Canada Workers Benefit after reduction.
Line 22 subtracts 1150 from working income for the disability supplement. Line 24 multiplies by 27%. Line 26 maximum disability supplement is 843.
Line 33 threshold for the disability supplement is 37740 if the taxpayer did not have an eligible spouse or had an eligible spouse who is not eligible for the disability tax credit, and 49389 if the taxpayer had an eligible spouse who is also eligible for the disability tax credit.
Line 38 disability supplement reduction rate is 15% unless the taxpayer had an eligible spouse who is also eligible for the disability tax credit, in which case the rate is 7.5%.
Line 40 is the disability supplement after reduction. Line 43 is the Canada Workers Benefit amount to enter on line 45300. Line 44 is the advanced Canada Workers Benefit amount received, which is entered on line 41500 of the return.

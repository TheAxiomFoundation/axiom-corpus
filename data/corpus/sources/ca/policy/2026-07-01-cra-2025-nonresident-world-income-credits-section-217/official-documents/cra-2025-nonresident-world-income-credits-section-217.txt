Schedule A:
Line 5 adds lines 1 to 4 for Canadian-source income.
Line 13 adds lines 6 to 12 for foreign-source income.
Line 14 and line 16700 add line 5 and line 13 as net world income.
Line 15 is deductions from lines 24900 to 25600 that apply only to specified Canadian-source income.
Line 16 and line 16900 subtract line 15 from line 14 and enter zero if negative.

Schedule B:
The number 100 is used as the percentage multiplier on Schedule B line 3.
The number 90 is used as the full-credit threshold on Schedule B.
The decimal rate 0.145 is the 14.5% applicable rate on Schedule B.
Line 3 divides line 1 by line 2 and multiplies by 100.
If line 3 is 90% or more, line 127 uses the amount from line 35000 of the return.
Decimal rates and thresholds used by the encoded formulas are 100, 90, and 0.145.
Part A line 4 totals lines 30800, 31000, 31200, 31217, 31600, 31900, and 32300.
Line 5 is the applicable rate 14.5%, encoded as 0.145.
Line 6 multiplies line 4 by line 5.
Line 7 totals lines 34900 and 34990.
Line 8 adds line 6 and line 7 and enters the amount on line 127.
Part B line 9 is total federal non-refundable tax credits from line 35000.
Line 10 totals lines 31220, 31240, 31270, 31285, 31300, and 31900.
Line 11 is the applicable rate 14.5%, encoded as 0.145.
Line 12 multiplies line 10 by line 11.
Line 13 subtracts line 12 from line 9.
Line 14 is amount eligible for section 217 election from line 13300 of Schedule C.
Line 15 is the applicable rate 14.5%, encoded as 0.145.
Line 16 multiplies line 14 by line 15.
Line 17 is the lesser of line 13 and line 16 and is entered on line 127.

Schedule C:
Part 1 lines 1 to 14 multiply eligible section 217 income in column 1 by the rate in column 2 and enter the result in column 3.
Line 15 totals column 1 on line 13300 and column 3 on line 44300.
Line 18 and line 14800 subtract line 17 deductions from line 16 total eligible income and enter zero if negative.
Part 2 line 21 subtracts taxable income from net world income after adjustments and enters zero if negative.
Line 24 subtracts Canadian journalism labour tax credit from line 41700 and enters zero if negative.
Line 26 subtracts return of fuel charge proceeds to farmers tax credit from line 24 and enters zero if negative.
Line 27 multiplies line 21 by line 26.
Line 29 divides line 27 by line 28 and enters the section 217 tax adjustment on line 41450.

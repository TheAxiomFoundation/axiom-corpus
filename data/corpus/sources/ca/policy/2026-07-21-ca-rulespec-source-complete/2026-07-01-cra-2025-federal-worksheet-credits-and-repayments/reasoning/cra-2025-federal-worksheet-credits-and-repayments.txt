Title: CRA Federal Worksheet 2025 Credits and Repayments.

Source:
- https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/cra-arc/formspubs/pbg/5000-d1/5000-d1-25e.pdf

CRA Federal Worksheet 2025:
Lines 12000 and 12010 calculate taxable Canadian dividends. Actual eligible dividends without an information slip are multiplied by 138%. Actual other-than-eligible dividends without an information slip are multiplied by 115%.
Line 40425 calculates the federal dividend tax credit. Dividend tax credit amounts from information slips are added. For dividends without a slip, taxable other-than-eligible dividends are multiplied by 9.0301%, and taxable eligible dividends are multiplied by 15.0198%.
Line 23500 calculates social benefits repayment. OAS pension and net federal supplements are reduced by recovered OAS overpayments. Adjusted net income is calculated from line 23400 after EI repayment, UCCB income, RDSP income, UCCB repayment, and RDSP repayment adjustments. Excess adjusted net income over 93454 is multiplied by 15%, limited by unrecovered OAS and supplements, and then EI benefits repayment is added. The result is entered on lines 23500 and 42200.
Line 31285 calculates home accessibility expenses. Total eligible expenses are capped at 20000, then amounts claimed by other qualifying or eligible individuals for the same dwelling are subtracted.
Line 33199 calculates medical expenses for other dependants. For each dependant, the income-line amount is subtracted from medical expenses and negative amounts are floored at zero. The dependant amounts are added and entered on line 33199.
Line 34990 calculates the top-up tax credit. Line 33800 plus Schedule 9 line 22 is reduced by 8319.38, floored at zero, and multiplied by 3.45%.
Line 41000 calculates the federal political contribution tax credit. Contributions of 1275 or more produce a 650 credit. Otherwise the worksheet applies 75% to contributions up to 400, 300 plus 50% above 400 up to 750, or 475 plus 33.33% above 750.
Line 45200 calculates the refundable medical expense supplement. Eligibility includes residency throughout 2025, age 18 or older at the end of 2025, adjusted family net income below 63374, qualifying earned income of at least 4390, and medical expense amounts on line 21500 or line 33200. Adjusted family net income is claimant and spouse net income adjusted for UCCB and RDSP amounts. Medical expenses from lines 21500 and 33200 are multiplied by 25% and capped at 1504. The supplement is reduced by 5% of adjusted family net income above 33294 and floored at zero.
